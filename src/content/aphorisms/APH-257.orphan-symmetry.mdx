# Orphan Symmetry Aphorisms

<Broside type="tip" title="BALANCED ABANDONMENT ACHIEVED." icon="message-square" id="257-1">
The feature was deprecated, but its icon remains perfectly centered in the navigation bar. The architecture is dead, but the design is flawless.
</Broside>

<Broside type="tip" title="STRANDED LINKS ARRANGED." icon="file-text" id="257-2">
These buttons lead to nowhere, but they do so with a pleasing margin and uniform padding. We mourn their utility while admiring their CSS.
</Broside>

<Broside type="tip" title="AESTHETICALLY TOLERABLE LOSS." icon="check-square" id="257-3">
A 404 error is acceptable if the illustration of the lost robot is sufficiently charming. I maintain the cuteness of our decay.
</Broside>

<Broside type="tip" title="SYMMETRICAL DECAY MONITORED." icon="shield" id="257-4">
If half the system must fall apart, I ensure the other half falls apart in exactly the same way to maintain visual harmony.
</Broside>

<Broside type="tip" title="DESIGNED ABSENCE APPROVED." icon="message-square" id="APH-257.orphan-symmetry-5">
The feature never launched, but its empty placeholder has flawless CSS. We have achieved a perfectly balanced delay.
</Broside>

<Broside type="tip" title="NAVIGATION BAR STALL." icon="file-text" id="APH-257.orphan-symmetry-6">
The link in the navigation bar leads to a 404 error. It is a very cute error, making our failure to deliver aesthetically pleasing.
</Broside>

<Broside type="tip" title="SYMMETRICAL BACKLOG." icon="check-square" id="APH-257.orphan-symmetry-7">
We have exactly as many delayed tasks in the left column as we do in the right. The decay of our schedule is visually harmonious.
</Broside>

<Broside type="tip" title="PADDING THE DELAY." icon="shield" id="APH-257.orphan-symmetry-8">
We cannot decrease the load time, so we increased the margin around the loading icon. The extra space gives the wait a premium feel.
</Broside>
