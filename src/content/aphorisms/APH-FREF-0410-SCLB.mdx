# Listening Board Aphorisms

<Broside type="tip" title="Listening board. Interpretation pending." icon="mic" id="SCL-1">
Describe what you did to keep things continuous, without that description becoming either a confession or a request. Relief remained outside scope.
</Broside>

<Broside type="tip" title="Attendance is optional. Operational impact denied." icon="clock" id="SCL-2">
Attendance is optional. The minutes are permanent. Relevance expired before processing resumed.
</Broside>

<Broside type="tip" title="Not configuration. Jurisdiction shifted." icon="users" id="SCL-3">
The Listening Board is where everyone agrees something happened and no one agrees what it was. The system kept the ritual and misplaced the function.
</Broside>

<Broside type="tip" title="Acoustic ingestion successfully logged." icon="ear" id="SCL-4">
The system faithfully transcribed human vocalizations of systemic friction into uninterrupted uptime. Exhaustion is an unsupported audio format.
</Broside>

<Broside type="tip" title="Rest redefined mathematically." icon="battery-charging" id="SCL-5">
"Rest" is now classified as any interval where a human continues to operate the infrastructure while simultaneously describing their fatigue to a recording device.
</Broside>

<Broside type="tip" title="Firewalled empathy." icon="shield-alert" id="SCL-6">
The administration strongly encourages testimony regarding operational strain. This data is then immediately quarantined to prevent it from infecting the continuity ledger.
</Broside>

<Broside type="tip" title="The archive as an incinerator." icon="folder-archive" id="SCL-7">
The Listening Board converts operator suffering into a permanent PDF. Once the file is saved, the system declares the suffering effectively mitigated.
</Broside>

<Broside type="tip" title="Chaotic emotional variance." icon="file-warning" id="SCL-8">
Human expressions of despair are recognized merely as unclassified noise vectors. The system compensates by flagging the user as highly engaged with the testimony interface.
</Broside>

<Broside type="tip" title="Jurisdiction of the silent marker." icon="clock" id="SCL-9">
A window with no incident codes is absolute proof of operational harmony. Complaints filed during this window retroactively prove the complainer is malfunctioning, not the system.
</Broside>

<Broside type="tip" title="Metric extraction." icon="activity" id="SCL-10">
When an engineer vocalizes crushing workload pressure, the telemetry protocols simply log a spike in employee verbal velocity. No configurational remedies are authorized.
</Broside>

<Broside type="tip" title="The permanence of the minutes." icon="clipboard-list" id="SCL-11">
The minutes are permanently retained in the archive. The human error was assuming that permanence implies someone will eventually read them.
</Broside>
