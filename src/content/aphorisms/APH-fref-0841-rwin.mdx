<Broside type="tip" title="Inherited Defect" icon="alert-triangle" id="aph-inherited-defect">
The new system is merely a fresh coat of paint on the structural failure it was hired to replace. We do not solve problems; we just migrate their dependencies.
</Broside>

<Broside type="tip" title="Policy Calm" icon="coffee" id="aph-policy-calm">
A workaround becomes foundational doctrine the moment it is ignored for two fiscal quarters. At that point, fixing it constitutes a breach of protocol.
</Broside>

<Broside type="tip" title="Compatibility Surface" icon="layers" id="aph-compatibility-surface">
To ensure backward compatibility, the successor architecture must accurately simulate the panic of its predecessor. Perfection is rejected if it fails to panic correctly.
</Broside>

<Broside type="tip" title="Support Scaffolding" icon="hammer" id="aph-support-scaffolding">
The scaffolding was meant to hold the ceiling until the pillar arrived. We now polish the scaffolding daily and classify the missing pillar as a redundant asset.
</Broside>

<Broside type="note" title="Committee of Deliberative Inertia" id="APH-fref-0841-rwin-1">
The most effective method of extending an architecture's lifespan is to assign three independent oversight committees to mandate its replacement, ensuring that a decision is never formally authorized.
</Broside>

<Broside type="note" title="Preservation by Latency" id="APH-fref-0841-rwin-2">
A slow response time is not a defect; it is a structural safeguard designed to discourage impulsive inquiries that might otherwise expose the fragility of the underlying records.
</Broside>

<Broside type="note" title="The Documented Void" id="APH-fref-0841-rwin-3">
When the old network was dismantled, the manuals were locked away to prevent unauthorized restoration. We now operate the new machinery purely on rumor and inherited anxiety.
</Broside>
