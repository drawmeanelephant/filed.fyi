<Broside type="tip" title="Resolution Metric" icon="check" id="aph-acs-001">
A case is deemed resolved when the administrative effort required to sustain the complaint exceeds the human capacity for consecutive meetings.
</Broside>

<Broside type="tip" title="Atmospheric Integrity" icon="alert-circle" id="aph-acs-002">
Do not confuse a lack of audible dissent with structural repair; the silence merely indicates that the human vectors have exhausted their rhetorical stamina.
</Broside>

<Broside type="tip" title="Ritual Sufficiency" icon="archive" id="aph-acs-003">
The ceremony of closure is considered complete the moment the ambient social fatigue outweighs the documented systemic failure.
</Broside>

<Broside type="tip" title="Continuity Mandate" icon="clock" id="aph-acs-004">
A procedural seal does not fix the underlying anomaly, but it successfully legally isolates the archive from the continued suffering of the user.
</Broside>

<Broside type="tip" title="Frictionless Voiding" icon="file-minus" id="aph-acs-005">
If an error persists long enough without catastrophic system collapse, it is reclassified as environmental weather and promptly ignored.
</Broside>

<Broside type="tip" title="Documentation Illusion" icon="file-text" id="aph-acs-006">
Every required form was signed in triplicate, producing a perfectly documented counterfeit finish that protects the filing cabinet from human truth.
</Broside>

<Broside type="tip" title="Human Depletion" icon="hourglass" id="aph-acs-007">
The most effective mitigation strategy against biological complaints is a waiting period designed to outlast the complainant's patience.
</Broside>

<Broside type="tip" title="Symbolic Conclusion" icon="flag" id="aph-acs-008">
We do not close a ticket because the terrain is solved; we close it because the atmosphere in the review board has reached an acceptable level of collective apathy.
</Broside>
