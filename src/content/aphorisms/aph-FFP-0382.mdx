<Broside type="tip" title="Archival Termination" icon="power" id="ffp382-aph-1">
A service does not die; it merely transitions from processing chaotic user input to successfully logging its own permanent absence.
</Broside>

<Broside type="tip" title="Double Tense Deletion" icon="calendar-off" id="ffp382-aph-2">
To ensure complete system shutdown, the discontinuation notice must be issued in both the past and future tenses to trap the service in the present void.
</Broside>

<Broside type="tip" title="Dead Service Layer" icon="archive" id="ffp382-aph-3">
Once a service has been formally categorized as 'no longer available', any residual functionality exhibited by the system is considered a strict breach of policy.
</Broside>

<Broside type="note" title="Tender Discontinuation" icon="heart" id="ffp382-aph-4">
We do not sever the connections abruptly. We unplug the endpoints one by one, whispering a soft thank-you into every dead port.
</Broside>

<Broside type="tip" title="The Lingering Exhibit" icon="moon" id="ffp382-aph-5">
The service is discontinued, but the exhibit remains. It is a quiet museum of all the payloads that tried their hardest to arrive on time.
</Broside>

<Broside type="tip" title="Graceful Sunset" icon="archive" id="ffp382-aph-6">
A sunset protocol is not an execution. It is the archive pulling a heavy quilt over a system that has earned its deep sleep.
</Broside>

<Broside type="note" title="Archived Promises" icon="feather" id="ffp382-aph-7">
The banner says the service is gone, but we still leave a small buffer allocated in memory, just in case its ghost needs to stretch.
</Broside>

<Broside type="tip" title="Quiet Departure" icon="wind" id="ffp382-aph-8">
The logs show zero traffic. We close the binder, turn the key, and let the dust settle over the greatest API we ever built.
</Broside>
