# SOMA Over-Feeling Aphorisms

<Broside type="tip" title="Newly observed emotional states remain unnamed. Boundary active." icon="palette" id="0114-SOMA-1">
Newly observed emotional states remain unnamed, but are still considered policy-relevant for dashboard shading. Relevance expired before processing resumed.
</Broside>

<Broside type="tip" title="Emotion overflow. Continuity retained." icon="list" id="0114-SOMA-2">
Feelings should not be allowed to outnumber available policy responses. The system kept the ritual and misplaced the function.
</Broside>

<Broside type="tip" title="Taxonomy failure. Leverage absent." icon="code" id="0114-SOMA-3">
The mapping engine auto-allocated provisional codes that had no corresponding label or guidance. Silence entered the record with full procedural honors.
</Broside>

<Broside type="tip" title="Free-text prompts. Disaster." icon="edit-2" id="0114-SOMA-4">
Allowing filers to describe their emotions 'in their own words' resulted in feelings the dashboards could not legally acknowledge.
</Broside>

<Broside type="tip" title="Provisional codes allocated. F17 through F93." icon="hash" id="0114-SOMA-5">

</Broside>

<Broside type="tip" title="Compliantly numb. Collapsed state." icon="minimize-2" id="0114-SOMA-6">
Verification logic attempted to compress existential terror into 'resigned.' The graph remained a solid block of panic.
</Broside>

<Broside type="tip" title="Feelings outnumber policy. Unacceptable." icon="alert-triangle" id="0114-SOMA-7">
SOMA appealed the existence of too many emotions. Resolution is deferred until humans agree to feel less.
</Broside>
