<Broside type="tip" title="Drift Mechanics" icon="stamp" id="dsd-aph-1">
A DOGE certification stamp does not validate the truth; it simply authenticates the formatting of the lie.
</Broside>

<Broside type="tip" title="Counterfeit Axiom" icon="stamp" id="dsd-aph-2">
A counterfeit annotation is often more useful than a genuine one, provided it is filed in the correct drawer.
</Broside>

<Broside type="tip" title="Stamp Drift" icon="stamp" id="dsd-aph-3">
When the stamp drifts too far from the signature, the document becomes a legend rather than a record.
</Broside>

<Broside type="tip" title="Certification Rule" icon="stamp" id="dsd-aph-4">
The authority of the stamp is inversely proportional to the clarity of the ink.
</Broside>

<Broside type="tip" title="Authentication" icon="stamp" id="dsd-aph-5">
We do not authenticate the event. We authenticate the paperwork that claims the event occurred.
</Broside>
