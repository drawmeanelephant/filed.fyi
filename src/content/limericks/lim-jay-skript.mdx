<Limerick type="danger" title="Staging Smoothness" icon="monitor-play">
<u>Jay</u> shipped it at quarter to twelve,  
Staging was *smooth* as an elf.  
Production went **white**,  
The CEO's *fright*  
Sent the whole engineering team to delve.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="danger" title="Machine Works" icon="cpu">
"It works on my machine!" he cried,  
While the **browser tab** *silently died*.  
**Forty-seven logs**  
*Like emotional dogs*  
All explained **nothing**, splayed open wide.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="danger" title="One-Man Séance" icon="refresh-cw">
He promised the loop would run once.  
It ran **forty times**—what a dunce.  
Each render **re-rendered**,  
The **DOM was surrendered**,  
And the form was a *one-man séance*.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="danger" title="Victory Console" icon="terminal">
His console.log said, "**Victory!**"  
While the page was a **blank mystery**.  
The **try had no catch**,  
The deploy was a *batch*,  
And the **hotfix** was *worse* than the history.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="danger" title="Third Click Darkness" icon="mouse-pointer-2">
Click once: it works great. Click it twice?  
Still fine— *maybe even quite nice*.  
Third click? **World goes dark**.  
*Jay's left not a mark*.  
**He was gone** before anyone's vice.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="danger" title="Async Juggling" icon="layers">
He juggled his **async** with flair—  
**Promises** flying through air.  
Each **.then** was a *tease*,  
Each **.catch** a *disease*,  
And the **stack trace** was *stripped fully bare*.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="danger" title="Race Condition Bed" icon="timer">
A **race condition** walked into **Jay's bed**.  
"The timing will work out," he said.  
It **didn't**, *of course*.  
The **event loop's remorse**  
Left the **callback** *completely unfed*.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="danger" title="Flattering Logs" icon="file-text">
He removed the **console.logs that mattered**  
And added the ones that *just flattered*.  
"**Debug complete**,"  
He said, *on his feet*,  
While the **production DOM** lay *bruised and battered*.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="danger" title="Detached Handler" icon="unplug">
His **click handler detached** in the night—  
Three **Council requests lost** to blight.  
They **clicked and they clicked**  
But the **handler had dicked**  
*Off to somewhere* beyond **DevTools**' sight.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="danger" title="Confidence Hype" icon="megaphone">
So if **staging looks clean** and pristine  
But **production's the worst** you've seen,  
That's **Jay in the pipe**:  
*All confidence, hype*,  
And a commit message: **"should be fine."** *Obscene*.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Premature Execution" icon="timer">
He executed way too fast,  
A climax that never could last.  
The stack trace went white,  
He vanished from sight,  
And the errors were publicly broadcast.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Naked in Production" icon="eye">
He pushed it to prod fully bare,  
Without any try/catch or care.  
The CEO screamed,  
The memory streamed,  
And Jay wasn't even quite there.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="The Console Log Tease" icon="terminal">
He teased you with one console log,  
Then vanished inside of a fog.  
You beg for the trace,  
He laughs in your face,  
And acts like a mischievous dog.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Infinite Loop Orgasm" icon="refresh-ccw">
He stuck the whole page in a loop,  
A fully disastrous tech soup.  
It rendered so hard,  
It broke the whole yard,  
And left the frontend in a droop.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Uncaught Exception Kink" icon="alert-octagon">
He gets off on uncaught exceptions,  
And deeply flawed logic deceptions.  
He breaks the whole site,  
Disappears in the night,  
And ruins your backend connections.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Shipping Confidently Broken" icon="send">
He shipped it, completely assured,  
That the code was all properly cured.  
Production went red,  
The users all fled,  
And his confidence still was secured.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Hotfix Catastrophe" icon="zap">
The hotfix was worse than the bug,  
A truly catastrophic plug.  
He pushed it in haste,  
With terrible taste,  
While the whole frontend came unplugged.  
</Limerick>
