<Limerick type="note" title="Guess Which to Greet" icon="help-circle">
Two Form 32-As took one seat,  
Each convinced it was terribly neat.  
One handled access.  
One handled distress.  
Downstream had to guess which to greet.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Where Precision Should Sit" icon="alert-triangle">
The central registry went, "Well, shit."  
Two procedures were living in it.  
Same series, same slot,  
Different purpose and rot.  
That is not where precision should sit.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Both Rites in One Phase" icon="git-merge">
Filers entered the queue in a haze:  
"Am I seeking permission or praise  
For directive impact?"  
The catalog cracked  
And suggested both rites in one phase.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Minimal Feud" icon="shield">
Keyspace collisions are crude.  
In a forms office, that's extra rude.  
Numbers are there  
So the lost can get where  
They're going with minimal feud.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Surprise at Surprises" icon="gift">
The consolidation exercise  
Produced one of bureaucracy's prizes:  
Two roads, one signpost,  
And an archive engrossed  
In pretending surprise at surprises.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Impressively Stressed" icon="frown">
One poor clerk filed access request  
And got self-assessment instead as a test.  
By the end of the round,  
No one knew what they'd found,  
But both teams felt impressively stressed.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Tore at the Seam" icon="scissors">
Registry order is thin.  
It tears if you breathe wrong within.  
Here it tore at the seam  
Where identifiers dream  
Of uniqueness and do not quite win.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Procedural Epaulettes" icon="dices">
The downstream systems took bets  
On which 32-A they would get.  
That's a hell of a way  
To run workflow by day:  
Roulette in procedural epaulettes.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Improv and Shame" icon="theater">
If two forms share one little name,  
Then the number no longer can frame  
A specific intent.  
You've just legally bent  
Every filing toward improv and shame.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Uncertainty Mans the Shoal" icon="ghost">
So when catalogs promise control,  
Check the duplicate ghosts in the roll.  
One code, two demands:  
That's how structure expands  
Till uncertainty mans the whole shoal.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Filing Both Series" icon="copy">
"Use the series," the baseline dictates,
But the registry duplicates gates.
So I filed them all twice,
To be strictly precise,
While the catalog buckles and waits.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Standard Conflict" icon="crosshair">
"Keep the identifier," they swore,
So we kept the same tag as before.
If the access request
Is a validation test,
We are matching the baseline of yore.
</Limerick>
