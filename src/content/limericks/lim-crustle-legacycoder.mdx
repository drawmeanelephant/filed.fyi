<Limerick type="note" title="Russet Repository Ark" icon="database">
Old Crustle compiles in the *dark*  
With ancient code, flawless as bark.  
No update she'll take,  
No OS forsake—  
Her Russet Repository's her ark.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Legacy Bytecode Heresy" icon="file-code">
She misfiled a harvest as "*legacy  
Bytecode*"—a pomological heresy.  
The auditors blinked,  
The format was linked  
To a system from nineteen-eighty-three.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Obsolete Format Batch" icon="alert-triangle">
She compiled a batch in a *format*  
So obsolete, no modern doormat  
Could read it. The Council  
Just stared at the foundrel  
And said, "That's a really old format."  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Legacy Endures" icon="hard-drive">
She refused to update her *OS*—  
The audit compatibility: a mess.  
But her code never fails,  
Her Russet prevails,  
And the legacy endures, nonetheless.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Code Deep" icon="binary">
"*Code deep, endure eternal,*" she said,  
While gnarled boughs whispered overhead.  
Her pomological secrets  
In bytecode's aesthetics  
Were etched in a format long dead.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Resilient Secrets" icon="shield">
Her resilient yet *secretive* tone  
Means Russet Records are her alone.  
The code hymns she chants  
Under gritty-skinned plants,  
And the repository's not overthrown.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Ancient Abode" icon="factory">
Her *Enduring Core Compiler* made  
This sentinel of the orchard brigade.  
Her flawless old code  
In its ancient abode  
Preserves what the modern would trade.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Cache-Invalidation Dance" icon="refresh-cw">
She hoards *stale breadcrumbs* dimension-wise,  
Each one a pomological prize.  
The cache-invalidation  
Dance? Her creation—  
Three steps, then forgets. No surprise.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Russet-Skinned Truth" icon="check-circle">
Moderns smile at her antique style,  
But her logic is *gold*—every file  
Compiles without error,  
A deprecated bearer  
Of truth in each russet-skinned aisle.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Ancient Code Load" icon="cpu">
So if harvest logs read like bytecode  
And the format is decades out-of-mode,  
That's *Crustle's* domain:  
Where the legacy's sane,  
And the ancient code carries the load.  
</Limerick>
