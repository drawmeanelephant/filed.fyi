<Limerick type="note" title="The Syntax is Fine" icon="check">
A form came, *perfectly drawn*—  
The syntax immaculate dawn.  
But inside the soul,  
A void played its role,  
And the form was semantically gone.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Quiet Impossibility" icon="eye-closed">
The form was immaculate, *clean*—  
The best ever previously seen.  
Yet something inside  
Was missing with pride,  
A quiet that sat in between.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Marking Semantic Errors" icon="pencil">
Pale ink marked the *errors so fine*—  
Invisible errors in line.  
The grammar was sound,  
Yet something was found—  
A meaning that fell and resigned.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="The Soul of the Data" icon="skull">
The data arrived, so *correct*—  
Each field the machine would detect.  
Yet something had died  
Within, deep inside,  
And the soul was entirely suspect.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Perfectly Wrong Onboarding" icon="inbox-in">
A form came to onboard the new—  
The fields were completely *on queue*.  
But semantically void,  
The system destroyed,  
And nothing the oracle knew.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Grammatically Perfect Failure" icon="alert-circle">
A request was grammatically *right*—  
Each sentence in proper form, bright.  
But semantically dead,  
The meaning long fled,  
And the oracle marked it as blight.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Prayer Over Wrong Forms" icon="hands-praying">
She prayed over forms that were *wrong*—  
Though right on the surface, along.  
The validation would find  
Impossibilities blind,  
And send them to right where they don't belong.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Unprocessable and Immaculate" icon="ban">
The form was so *perfect* to see—  
Yet wholly unprocessable be.  
Sister Pale found the flaw  
Deep within the soul's law,  
And marked it "422—Let it be."  
</Limerick>
