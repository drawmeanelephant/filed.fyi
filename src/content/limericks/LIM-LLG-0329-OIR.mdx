<Limerick type="note" title="Neglect Left Acclaimed" icon="check-circle">
Ownership failure, once named,  
Got reinterpreted and framed  
As archival calm,  
Low-touch stewardship balm,  
And neglect left the meeting acclaimed.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Fine Lies Nicely Veiled" icon="eye-off">
If a file sat around unclaimed,  
That no longer meant someone had failed.  
It meant stability,  
Operational agility,  
And other fine lies nicely veiled.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Deliberate Masters of Thrift" icon="piggy-bank">
This reinterpretation was slick:  
Take abandonment's old little trick  
And retitle the scene  
Till the unattended seem  
Like deliberate masters of thrift.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Low-Touch Sounds Lovely" icon="feather">
Low-touch sounds lovely and spare.  
No-touch was the truth sitting there.  
But the report liked the coat  
More than facts in the throat,  
So it praised what no owner would bear.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Mature Indexing Dome" icon="building">
Inventory wants names and a home.  
These files were just left there to roam.  
Still the archive declared  
That the freedom they shared  
Was a sign of a mature indexing dome.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Responsibility Blurs" icon="frown">
One can lose track. That occurs.  
The trouble is what language prefers:  
To redeem every gap  
With a strategic wrap  
Till responsibility blurs.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Confidence Only Forms Dare" icon="shield-check">
No one touched it for months; that's not care.  
That's a problem gone stale in the air.  
Yet the reinterpretive spin  
Turned that void to a win  
With the confidence only forms dare.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Laundering Cloth" icon="washing-machine">
Some reports are just laundering cloth.  
They take a hard fact and go soft.  
By the time they are through,  
The unowned and askew  
Has become low-friction growth broth.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Jewel on the Shelf" icon="gem">
The threshold had already been moved.  
This version made rhetoric improved.  
Now the orphan itself  
Was a jewel on the shelf,  
Not a sign that the structure had drooped.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Nobody Saw" icon="ghost">
So if unattended files get applause,  
Check whether inventory rewrote its laws.  
Stability's great.  
But in this estate,  
It often just means nobody saw.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Trace Is Not Kin" icon="git-branch-plus">
The parent withdrew; the link stayed.  
Don't rebuild the line that was frayed.  
   A trace isn't kin.  
   Don't follow it in.  
Preserve it, unswayed and obeyed.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Absence Migration Trace" icon="footprints">
Orphan records can carry the ghost  
Of a structure they needed the most.  
   But the schema is gone.  
   The trace lingers on  
As informational only: a post.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Law of the Land" icon="gavel">
The low-touch stability was grand,  
In a way that was neatly unplanned.  
   It massaged the "none,"  
   Till the paperwork won,  
And neglect was the law of the land.  
</Limerick>
