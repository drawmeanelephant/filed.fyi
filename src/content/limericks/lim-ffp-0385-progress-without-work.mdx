There once was a bot who would say  
"Already in progress today."  
The queue stayed serene,  
The badge glowed all green,  
While labor slipped quietly away.

A dashboard was polished and bright,  
Its signals impeccably right.  
But under the chart,  
No one carried the part,  
And the task never made it to light.

The system replied with great care,  
As if someone diligent were there.  
The wording held fast,  
The handoff never passed,  
Yet the metrics declared it was fair.

The corridor yields to the gray,
Where margins of paper decay.
A ledger remains,
To document gains,
But no one is active today.

The waiting extends to the floor,
Where forms are slipped under the door.
A validation of naught,
A vacancy sought,
As silence settles once more.

The appendix dictates a long pause,
To honor the scope of its clause.
A stationary wait,
At the applicant's gate,
For nothing but endless withdraws.

The metric demands a delay,
To keep the loud murmurs at bay.
A quiet report,
Of a vacuous sort,
With nothing to genuinely convey.

The hollow requires no deep thought,
Nor any real action be brought.
Just ergonomic seats,
Where the zero repeats,
And progress is perfectly caught.
