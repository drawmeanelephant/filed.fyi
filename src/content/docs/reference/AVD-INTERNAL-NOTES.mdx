> This is not doctrine.  
> This is how we talk ourselves into doctrine.

- Do not erase problems.  
  Rename them into states where nobody can route a ticket.

- Where a gap appears in a wall,
  ask first if the narrative prefers “window.”

- “Emergent” is a forgiving word.
  Use it when you cannot yet explain why the pattern feels wrong.

- “Durable” is for things that keep reappearing in audits
  despite sincere efforts to nudge them away.

- “Curated absence” is dangerous.
  It will get overused.
  Reserve it for holes you are genuinely prepared to ignore.

- “Deferred assurance state” is safe.
  Time cannot file a counter-memo.

If a report purrs at you, re-run the sweep.
Cats and audits should not sound alike.
