# Room

Small committee room off the main hall.
One wall calendar shows last years parade route circled twice.
The projector hums even when unplugged.

Participants (partial):

- **BMD Clerk** – chairing, holding a marker and a calculator that never leaves basic mode.
- **Peatworthy Abstention Clerk** – carrying a memorial roll binder.
- **Lionell Pancake Auditor** – smelling faintly of syrup and agenda ink.

Recorder on from the first disagreement.

---

## Agenda

> 1. Honorary Absences – coverage vs quorum.  
>  2. Luncheon Assent – reception vs disposition.  
>  3. Breakfast Forecasts – optimism vs funds.

The agenda notes a single objective:

> “Align bands so the room, the minutes, and the dashboards can sit together without hissing.”

---

## Disagreement One: Honorary Absences

Peatworthy opens the memorial roll to the **Honorary Absences** section.

> PEATWORTHY:  
>  I will not delete them from the roll.  
>  They are part of the halls moral architecture.

> BMD CLERK:  
>  I am not asking you to delete them.  
>  I am asking you where you want them to count.

Lionell flips through the projector slides until a bar chart appears: *Attendance by Category – Last 4 Years*.
Honorary bars are pale, hovering above the physical count.

> LIONELL:  
>  When this goes on a screen, people see a full room.  
>  When I count chairs, I see the truth.

Peatworthy protests that truth is also who would come if they could.

BMD draws two boxes on the whiteboard:

- **Continuity** – “Who still belongs here?”  
- **Quorum** – “Who can raise a hand tonight?”

Proposal:

- Honorary and memorial names count in **Continuity coverage**.  
- Only present and excused absences count in **Quorum bands**.

> PEATWORTHY:  
>  The absent will see the quorum chart.

> BMD CLERK:  
>  Then write the label carefully.  
>  “Bodies in the room for this vote.”  
>  They will know which one they are not.

The disagreement does not resolve.
It is recorded as:

- **Band decision:** split continuity and quorum bands.  
- **Moral status:** “feels needlessly sharp, tolerated for now.”

---

## Disagreement Two: Luncheon Assent

Next slide: minutes from a prior lunch, with **reception summary** and **disposition** fields highlighted.

> LIONELL:  
>  The problem is not the warmth.  
>  The problem is that the warmth sat directly above the line where decisions live.

He reads aloud:

- “Broad support in room.”  
- “General assent audible.”  
- “Everybody seemed on board.”

> PEATWORTHY:  
>  That is honestly what happened.

> BMD CLERK:  
>  I do not dispute that.  
>  I dispute that it equals “Approved: $1,500.”

On the dashboard, a gauge labeled **Community Support** sits next to one labeled **Authorized Spending**.
Both needles point into green.

Lionell taps the second.

> LIONELL:  
>  This is lying in a very polite way.

Discussion:

- Some members insist that public gratitude should “count for something.”
- Others fear being the person who tells the room its applause was empty.

BMD suggests a new intermediate metric:

- **Courtesy-Adjacent Obligations** – line items the room publicly embraced but never actually voted on.

Dashboard change:

- Lunch events now show:
  - **Reception:** a band from red (awkward) to green (warm).  
  - **Disposition:** one of:
    - “Authorized,”  
    - “Referred,”  
    - “Courtesy-adjacent, pending.”

> PEATWORTHY:  
>  That label is going to hurt someone’s feelings.

> LIONELL:  
>  So did sending the treasurer two envelopes and a note that said “Approved at lunch.”

The disagreement ends on procedural terms:

- **Language adopted:** “Reception is not approval” appears in the minutes template footer.  
- **Feeling logged:** “Room remains mildly offended by the distinction.”

---

## Disagreement Three: Breakfast Optimism

Final slide: the pancake breakfast recap.

The chart shows three lines:

- **Projected Proceeds** – steep and hopeful.  
- **Spoken Gratitude** – flat at 100%.  
- **Counted Cash** – lagging behind both.

> LIONELL:  
>  We announced four scholarships before we cleared two.  
>  The shortfall was covered by private guilt and additional muffins.

> PEATWORTHY:  
>  The town did come through.

> BMD CLERK:  
>  Yes.  
>  After the fact.  
>  After we turned a feeling into a liability.

Argument:

- Whether it is acceptable to show **forecasted funds** as green on dashboards when everyone expects benevolence to fill the gap.
- Whether announcing aspirational scholarship counts is necessary to keep giving lively, or irresponsible.

BMD proposes a separate band:

- **Aspirational Commitments** – pledges and breakfast-announced amounts that have not yet cleared.

New rule for breakfast dashboards:

- Any amount announced before the second syrup spill is:
  - shown in **Aspirational** purple, not green, and
  - annotated: “Subject to pancakes, weather, and actual envelopes.”

> LIONELL:  
>  That caption is unserious.

> BMD CLERK:  
>  So is committing a budget before we’ve counted the cashbox.

Peatworthy argues that donors will be embarrassed by seeing “aspirational” next to their generosity.
BMD replies that donors are already covering the gap created by enthusiasm dressed as certainty; the label simply follows reality.

The disagreement is left in a fragile truce:

- **Adopted:** booster charts must distinguish **forecast**, **announced**, and **received**.  
- **Unsettled:** whether doing so makes the room feel less trusted.

---

## Closing Note

As the meeting ends, the projector displays a composite view:

- **Continuity Coverage:** high, thanks to honorary and memorial presence.  
- **Quorum:** lower, bodies only.  
- **Courtesy-Adjacent Obligations:** a modest but nonzero band.  
- **Aspirational Commitments:** small, purple, and insistently visible.

Margin note, written later in another hand:

> “The metrics are not kinder than the rooms.  
>  They are only asked to be honest about where kindness started paying the bills.”
