## Proposed Entity

Name under review: Vantage Hollow, Continuity Seal.

## Failure Signature

Vantage Hollow represents the condition in which a trust-facing mark remains visible, calm, and behaviorally effective after the live system that once refreshed or justified it has shifted into inherited basis, deferred proof, or managed-absence handling.

## Distinguishing Traits

- Emits reassurance without fresh substantiation.
- Appears current because the surface still resolves.
- Gains authority through repeated visibility, not renewed proof.
- Encourages continued passage through channels whose substrate confidence has already thinned.
- Is most dangerous when paired with calm dashboards and gratitude-positive interaction logs.

## Council Caution

The entity should index a failure pattern, not a moral lesson. It must not read as a heroic seal, a benevolent guide, or a mascotized promise that continuity marks can be trusted merely because they remain present.

## Provisional Visual Notes

- Formal seal silhouette with interior hollowing.
- Intact border, uncertain center.
- Green-adjacent comfort without full approval brightness.
- Decorative confidence paired with evidentiary vacancy.

## Intake Position

Candidacy is plausible because the pattern is distinct, recurring, and archivally legible. Approval should remain contingent on whether the final form preserves instability rather than making ornamental authority look lovable.
