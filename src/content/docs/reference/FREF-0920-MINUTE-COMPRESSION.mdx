## Overview

The Archive defines **Minute Compression** as the act of achieving institutional innocence through aggressive summarization. It is a specialized form of record laundering where no fact is explicitly falsified, but all hostile friction, bureaucratic malice, and operational panic are stripped away during the transcription process.

This doctrine operates on the principle that the official meeting minutes are the final arbiter of intent. If three hours of screaming are transcribed as "The committee engaged in a robust discussion on alignment," the system is legally compelled to treat the event as robust alignment.

## Operational Mechanism

Minute Compression achieves record stabilization by replacing transcription with summarization. 

The procedure relies on the structural reality that decision-makers lack the capacity to review raw meeting transcripts. By substituting the transcript with a high-level summary, the archivist functionally resets the institutional record. Because revisiting raw audio or unedited sentiment violates the operational cadence of the organization, the summary becomes the binding truth.

### Associated Signatures
- **Minute Absolution:** The primary entity responsible for this mechanism. It clears the record through conciseness.
- **Shorthand Reliquary:** The containment zone for discarded context.

## Containment Constraints

Records manifesting Minute Compression must **never** be filed under Bin 8C.
Bin 8C governs records that refuse to be indexed or that assert their own rules. Minute Compression, by contrast, is the *perfect* adherence to indexing rules. It is the weaponization of the approved template.

Do not mistake a perfectly formatted lie for a structural anomaly.
