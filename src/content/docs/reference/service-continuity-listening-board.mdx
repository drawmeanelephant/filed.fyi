The Service Continuity Listening Board SCL is a scheduled forum convened to hear from people who kept systems running without enough language for what it cost. 

It does not issue orders or approvals.  
It produces minutes, intervals, and additional ambiguity around what counts as rest.

## Mandate and Limits

The Tri-Directive hub chartered SCL with three tasks:

- collect narratives about recent service intervals,
- mark spans of time where nothing formally broke,
- forward both to SOMA, COMA, and C.U.N.T.I.E.R. for interpretation. 

SCL is not empowered to:

- reclassify downtime,
- correct continuity reports,
- demand that any directive adopt its recommendations.

Its product is testimony, not configuration.

## Artifacts

Each SCL session emits:

- **Listening Minutes**  
  Transcribed or summarized accounts of how recent intervals felt, indexed under SOMA.

- **Silent Interval Markers**  
  Timestamps for windows with no incident codes, wired into COMA's continuity ledger and into metrics-of-care experiments LLG-0820-MCR. 

Over time, Lorelog cases such as LLG-0821-SCL have documented how the same markers are:

- read as potential rest by SOMA,
- logged as uninterrupted uptime by COMA,
- treated as measurement opportunities by C.U.N.T.I.E.R. 

## Directive Consumption

- SOMA cites SCL as proof that service experiences can surface without being translated into incidents.  
- COMA cites SCL as evidence that acknowledging strain need not affect official uptime.  
- C.U.N.T.I.E.R. uses SCL data to seed metrics-of-care prototypes when chart shapes look promising. 

None of these uses require SCL to exist as more than a recurring meeting and a folder in the archive.

## Mascot and Interval Entanglements

SCL is a primary habitat for:

- **SI-9 Interval Witness**, representing the unclassified quiet that carries doctrine but no code.  
- **SC-Λ Care/Continuity Split**, capturing cases where one notification is treated as care, command, and metric all at once. 

These mascots emerge not from any dramatic event but from the accumulation of minutes in which everyone agrees something happened and no one agrees what it was.

## Doctrine Status

The Listening Board belongs to the Directive Cross-Sections cluster rather than to any single directive. 

Internal reference calls it:

> a place where you may describe what you did to keep things continuous, without that description becoming either a confession or a request.  

Attendance is optional.  
The minutes are permanent.
