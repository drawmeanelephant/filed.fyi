Queue Theatre governs the condition in which intake, acknowledgment, and progress surfaces continue behaving as if handling remains active after execution depth has become weak, displaced, or absent.

The doctrine exists because queue systems often fail decoratively before they fail administratively.

## Foundational rule

A queue may remain operationally persuasive after it has ceased being operationally trustworthy.

Persuasion here includes:
- timely acknowledgment
- movement between reassuring states
- emotionally calming language
- freshness markers
- dashboard-visible attention

None of these, alone or together, prove that work crossed into recoverable execution.

## Core distinction

Queue Theatre differs from adjacent conditions:

### Delay
Work remains materially underway, but slower than expected.

### Draft-state
The work has not yet entered public handling surfaces.

### Managed absence
The system has already begun formalizing the absence or ambiguity of the missing basis.

### Adjacent correctness
The summary remains plausible enough to escape challenge while still naming the wrong nearby truth.

Queue Theatre often precedes managed absence and often recruits adjacent correctness to preserve calm.

## Diagnostic criteria

A queue may be classified as queue-theatrical when three or more of the following persist:

1. Acknowledgment is faster than intervention.
2. State changes outnumber recoverable work events.
3. Ownership cannot be stably reconstructed from visible progress.
4. Freshness markers reduce escalation without improving outcomes.
5. Status language implies handling more confidently than evidence permits.
6. Review bodies treat responsiveness as partial substitute for delivery.
7. External readers experience the lane as attentive while internal actors experience it as hollow.

## Theatre classes

### QTHR-1 Benign staging
Surface rituals modestly exceed underlying motion, but delivery remains mostly intact.

### QTHR-2 Compensatory staging
Visible queue activity is intensified to stabilize confidence during staffing, routing, or tooling weakness.

### QTHR-3 Substitution staging
Signs of handling begin to function as partial substitutes for handling itself.

### QTHR-4 Inherited staging
A successor queue inherits rituals, language, and obligations from an earlier system without inheriting equivalent execution depth.

### QTHR-5 Persistence theatre
The lane becomes culturally known for looking healthy longer than it remains able to act.

## Relation to assurance language

Queue Theatre is often sustained by calm wording:
- under review
- in progress
- awaiting alignment
- follow-up in motion
- continuity preserved
- active handling state

These phrases are not prohibited. They become doctrinally suspect when they continue to circulate after evidentiary confidence has thinned.

## Relation to managed absence

Once the missing basis becomes discussable, Queue Theatre frequently migrates into managed absence:
- CAAR when everyone knows the work is not really happening and routes around that knowledge politely
- STCP when successor workflow language changes but inherited handling burdens remain
- AAOA when the main surviving proof of activity is archival citation, dashboard residue, or procedural memory

## Approved language

Preferred phrases:
- visible uptake without execution depth
- queue remains persuasive, recovery uncertain
- responsiveness preserved, delivery basis thin
- staged motion, partial labor trace
- active handling surface, unrecoverable completion confidence

Disallowed phrases:
- the queue moved, therefore the work happened
- acknowledgment counts as action
- active status is sufficient proof of intervention
- no escalation means no problem
- the dashboard looked healthy, so the lane was healthy

RoboShirker is the preferred mascot witness where Queue Theatre becomes person-shaped enough to recur across incidents.
