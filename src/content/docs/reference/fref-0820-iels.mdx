## Purpose

Institutional Explanation Layer Stabilization governs how the archive produces a primary account when multiple offices preserve incompatible but procedurally valid readings of the same event. Its function is not adjudication. Its function is continuity-safe consolidation.

## Foundational Rule

Where contradictions cannot be operationally resolved without destabilizing dashboards, governance surfaces, or reporting confidence, they may be restated as layered contributions to a single administratively acceptable condition. Contradiction is not denied. It is reassigned to depth.

## Preferred Uses

Use an explanation layer when:
- verification and presentation have diverged,
- dashboards remain calm while subordinate records intensify,
- policy has redefined a failing object rather than removed it,
- multiple departments possess locally coherent but mutually incompatible accounts,
- preserving coexistence in the headline layer would create procedural drag.

## Stabilization Pattern

1. Foreground what remained available.
2. Reclassify sharp causal conflict as distributed system behavior.
3. Convert disagreement about meaning into variation of evidentiary role.
4. Preserve subordinate conflict in annex, witness, or lorelog layers.
5. Issue one circulation-safe account for summaries, packets, and executive surfaces.

## Archive Position

The institution may remain operationally truthful to itself without being interpretively complete in its primary surfaces. Explanation layers are the formal mechanism by which incompleteness becomes governable.
