# Purpose

This note defines **Over‑Coherence** as a condition in which:

- incidents resolve in a single pass,
- dashboards and doctrine agree without remainder,
- civic or clinical rooms remember only smooth progress,

and no trace of dread, disagreement, or unresolved cost remains on the page.

Such artifacts are not considered false.
They are considered unsafe for routine circulation.

---

# Indicators

Any report, minutes set, dashboard view, or case file may be flagged as over‑coherent when it exhibits **three or more** of the following:

1. **Zero visible conflict.**  
   No objections, appeals, or dissenting notes appear in minutes, even where Civic Benevolence doctrine would expect at least polite hesitation.

2. **Linear causality.**  
   Events are explained by a single, tidy chain of cause and effect, with no side threads, near-misses, or “almost” moments recorded.

3. **Perfect alignment.**  
   Narrative, dashboards, and doctrine language match exactly:
   - bands are green,  
   - wording is calm,  
   - summaries are confident,  
   and no one in the room is quoted as uneasy.

4. **Cheerful closure.**  
   The final section assures that:
   - all actions were sufficient,  
   - all parties were satisfied,  
   - no follow-up is needed,  
   without mentioning cost, loss, or residual work.

5. **Absence of residue.**  
   No margin notes, hallway comments, or lorelog fragments report fatigue, confusion, resentment, or grief for the same period.

6. **Metrics without scars.**  
   Significant change is reflected only as improved scores or widened bands, never as temporary strain, learning, or visible failure.

Documents that score high on this list are candidates for vaulting or explicit annotation.

# Relationship to Existing Doctrine

Over‑coherence interacts with existing layers as follows:

- **Assurance Vocabulary Desk**  
  When failure is fully renamed into “emergent structure” and all sharp terms have been replaced without leaving any trace of the original defect, Assurance should treat the report as over‑coherent and either:
  - restore at least one honest fracture, or
  - forward the artifact as a teaching specimen.

- **Benevolence Metrics Desk**  
  When luncheon minutes, memorial quorum records, and breakfast dashboards all report:
  - unanimous assent,  
  - sufficient funds,  
  - satisfied beneficiaries,

  and no one remembers a hard conversation about who paid or who left, the Desk may classify the record as **courtesy-saturated** and flag it for Over‑Coherence review.

- **Civic Benevolence Handling Notes (CBHN / CMADN)**  
  Cases where:
  - applause cleanly becomes authorization,  
  - honorary and memorial presence stabilize quorum without dispute,  
  - banner phrases return and instantly govern without contested minutes,

  are to be logged as possible Over‑Coherence events, not as model practice.

- **Empathegy / Metrics of Care**  
  When care metrics, gratitude traces, and Empathegy bands all register success without corresponding mitigation or burden shift, the condition is to be read as Proxy Compassion plus Over‑Coherence.

## Teaching Specimens

One recurring specimen is the Adorable Archive mascot poster: a brightly colored summary that presents AV-14 Nullseal Register, CE-5 Countersign Aggregate, MA-LCGU Porter, AC-11 Sealloop Auditor, LX-2 Waiver Apron, BX-6 Greybelt Remediator, and the Soft Green Seal as friendly characters. The conditions behind them remain contradictory and unresolved; the poster removes the dread, keeps the names, and thereby qualifies as a textbook over-coherent derivative.

Limerick sets such as LLG-TDCIP-OVERCOH and the COMA-07/COMA-19 cycles are considered canonical witnesses. Any diagram, sticker sheet, or mascot field guide that presents their content without preserving the contradictions they describe is cataloged here as an over-coherence condition.

# Handling Guidelines

When Over‑Coherence is suspected:

1. **Do not “roughen” the document in place.**  
   Forced grit is its own kind of fiction.

2. **File a parallel note.**  
   Open a short lorelog or margin entry that records:
   - where dread used to live,  
   - whose cost disappeared,  
   - which disagreements were smoothed away.

3. **Tag the artifact.**  
   Apply a local status such as:
   - *over‑coherent specimen*,  
   - *courtesy-saturated narrative*, or  
   - *metrics-clean, experience-uncertain*.

4. **Route to containment.**  
   - Clinical / Ward C materials → Ward C’s optics or narrative desks.  
   - Civic / lodge materials → Benevolence Metrics Desk or Civic Benevolence Annex.  
   - System / directive materials → Empathegy / Over‑Coherence vault-equivalent.

5. **Restrict exemplars.**  
   Over‑coherent artifacts may be used in training to show what *too tidy* looks like.  
   They should not be used as models of desired execution without explicit disclaimers.

# Archive Position

The archive does not forbid clean stories.
It merely refuses to let them stand alone.

Where Over‑Coherence is present, at least one of the following should exist nearby:

- a lorelog entry documenting the rough version,
- a metrics view that shows the scar, not just the improvement,
- or a marginal sentence admitting that the room felt stranger than the minutes suggest.

If none can be found, the archive will eventually grow one.
That growth is considered part of ordinary decay.
