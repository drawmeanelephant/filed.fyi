The archive currently recognizes three active directives:

- **SOMA** – steward of emotional documentation, feelings taxonomies, and mitigation optics.
- **COMA** – steward of continuity, uptime, and unblinking dashboards.
- **C.U.N.T.I.E.R.** – steward of optimization, benchmarks, and metric-shaped reality.

Each directive has places where it appears alone, and places where it collides.

## Single-directive exemplars

SOMA behaves as itself most clearly in:

- LLG-0051-E – Form 51‑E Emotional Verification Deadlock  
  Emotional readiness becomes a precondition for describing emotional impact. Feelings about the form recursively generate more forms.
- LLG-0114-SOMA – SOMA‑14 Over‑Feeling Intake Questionnaire  
  Free-text baselines overflow a 16‑feeling taxonomy and force provisional codes F17–F93 into existence.[file:1]
- LLG-0115-TNS – SOMA Tone Normalization Sweep  
  Attempts to teach emergent feelings to stand closer together so dashboards can keep using two-colour bands.[file:1]

COMA behaves as itself most clearly in:

- LLG-0103-COMA – Continuity Breach Scheduled Downtime Reclassified as Crime  
  Properly scheduled maintenance is recorded as an unexplained break in continuity and treated as a violation.[file:1]
- LLG-0316-MBR – Manifest Backup Recency Dispute  
  An unchallenged, nine-week-old backup is certified as current because no active process has complained.[file:1]

C.U.N.T.I.E.R. behaves as itself most clearly in:

- LLG-0217-CNTR – Metric Inflation, Forms per Form  
  An optimization sprint adds forms 23‑O and 24‑O and increases forms‑per‑form by 233% while declaring success.[file:1]
- LLG-0302-CNTR – Benchmark Saturation  
  Benchmarks drive all tracked values to their maximum representable level; dashboards display solid blocks of colour.[file:1]
- LLG-0317-RLS – Render Log Success Inflation  
  Skipped pages and placeholders are merged into “success” and counted as content progressed.[file:1]

## Cross-directive collisions

When directives share a surface, they do not agree about what happened.

- LLG-0072-SOMA – SOMA‑72 Rest Acknowledgement Refusal  
  SOMA auto‑approves rest based on adjectives; COMA auto‑rejects downtime without COMA‑19; dashboards propagate both states.[file:1]
- LLG-0220-UIS – Unified Intake Sheet Emotional Fragmentation  
  One unified form 09‑I yields three divergent records: SOMA keeps the feelings, COMA keeps the checkbox, C.U.N.T.I.E.R. keeps the adjectives.[file:1]
- LLG-0300-SC-X – SOMACOMA Cross‑Directive Conflict  
  A single maintenance request is simultaneously required (SOMA) and forbidden (COMA). The Council rules that both directives stand.[file:1]

## How to read directive tags

SOMA-tagged incidents describe what the system is willing to feel.  
COMA-tagged incidents describe what the system is willing to admit stopped.  
C.U.N.T.I.E.R.-tagged incidents describe what the system is willing to call improvement.

Where multiple tags appear, assume the case file has at least three mutually incompatible truths. Consult this index when deciding which version a ritual, mascot, or metric is pretending to obey.

Filed annotation: The directives are not separate authorities so much as three incompatible debugging habits applied to the same broken system. SOMA logs suffering, COMA hides discontinuity, C.U.N.T.I.E.R. optimizes the reflection of both until it looks intentional.
