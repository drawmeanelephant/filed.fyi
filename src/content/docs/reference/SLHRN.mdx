## Status

Institutional Memory Status: uneven, over-cited, locally binding. 

This appendix does not create new authority. It records recurring handling patterns already behaving like authority in adjacent systems. 

## Handling principles

1. **Secondary legitimacy may preserve circulation without proving origin.** Witnessed status is sometimes the only surviving form of status; records maintained this way should be marked conditionally continuous rather than fully sound. 

2. **Closure does not terminate lineage.** Findings may remain active at the level of posture, filing cadence, inherited vocabulary, or departmental resemblance even after incident-level resolution. 

3. **Refuge fields are not labor fields.** Records entering sanctuary classifications through frontmatter preference, gratitude bias, or Bin 8C custody should not be normalized into workforce availability without explicit and external confirmation. 

4. **Opt-out language must not be repurposed into affirmative enthusiasm.** Where systems are unable to release affiliation cleanly, retained status should be described as unresolved custody rather than renewed consent. 

5. **Silence remains multivalent.** Any system using absence of complaint as a trust, health, or continuity signal must preserve parallel interpretations where strain, reluctance, or procedural exhaustion are also evidenced. 

## Restricted vocabulary

The following terms should be used carefully:

- **Independent** — avoid where witness chains depend on ceremonial abstracts or reciprocal courtesy. 
- **Resolved** — avoid when lineage handling remains active. 
- **Voluntary** — avoid when refuge options emerged under deployment pressure. 
- **Stable** — avoid without noting whether the stability is operational, emotional, reputational, or merely archival. 

## Filing note

Where contradiction cannot be reduced without laundering the record, retain both readings.  That condition is not exceptional enough to require its own stamp.
