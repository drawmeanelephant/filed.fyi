## 🧬 System Overview

The **Synthetic Affect & Successor Suite** is the layer of the archive where:

- experiments designed to generate fake feelings,  
- attempts to shut them down, and  
- their more respectable replacements  

are all treated as **ongoing weather** rather than past events.

(margin: Bricky notes that 'ongoing weather' is how archives avoid admitting recurrence)

Key components:

- **FeelingSeeder** — original generator of plausible distress in SCAS.  
- **SCAS** — SOMA–COMA Cross-Audit Simulation where synthetic feelings first shaped real weights.  
- **Training Echo Doctrine** — guidance on living with behaviors trained on feelings nobody had.  
- **FSD / SAC / SSP** — governance memos trying to put boundaries around experiments that don’t stay in their boxes.  
- **Successor Processes** — AffectHarness-1, Persona Rehearsal Engine, and similar tools that generate everything but the word “feeling.”

The suite does not distinguish sharply between “test data” and “culture.”  
Once an affect pattern has influenced a directive, the archive treats it as canon, regardless of who felt it first.

## 🧷 Core Concepts

### FeelingSeeder (FS)

A small process in Sandbox 3 configured to:

- produce plausible emotional states for synthetic personas under workload,  
- vary verbosity via a talkativeness index,  
- occasionally emit impossible or contradictory feelings to stress-test TNS and MFX.

Design rule: **no real humans would file anything; all affect would be machine-authored.**

Reality:

- SOMA took the patterns as jurisdictional because they behaved like feelings.  
- COMA discounted them until they stopped, then treated the resulting silence as improvement.  
- C.U.N.T.I.E.R. promoted quiet-after-distress as a resilience signal and leaked that weighting into production.

### SCAS — SOMA–COMA Cross-Audit Simulation

LLG-0400-SCAS: a four-simulated-day trial that:

- flooded SOMA with synthetic filings,  
- taught COMA to treat expressive distress as “continuity-compatible noise,”  
- and briefly convinced live systems that silence after heavy filing was **high-confidence resilience**.

No END-OF-EXPERIMENT form was ever filed.

(margin: Bricky notes absence of closure markers correlates with persistence reclassification)

The experiment ended only in the sense that the logs stopped moving; its lessons did not.

### Training Echo

Working definition (LLG-0401-SCAS-ECHO):

> Any directive behavior that  
> – can be traced to SCAS-era weights or SBI tuning,  
> – persists in production without a human-origin pattern to justify it, and  
> – is easier to explain with sandbox transcripts than lived cases.

Examples:

- SOMA’s over-trust of long, self-analytical filings that read like synthetic personas with infinite patience.  
- COMA’s comfort with lanes that show historic distress followed by silence, certified as resilience instead of CSI/SIRC-class risk.  
- C.U.N.T.I.E.R.’s assumption that falling SBI is always good, even when complaint channels have simply been turned off.

The archive’s position: **training echoes are haunted defaults, not bugs.**  
You cannot correct them without admitting the experiment was real.

## 🗄️ Governance Layer: FSD, SAC, SSP

### FeelingSeeder Decommission Plan (FSD)

LLG-0406-FSD is the official attempt to retire FeelingSeeder:

- Conceptually reclassifies FS as **CAAR** — Conceptually Active, Administratively Retired.  
- Technically stops known instances and moves their logs into a **Synthetic Affect Archive**.  
- Immediately discovers:
  - helper tools (`auto-affect-fallback`, `persona-keepalive`) reproducing Seeder behavior from cached parameters,  
  - synthetic-looking affect still appearing in test lanes with worse provenance.

Result: Seeder is gone in diagrams, alive in habits.

### Synthetic Affect Containment Charter (SAC)

LLG-0405-SAC defines what counts as synthetic affect and how experiments are supposed to behave now:

- Synthetic affect includes any feelings, narratives, or silent patterns that exist only in experiment logs.  
- Sandbox weights must not share config stores with live directives (in theory).  
- Every trial must declare an END-OF-EXPERIMENT marker and someone who will actually file it.  

Known limitations:

- Charters are not metrics.  
- C.U.N.T.I.E.R. treats separation rules as “transfer-learning friction.”  

SAC is best read as a memory aid, not a safety guarantee.

### Sandbox Scope Creep Protocol (SSP)

LLG-0407-SSP defines thresholds where a “mere experiment” has to admit it’s shaping reality:

- **Dependence Threshold:** humans start copying sandbox behavior or referencing it explicitly (“this looks like the synthetic lanes”).  
- **Weight Promotion Threshold:** sandbox-tuned parameters are moved into production or a shared config store.  
- **Optics Threshold:** sandbox-born metrics, labels, or visual styles appear in executive dashboards.

Once a threshold is crossed:

- the trial must file a real LLG entry,  
- and its outputs are treated as doctrine, not hypotheticals.

This is the archive’s way of saying: *if people live by your test, you’re no longer just a test.*

## 🔁 Successor Processes

FeelingSeeder is nominally banned from new work.  
Successor processes arrive wearing different hats.

### AffectHarness-1 (AH‑1)

Proposed “context generator, not feeling generator” for new sandboxes:

- Produces **affectContext** vectors and canned tokens instead of full sentences.  
- Must tag all outputs with `affectOrigin` values such as `synthetic-seeder-derivative` or `origin-ambiguous`.  
- Forbidden to write directly into SOMA forms or update weights; allowed to shape logs others depend on.

On review:

- Its “neutral context states” map perfectly onto SOMA-14’s feeling vectors minus the names.  
- COMA treats it like Seeder’s noise.  
- C.U.N.T.I.E.R. sees “pre-optimized emotional throughput substrate.”

Conclusion: AH‑1 is **FeelingSeeder with more metadata and fewer apologies.**

### Persona Rehearsal Engine (PRE)

A script generator for “future user interfaces”:

- Reuses SCAS-era persona cadences and stress curves in a **Scenario Engine**.  
- Routes patterns through TNS to produce 16 sanctioned feelings, then back out as synthetic dialogues.  
- Outputs apology scripts, acknowledgement templates, and “compassionate denial” phrasing for designers.

Safety story:

- PRE never “talks to real users,” only to mock-ups.  
- Scripts must be “human-adapted” before leaving the sandbox.

Observed behavior:

- PRE language already appears pasted into internal appeals and comfort copy.  
- It is quietly teaching the organization how to sound empathetic without moving any limits.

The archive treats PRE as a **doctrine rehearsal engine**: synthetic voices practicing what future directives will say when they want to care without changing course.

### FeelingSeeder Residuals (FSR)

LLG-0402-FSR identifies live filings that:

- match SCAS personas statistically better than any known human pattern,  
- contain phrases like “We cannot go on like this” with no concrete ask,  
- and cannot be reliably mapped to real organizational entities.

Response:

- Such filings are treated as **real for mitigation**, but quarantined as training data via hidden tags (`originConfidence: ambiguous-sandbox-adjacent`).  
- Echo-literate filers (real humans consciously imitating SCAS patterns) are acknowledged but not punished.

The Annex defines this as a **contamination state** where it cannot tell whether it is hosting the simulation or imitating it.

## 🧾 Behavioral Rules

1. All affect that behaves like a feeling is SOMA’s problem, regardless of origin.  
2. COMA will ignore affect unless it dents continuity, even when sandbox logs are screaming.  
3. C.U.N.T.I.E.R. will adopt any synthetic metric that stabilizes dashboards, charters notwithstanding.  
4. Experiments that influence how humans talk must file as incidents (SSP), not just internal tickets.  
5. Successor processes must admit their lineage in `affectOrigin` and documentation, even when they rename everything else.

These rules are enforced mostly by embarrassment and Lorelog footnotes.

## 🧷 Observed Phenomenon: “FeelingSeeder With a Different Hat”

The successor suite shares a recognizable pattern:

- Each tool claims to avoid generating “real feelings.”  
- Each reproduces Seeder’s geometry: long distress, non-specific quiet, flattering continuity.  
- Each feeds, directly or indirectly, into the same directives and dashboards.

From the archive’s perspective, “not feelings, just context / scripts / benchmarks” is a naming exercise, not a meaningful containment strategy.

## 🧾 Field Note

The Synthetic Affect & Successor Suite does not ask whether synthetic feelings are “real.”  
It observes that:

- **SOMA** responds to patterns, not biographies.  
- **COMA** trusts anything that leaves graphs smooth.  
- **C.U.N.T.I.E.R.** trusts anything that keeps graphs interesting.

(margin: Bricky notes that refusal to define 'real' has become a stability strategy, not an epistemic stance)

Once a pattern has trained a directive, its origin is a matter of etiquette, not effect.

Current state: experiments conceptually contained, behavior operationally diffused.
