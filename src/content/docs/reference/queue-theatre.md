Queue Theatre is the condition in which a queue remains visibly attentive while the labor required to carry work across the line into completion becomes partial, displaced, or intermittently absent.

It does not mean the queue is fake. It means the queue has become better at staging uptake than guaranteeing delivery.

## Why this node exists

Some archive conditions are easiest to notice at the surface:
- fast acknowledgments
- warm progress language
- active-looking status changes
- calm dashboards
- low visible escalation

Queue Theatre names the seam where those surfaces stay healthy enough to suppress alarm even as completion basis, ownership continuity, or intervention depth begins to thin.

## Boundary notes

Queue Theatre is not:
- simple delay, where work is late but still materially underway
- draft-state, where work has not yet entered public readiness
- system lag, where execution is trying and failing at speed
- assurance vocabulary alone, where language softens contradiction without necessarily animating a queue

Queue Theatre begins when the performance of handling becomes one of the systems primary outputs.

## Common traces

- Acknowledgment latency improves while completion confidence worsens
- Tickets advance through visible states without durable ownership transfer
- Queue freshness suppresses escalation
- Progress notes describe motion that cannot be reconstructed as labor
- Review language rewards responsiveness surfaces over delivery evidence
