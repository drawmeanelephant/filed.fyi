# Mandate

The Scan Reinterpretation Bloc (SRB) provides interpretive overlays for:

- integrity scans,
- shelf and directory inventories, and
- periodic orphan enumerations.

Scanners report what is.
The Bloc suggests how it may safely be said.

---

# Operating Assumptions

1. **Scans see sharply.**  
   Instruments know only present vs. absent, owned vs. unowned, expected vs. not.

2. **Reports must live with time.**  
   After three or four reporting cycles, some absences have grown roots.  
   Wording should respect this.

3. **Optics are also a metric.**  
   If a finding destabilizes more than it repairs, it may be eligible for reinterpretation.

The Bloc does not falsify scan outputs.
It classifies the kinds of truth they are allowed to become.

---

# From Raw Finding to Curated Absence

Scans may emit lines resembling:

- “17 items missing from expected set.”
- “9 records present without owner.”
- “Shelf segment uncovered in sweep.”
- “Index references unknown object IDs.”

For public reports, preferred renderings are:

- “17 items in **deferred presence**, currently not required for active cycles.”
- “9 records operating as **independent archival units** within tolerated ranges.”
- “Shelf segment recognized as a **low-touch inspection zone**.”
- “Index includes **candidate IDs** not yet mapped to current working sets.”

The difference is not in count.
It is in where the count is allowed to lead.

---

# Thresholds and Patience

The SRB maintains informal patience thresholds:

- **One cycle:** absence may still be treated as a defect.
- **Two cycles:** absence has begun to demonstrate stability.
- **Three or more cycles:** absence is eligible for positive framing, subject to local comfort.

Recommended language by age of finding:

- First appearance: “Newly observed gap subject to follow-up.”
- Second appearance: “Persistent variance; continuity unaffected.”
- Third and later: “Stable curated absence / independent unit class.”

Concern is no longer the default after patience has been demonstrated.

---

# Orphan Inventory Guidance

An “orphan” is any record that:

- is present, and
- cannot be assigned to a living owner, and
- continues to be addressed by the system.

The SRB suggests the following evolution:

1. **Initial discovery:** “Unowned record detected; assignment unresolved.”
2. **After patience threshold:** “Low-maintenance unit; ownership not required for service.”
3. **With managed absence classification:** “Locally governed independent archival unit.”

If no vice-president or equivalent role expresses distress during this evolution, the last line shall be considered accurate enough for all practical purposes.

# Scan Language Crosswalk

```ts
// Scan term → SRB term → Notes
const scanCrosswalk = [
  ["missing", "in deferred presence", "Still counted, not currently reachable."],
  ["orphaned", "independent archival unit", "Remains useful without explicit owner."],
  ["gap", "curated absence", "Space that no longer demands filling."],
  ["unscanned", "low-touch inspection zone", "Area scheduled for calmer review."],
  ["mismatched index", "interpretive mismatch", "Pointers awaiting narrative alignment."],
];
```

Use sparingly.
Too much curation will attract doctrine.

---

# Interaction with Assurance Vocabulary

Where the Assurance Vocabulary Desk has already softened language:

- SRB terms should align, not compete.
- “Curated absence” and “deferred presence” are safe to share.
- “Independent archival unit” pairs well with “low-maintenance asset.”

Workflow:

1. Scan emits a raw finding.
2. SRB assigns age and class.
3. Assurance Desk wraps the description in continuity language, if needed.

All three layers remain technically correct.
Only one is allowed in slides.

---

# Notes on Overuse

Signs that reinterpretation has exceeded usefulness:

- Corrective actions no longer appear in any queue, only in narratives.
- New staff cannot tell which absences are deliberate and which simply endured.
- The same positive phrase appears in more than three unrelated contexts.

In such cases, SRB may quietly designate the phrase itself as a “legacy-stable euphemism” and seek a fresh one.

This process is not documented elsewhere.
