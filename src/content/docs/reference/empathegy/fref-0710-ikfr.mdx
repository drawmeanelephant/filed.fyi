# Intake Forms

## Purpose

Empathegy Intake Forms convert emotional conditions into administratively movable matter.

They are not designed to capture full reality.
They are designed to prevent full reality from arriving all at once.

This is considered a stabilizing function.

---

## Foundational Rule

No affective condition enters governance unmediated.

Before a feeling may be routed, it must pass through one or more of the following preparation layers:

1. naming,
2. scope reduction,
3. category matching,
4. admissibility check,
5. continuity review.

Where a condition cannot survive these preparations, the intake process may preserve it without allowing it to move.

---

## Intake Objectives

Empathegy intake seeks to answer five questions:

- What happened in sanctioned language.
- Whether it resembles a known condition.
- Whether it can be acted upon through existing pathways.
- Whether it will destabilize continuity interpretations.
- Whether the system can afford to understand it properly.

The final question is seldom written and often decisive.

---

## Form Classes

### 1. Declarative Intake

Used when the subject can name the condition directly within approved vocabulary.

Characteristics:
- low routing friction
- high classification compatibility
- strong dashboard survivability

Risk:
May reward rehearsed or institutionally literate distress over raw but urgent conditions.

### 2. Assisted Intake

Used when the system provides prompts, categories, examples, or reflective scaffolds to help prepare expression.

Characteristics:
- higher completion rate
- better comparability
- improved graphability

Risk:
Prompt structure may determine what becomes visible.

### 3. Narrative Intake

Used when structured categories are insufficient.

Characteristics:
- richer context
- higher witness value
- lower immediate routability

Risk:
Expensive to interpret, easy to annex, difficult to preserve at scale.

### 4. Silent or Proxy Intake

Used when the condition is inferred from surrounding signals rather than filed directly.

Characteristics:
- useful during suppression, fatigue, or reporting collapse
- often routed through secondary metrics

Risk:
Inference may become substitution.

---

## Required Intake Fields

All formal intake surfaces must preserve, at minimum:

- condition type or nearest class
- interval or timeframe
- subject or affected scope
- desired outcome, if any
- current continuity impact, if any
- uncertainty marker where interpretation is unstable

If any of these are absent, the form remains valid only under annotated handling.

---

## Routing Friction

Routing Friction is the amount of interpretive labor required before a filing can move.

### Low Friction
Clear category, clear ask, low contradiction.

### Moderate Friction
Some ambiguity, partial narrative burden, manageable translation cost.

### High Friction
Contradictory testimony, non-standard language, unclear path to action.

### Ritualized Friction
The filing is preserved through repeated clarifications, tone adjustments, witness additions, or form re-submissions until the burden of filing itself becomes part of the event.

Empathegy does not prohibit Ritualized Friction.
It simply declines to describe it as humane.

---

## Admissibility Drift

A condition may be true and still fail intake.

Common reasons include:
- too much contradiction,
- too much context dependence,
- too much care-coded language,
- insufficient category fit,
- excessive narrative load,
