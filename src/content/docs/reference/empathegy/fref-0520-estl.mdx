# Aesthetic Survival Layer

## Premise

Empathegy does not govern only feelings.
It also governs the visual and procedural signals by which participants remain institutionally legible.

When local design language is weak, borrowed correctness becomes a survival strategy.

This behavior is not aberrant.
It is expected.

---

## Core Rule

Within Empathegy systems, recognizable form outranks original form whenever recognizability improves review survival, deployment speed, or stakeholder confidence.

Originality may be admired.
Legibility is what gets routed.

---

## Aesthetic State Classes

### Native Coherence
Form emerges from actual domain constraints and remains structurally aligned with purpose.

### Borrowed Coherence
Form is imported from a higher-status or more legible system and functions adequately despite partial mismatch.

### Compensatory Coherence
Form is borrowed to stabilize uncertainty where no internal design confidence exists.

### Prestige Overlay
High-status visual grammar is applied to increase perceived competence without equivalent structural depth.

### Category Drift
Borrowed form introduces noticeable domain mismatch but remains administratively tolerated.

### Entropy Debt
A system accrues downstream confusion because surface legitimacy exceeds underlying functional alignment.

---

## Aesthetic Translation Drift Events

All category mismatches that do not immediately fail deployment are logged as Aesthetic Translation Drift Events, or ATDEs.

### ATDE-1 Benign Borrowing
Borrowed patterns improve legibility with minimal functional distortion.

### ATDE-2 Compensatory Mimicry
Borrowed patterns substitute for missing internal standards.

### ATDE-3 Prestige Laundering
Borrowed patterns import authority signals stronger than the system can justify.

### ATDE-4 Expectation Inflation
Surface quality raises assumptions that workflow, support, or reliability cannot satisfy.

### ATDE-5 Prestige Collapse
Surface legitimacy fails under contact with actual use, producing interpretive whiplash and governance cleanup.

---

## Evaluation Criteria

Aesthetic outputs are reviewed against the following questions:

1. Does the surface improve interpretability?
2. Does the borrowed grammar introduce false domain expectations?
3. Is the mismatch tolerable under current operational load?
4. Does the aesthetic increase approval probability?
5. Is the resulting entropy immediate, deferred, or ceremonial?

If the answer to Question 4 is yes and the answer to Question 5 is deferred, the system will often approve the surface.

This is working as designed.

---

## Relation to Emotional Governance

Aesthetic borrowing and emotional classification are parallel operations.

In both cases:
- unstructured reality is difficult to scale,
- recognizable forms are easier to govern,
- institutional systems reward what can be compared quickly.

The dashboard and the polished interface are siblings.
Both extract legibility from mess.

---

## Sanctioned Language

Preferred phrases:
- borrowed coherence
- prestige-adjacent surface
- compensatory structure formation
- temporary legitimacy gain
- structural entropy debt
- domain-misaligned but review-efficient

Disallowed phrases:
- fake
- derivative failure
- copied because no one knew what they were doing

Empathegy does not moralize imitation.
It routes it.

---

## Final Position

The goal of the Aesthetic Survival Layer is not to stop imitation.

The goals are:
- to detect imitation,
- to name its class,
- to estimate its debt,
- to preserve enough structure that the institution can survive what it has borrowed.
