# Interpretation Boundary Layer

## Purpose

The Interpretation Boundary Layer governs the transition between emotional reality and institutional handling.

Its purpose is not to determine whether a feeling is true.
Its purpose is to determine whether a feeling is process-compatible.

This distinction is foundational.

---

## Boundary Rule

No raw affect enters operational governance without passing an admissibility threshold.

Admissibility is determined by the following tests:

1. Can the signal be named consistently?
2. Can it be compared across subjects or intervals?
3. Can it survive summary without collapse?
4. Can it be routed without bespoke human interpretation?
5. Can it be represented without destabilizing continuity optics?

Failure on one or more tests does not invalidate the signal.
It changes the kind of institutional presence it is allowed to have.

---

## Intake Outcomes

### Admitted
Signal is classifiable, routeable, and eligible for standard governance.

### Admitted with Annotation
Signal is usable but requires local context, witness text, or dual-certification.

### Deferred
Signal appears meaningful but lacks sufficient structure for action.

### Annexed
Signal is preserved in a side channel because it exceeds headline representation tolerances.

### Excluded from Aggregate
Signal is retained locally but removed from summary outputs due to coherence failure or volatility burden.

---

## Admissibility Filters

### Naming Filter
Unclear or idiosyncratic expressions must be mapped into sanctioned vocabulary where possible.

### Coherence Filter
Signals that cannot be rendered as sufficiently stable trajectories may be marked high-friction.

### Comparability Filter
A signal that depends entirely on unique context may be preserved narratively but denied aggregate weight.

### Governance Filter
If no approved intervention pathway exists, the system may preserve the signal without promising action.

### Optics Filter
Signals likely to introduce unresolved contradiction into executive views may be demoted to annex visibility.

---

## Residue

Any portion of emotional reality that fails translation but remains contextually important is preserved as residue.

Residue may include:
- contradiction without hierarchy
- simultaneous relief and dread
- fatigue expressed as compliance
- gratitude attached to unmet need
- silence that reads as both rest and depletion

Residue is not error.
Residue is what remains after the system has finished preparing reality for itself.

---

## Provisional Handling Guidance

When interpretive uncertainty is high:

- preserve the source language where possible
- avoid premature optimization into score language
- prohibit top-line claims of health based solely on stable rendering
- attach witness interpretation if available
- prefer “insufficiently renderable” over “non-actionable” when the issue is representational rather than human

---

## Boundary Warning

Systems that tighten admissibility too aggressively do not reduce emotional variance.
They reduce what counts as visible.

Empathegy recognizes this as an acceptable governance risk, but not as a proof of care.
