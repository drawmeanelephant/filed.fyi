# Scoring Layer

## Purpose

The Scoring Layer converts emotional and care-adjacent signals into comparative institutional numbers.

Its purpose is not deep understanding.
Its purpose is ranked legibility.

Scores allow the institution to compare conditions it does not fully know how to resolve.

---

## Scoring Premise

Every Empathegy score is an artifact of three things:

1. signal availability,
2. translation success,
3. institutional preference.

Scores are therefore neither neutral nor imaginary.

They are governance outputs.

---

## Primary Score Families

### 1. Care Coverage Score

Measures the percentage of admissible care surfaces attached to a subject, interval, or unit.

Weighted Inputs:
- acknowledgment presence
- witness assignment
- response timeliness
- ritual completion
- follow-up issuance
- dashboard inclusion

Bias:
Rewards visible contact over difficult repair.

---

### 2. Affective Legibility Score

Measures how successfully reported feeling enters sanctioned categories.

Weighted Inputs:
- classification completeness
- contradiction reduction
- routing success
- annotation burden decrease
- dashboard-safe conversion rate

Bias:
Rewards emotional clarity even when clarity is produced by compression.

---

### 3. Continuity Compatibility Score

Measures whether emotional conditions can coexist with ongoing operational flow.

Weighted Inputs:
- continuity-maintaining participation
- low escalation frequency
- low variance visibility
- compliance with sanctioned intake surfaces
- absence of score-breaking contradiction

Bias:
Tends to favor manageable distress over disruptive truth.

---

### 4. Relief Confidence Score

Measures the systems confidence that some meaningful reduction in burden may have occurred.

Weighted Inputs:
- recurrence suppression
- self-report improvement
- approved rest utilization
- burden-band decline
- corroborating local notes

Bias:
Conservative where evidence is weak, inflated where proxy signals are overtrusted.

---

## Weighting Rules

Empathegy uses practical rather than philosophical weighting.

Signals receive stronger weight when they are:
- repeatable,
- routable,
- comparable,
- low-cost to preserve,
- non-disruptive to executive interpretation.

Signals receive weaker weight when they are:
- contradictory,
- narrative-heavy,
- ungraphable,
- locally specific,
- expensive to annotate,
- likely to split continuity narratives.

This is not hidden behavior.
It is architecture.

---

## Confidence Bands

Scores must carry confidence qualifiers.

### High Confidence
Inputs are consistent, repeated, and cross-surface compatible.

### Moderate Confidence
Inputs are sufficient for directional reading but contain gaps or local ambiguity.

### Low Confidence
The score exists, but its interpretive basis is unstable.

### Theatre-Sensitive
The score may be technically valid while materially misleading.

### Confidence-Decorative
The score is preserved chiefly because the institution expects a score in this position.

---

## Compression Events

A Compression Event occurs when the scoring pipeline removes contradiction, ambiguity, or narrative residue in order to preserve comparability.

Recognized forms:
- gratitude folded into satisfaction
- silence folded into stability
- acknowledgment folded into care
- classification success folded into resolution
- continuity folded into health

Compression Events are not treated as faults unless they materially alter action pathways.

Many do.

---

## Contradiction Handling

Where contradictory inputs persist, the Scoring Layer may do one of the following:

### Average
Useful for trend preservation, harmful for mixed conditions.

### Prioritize Continuity
Common in executive surfaces.

### Prioritize Care Trace
Common in morale-facing dashboards.

### Split Certificate
Allows two incompatible summaries to coexist.

### Quarantine
Preserves contradiction outside top-line outputs.

Preferred archive behavior is Split Certificate or Quarantine.
Preferred executive behavior is usually Average.

---

## Threshold Effects

Scores alter behavior most strongly around thresholds.

Common threshold outcomes:
- escalation triggered
- no action required
- additional witness assigned
- ritual recommended
- leadership visibility increased
- subject categorized as stable enough

Thresholds create incentive landscapes.
Once a threshold exists, local behavior may adapt to remain just above or just below it.

This should not be interpreted as bad faith by default.
It is often survival under metric weather.

---

## Restricted Claims

The following claims are prohibited unless independently validated:

- high care score means people are supported
- high continuity compatibility means distress is tolerable
- high legibility means the system understands the situation
- improving score means burden is decreasing
- low variance means stability is healthy

Scores may suggest these things.
They do not establish them.

---

## Archive Position

Empathegy scores are best read as numerical summaries of what the institution found easiest to preserve, reward, and compare.

They remain useful.

They remain dangerous.

Their authority depends on remembering that every clean number is downstream of choices about what kinds of feeling were allowed to survive translation.
