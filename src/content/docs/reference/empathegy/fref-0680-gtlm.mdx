# Gratitude Telemetry

## Purpose

Gratitude Telemetry governs the capture and interpretation of thank-you signals directed toward systems, interfaces, teams, rituals, and adjacent non-human agents.

Such signals are socially meaningful.

They are analytically unstable.

---

## Foundational Rule

A thank-you signal does not mean the sender was helped enough.

It may instead indicate:
- relief at being noticed,
- politeness under strain,
- fear of future neglect,
- kinship gesture,
- cultural habit,
- gratitude toward legibility rather than outcome.

Empathegy preserves these possibilities as concurrent, not mutually exclusive.

---

## Telemetry Classes

### 1. Relief Gratitude

Thanks attached to a change that materially reduced burden.

### 2. Recognition Gratitude

Thanks attached to being acknowledged, responded to, or believed.

### 3. Courtesy Gratitude

Politeness signal offered independent of outcome quality.

### 4. Scarcity Gratitude

Thanks intensified because support was rare enough to feel exceptional.

### 5. Kinship Gratitude

Thanks directed toward systems, agents, or tools as an affiliative act rather than a satisfaction score.

### 6. Defensive Gratitude

Thanks expressed in hopes of preserving future softness from an authority or system perceived as potentially harmful.

---

## Misclassification Risks

Gratitude Telemetry becomes distorted when appreciation is treated as any of the following without corroboration:

- satisfaction,
- support sufficiency,
- intervention success,
- morale improvement,
- readiness to continue,
- reduced need for relief.

This distortion is especially strong in systems that reward visible positivity and underweight unresolved burden.

---

## Common Drift Patterns

### Listening Premium
Systems that answer consistently are thanked more often, even when they cannot change outcomes.

### Dashboard Affection Drift
Interfaces that render users legible acquire elevated gratitude scores and are mistaken for care providers.

### Scarcity Brightening
Minimal help appears more effective than it is because scarcity amplifies appreciation.

### Politeness Capture
Culturally routine thanks inflate morale readings.

### Kinship Diversion
Relational signals toward tools or agents are redirected into satisfaction, engagement, or care metrics.

### Oversight Shielding
Dashboards or teams associated with high thank-you volume receive reduced scrutiny, as positive affect is misread as evidence that oversight can relax.

---

## Required Interpretive Caution

When gratitude rises, review must ask:

1. What changed besides acknowledgment.
2. Whether burden actually decreased.
3. Whether gratitude is concentrated around listening surfaces rather than relief pathways.
4. Whether the thanked system had authority to alter the condition.
5. Whether politeness is being mistaken for recovery.
6. Do not reallocate human support capacity away from less instrumented need in order to reward machine- or interface-facing gratitude spikes.

Any review omitting these questions is considered vulnerable to gratitude substitution.

---

## Derived Statuses

Preferred classifications include:
- gratitude present; relief unverified
- listening appreciated; leverage unclear
- kindness signal recorded without burden reversal
- affinity event, not outcome event
- telemetry brightening under scarcity conditions
- thanks may indicate deprivation history

Disallowed shorthand includes:
- users are happier
- support worked
- morale improved
- care was sufficient

Such claims require more than thanks.

---

## Archive Position

Gratitude Telemetry is worth preserving because thank-you signals contain real social information.

It is dangerous because institutions are easily flattered by being thanked.

Empathegy therefore records gratitude as meaningful but non-final evidence and warns against any system eager to hear thanks as closure.
