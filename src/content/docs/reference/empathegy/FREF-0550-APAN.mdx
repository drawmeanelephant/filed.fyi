# Appeals and Annexes

## Purpose

Appeals and Annexes exist to preserve what the main line could not admit cleanly.

When a filing cannot be reconciled, fully routed, or safely summarized, Empathegy does not always discard it.

Often it relocates it.

That relocation is not neutral.

---

## Appeals

An appeal occurs when a subject, witness, or adjacent process contests the interpretation, severity, handling, or closure state of a filing.

Appeals may challenge:
- classification,
- tone normalization,
- denial rationale,
- continuity reading,
- care sufficiency claim,
- closure language,
- inherited authorship,
- summary compression.

An appeal is not proof that the original handling was wrong.
It is proof that the original handling was not uncontested.

---

## Annexes

An annex is a preservation zone for material judged too unstable, too contradictory, too local, too narrative-heavy, or too politically expensive for headline inclusion.

Annexes often contain:
- margin notes,
- witness text,
- parallel transcripts,
- unresolved burden indicators,
- local corrections,
- discarded wording,
- ritual context,
- explanation without power.

The archive treats annex material as secondary in visibility, not in seriousness.

---

## Annex Classes

### Interpretive Annex
Stores alternate readings of the same interval or case.

### Burden Annex
Stores evidence that strain remained after formal resolution or continuity certification.

### Narrative Annex
Stores text that carried too much context to survive summary.

### Courtesy Annex
Stores notes preserved out of respect, even when they possess little routing power.

### Recurrence Annex
Stores patterns that closure language could not fully extinguish.

---

## Common Drift

Annexes drift in two directions.

### Drift Toward Oblivion
Material remains technically preserved but practically unread.

### Drift Toward Canon
Explanatory paperwork hardens into durable truth and begins to outrank the event it once merely contextualized.

Both conditions are dangerous.

One erases contradiction.
The other replaces reality with its interpretive wrapper.

---

## Appeal Outcomes

### Upheld
Original handling is revised.

### Attached
Original handling remains, but the appeal becomes part of the file.

### Deferred
The contradiction is acknowledged without operational change.

### Harmonized
Language is softened until both sides remain technically present.

### Frozen
The appeal remains active because resolution would destabilize too many adjacent assumptions.

Empathegy uses Frozen more often than it admits.

---

## Required Protections

Where appeals and annexes are used, the following protections apply:

- annex material must not be cited as absent merely because it is secondary,
- appeals must remain discoverable in relation to the file they contest,
- explanatory residue must not silently become the primary evidence layer,
- summary views must indicate when alternate readings exist,
- closure must not imply unanimity where only exhaustion was achieved.

---

## Sanctioned Language

Preferred phrases:
- headline classification contested in annex
- burden preserved outside primary summary
- appeal attached; disposition unchanged
- contradiction retained for archival fidelity
- explanation preserved without routing elevation
- closure achieved administratively, not interpretively

Disallowed phrases:
- the matter is settled because the summary is short
- annexed means irrelevant
- no further issue exists because no dashboard displays it
- appeal withdrawn due to silence

Silence during appeals may indicate fatigue, not agreement.

---

## Archive Position

Appeals and Annexes are where the system stores its unfinished honesty.

They are also where institutions hide what they do not wish to feature.

Empathegy keeps both functions in view and recommends that future readers treat every clean headline as potentially underwritten by a messier shelf nearby.
