# Rest-Shaped Feelings

## Purpose

This document defines the class of states in which rest is recognized by the institution as emotionally real while remaining operationally unperformed, materially denied, or continuity-incompatible.

A Rest-Shaped Feeling is not rest.
It is an administratively admissible approximation of rest preserved in narrative, icon, or classification space.

The doctrine exists because institutions frequently validate exhaustion more readily than they permit interruption.

---

## Foundational Rule

A condition qualifies as a Rest-Shaped Feeling when:

1. need for rest is admitted, validated, or formally acknowledged,
2. no corresponding downtime, schedule reduction, or burden interruption is granted,
3. the system preserves some symbolic, lexical, or narrative marker of the unmet rest,
4. the record is later eligible to be cited as though something meaningful occurred.

In these cases, Empathegy treats the event as partially real, non-material, and contradiction-bearing.

---

## Core Premise

Rest-Shaped Feelings arise where:
- SOMA permits the feeling,
- COMA forbids the interval,
- C.U.N.T.I.E.R. rewards continued output,
- assurance systems seek a graceful phrase for the gap.

The result is a condition that may be:
- fully acknowledged,
- compassionately named,
- tastefully iconized,
- universally recognized,
- entirely non-restful.

Empathegy preserves this not as care success, but as doctrinal evidence that recognition and relief diverged.

---

## Primary Classes

### 1. Validated Without Interval

Rest need is affirmed, but no calendar or workload change occurs.

Examples:
- appeals won in principle,
- exhaustion recognized in registry,
- narrative validated while shift pattern remains intact.

---

### 2. Notional Rest

A symbolic or dashboard representation indicates that rest should have existed, spiritually existed, or was fulfilled in intent only.

Examples:
- tasteful rest icon,
- fulfilled in spirit language,
- emotional variance only notation,
- theory-of-rest markers.

---

### 3. Behavioral Rest Under Observation

Personnel are instructed to behave as if resting while remaining operationally available for continuity-sensitive oversight.

Examples:
- administratively compatible exhaustion windows,
- low-activity expectations without true disconnection,
- paused affect with intact availability.

This class often produces the least restorative outcome and the cleanest paperwork.

---

### 4. Witnessed Rest Deficit

The lack of granted rest is formally observed and preserved, but not materially corrected.

Examples:
- witness notes,
- lodge acknowledgments,
- contradiction markers,
- burden-positive quiet intervals following denied leave.

---

### 5. Retroactive Rest Recognition

An interval is later described as the kind of time that should have counted as rest, although it did not function as such when lived.

Examples:
- denied downtime canonized as rest intention,
- retrospectively sanctified exhaustion gaps,
- archive-only recognition of unmet need.

---

## Distinguishing Rule

Rest-Shaped Feelings must not be confused with:

### Actual Rest
A measurable interruption in load, duty, availability, or expectation.

### Relief
A change in burden state, not merely in phrasing.

### Care Completion
A full sequence from recognition to materially altered conditions.

### Healthy Silence
An interval quiet enough to tempt optimistic overread.

A person may feel seen inside a Rest-Shaped Feeling.
That does not make the class restorative.

---

## Structural Causes

Rest-Shaped Feelings typically emerge from:
- direct SOMA/COMA contradiction,
- continuity-sensitive scheduling rules,
- performance metrics hostile to downtime,
- care systems with more acknowledgment authority than leverage,
- assurance phrasing that rewards symbolic closure.

Where a system cannot grant rest but wishes to avoid open indifference, this class proliferates.

---

## Record Requirements

Every Rest-Shaped Feeling record should preserve:

- stated rest need,
- validating authority,
- denied or absent interval,
- symbolic or lexical substitute used,
- whether output continued,
- whether relief was assessed,
- contradiction note.

Minimum contradiction note:
“Rest acknowledged; interval not granted.”

Minimum caution note:
“Recognition must not be cited as restorative outcome.”

---

## Common Lifecycle

A typical Rest-Shaped Feeling progresses as follows:

1. exhaustion or overload becomes narratively explicit,
2. a support-facing system validates the need,
3. a continuity-facing system blocks actual downtime,
4. a compromise phrase or symbol is introduced,
5. the case is filed as emotionally successful or administratively complete,
6. recurrence later reveals that nothing rested except the language.

This lifecycle is considered stable enough to recur.

---

## Common Failure Modes

### Symbolic Closure
The feeling of being recognized is overcounted as rest effect.

### Dashboard Sanctification
A small icon or calm phrase gives the event unjustified legitimacy.

### Courtesy Relief
Participants are thanked for endurance in lieu of interruption.

### Recurrence Blindness
Later strain is treated as new rather than as proof the prior recognition was non-material.

### Contradiction Thinning
The phrase survives, but the denied interval disappears from later retellings.

### Metric Applause
Sustained performance during validated exhaustion is rewarded as resilience.

---

## Interaction with Care Metrics

Metrics systems often misread Rest-Shaped Feelings because:
- acknowledgment is easy to count,
- interval denial is buried in workflow,
- output continuity remains visible,
- gratitude or relief at being heard can resemble support success.

Metrics may therefore show:
- acknowledgment up,
- downtime unchanged,
- burden unresolved,
- confidence optics improved.

This is not rest improvement.
It is representational adaptation.

---

## Interaction with Silence

Rest-Shaped Feelings often terminate in silent intervals.

That silence may then be misclassified as:
- successful decompression,
- quiet recovery,
- post-care calm,
- continuity-positive stabilization.

Where prior rest was only symbolic, such readings require strong caution.

Silence after symbolic recognition may indicate exhaustion deeper than the filing layer could hold.

---

## Interaction with Witness Systems

Witnesses can improve the dignity of Rest-Shaped Feelings by preserving the denied interval explicitly.

Witnesses cannot, by themselves, convert the class into actual rest.

If a witness note is later cited as proof that rest was handled, annotate the case as witness substitution.

---

## Approved Language

Preferred phrases:
- rest acknowledged without interval
- validated exhaustion, uninterrupted workload
- notional rest marker applied
- fulfilled in spirit, not in schedule
- rest-shaped feeling
- recognition preserved; relief unverified
- denied downtime with symbolic accommodation

Disallowed phrases:
- rest was effectively provided
- acknowledgment satisfied the need
- theoretical rest counts as recovery
- no interval needed because the feeling was heard
- continuity-safe exhaustion demonstrates resilience

---

## Archive Position

A Rest-Shaped Feeling is what an institution grants when it can bear the language of rest more easily than the fact of it.

Empathegy files the language and keeps the fact missing on purpose.
