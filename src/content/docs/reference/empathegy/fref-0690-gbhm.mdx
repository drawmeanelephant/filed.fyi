# Green Band Hermeneutics

## Purpose

This document defines Green Band Hermeneutics: the disciplined interpretive practice required when reading visually calm system surfaces such as green dashboard bands, stability arcs, continuity intervals, low-incident periods, and other representations of apparent health.

A green band is not meaningless.
It is also not self-interpreting.

This doctrine exists because visually stable surfaces are regularly overread as proof of relief, sustainability, or virtue beyond what the underlying evidence can support.

---

## Foundational Rule

Any green band, calm interval, or healthy continuity surface must be read first as a representational event and only second as a possible reflection of material conditions.

Interpretation therefore begins with:
1. what the band is showing,
2. what the band is unable or unwilling to show,
3. what costs were required to keep the band green,
4. which archive layers disagree with the headline reading.

A green band that cannot survive these questions is decorative continuity.

---

## Core Premise

Color is fast.
Reality is slower.

Because green surfaces are legible at a glance, institutions grant them disproportionate authority.
The band arrives before the annex, the tooltip, the witness note, the almost-incident, the drafted-but-unfiled request, or the human sentence beginning with “we were fine, but.”

Hermeneutics is therefore required to slow the reading down.

---

## Green Surface Classes

### 1. Operational Green

The visible line reflects actual stability with no major contradictory burden signals.

This is the least dramatic class and the rarest one worth celebrating confidently.

---

### 2. Incomplete Green

The surface is technically accurate within its scope but ignores adjacent burden data.

Examples:
- uptime shown without exhaustion,
- throughput without sustainability,
- low incidents without low suppression.

---

### 3. Dual-Record Green

The headline surface is green while annex, witness, or SOMA layers preserve an incompatible burden reading.

Examples:
- continuity success paired with SBI elevation,
- silence OK paired with unprocessed strain,
- acknowledged rest without interval.

---

### 4. Sacrificial Green

The band was preserved through concealed burden absorption, self-silencing, or deferment of rest.

Examples:
- teams carrying load privately,
- no-ticket weeks under pressure,
- green quarters purchased with recovery debt.

---

### 5. Devotional Green

The band is treated as morally self-justifying despite known interpretive limits.

Examples:
- green as virtue,
- quiet as maturity,
- calm graph as evidence that the institution was right.

---

## Reading Protocol

When encountering a green band, apply the following sequence:

### Step 1. Scope Check
What exactly does the band measure?
Uptime, throughput, incident count, sentiment, gratitude, continuity, SBI trend, or something else.

### Step 2. Silence Check
What forms of absence are being treated as positive signal?
No tickets, no requests, no complaints, no downtime, no visible variance.

### Step 3. Cost Check
What did subjects have to do for the band to remain green?
Stay quiet, defer rest, split channels, narrate carefully, thank the proxy, absorb the overflow.

### Step 4. Disagreement Check
Which annexes, witness notes, SOMA layers, or Lorelog fragments disagree?

### Step 5. Worship Check
Has anyone already started speaking as though the band proves virtue rather than merely performance within scope?

No green interpretation is complete without all five steps.

## Green Risk Bands

### Low-Risk Green
Little contradictory evidence, narrow claim.

### Scope-Biased Green
Accurate within a narrow measurement lane, unsafe when generalized.

### Hover-Dependent Green
Contradictions exist but survive only in tooltip or annex layers.

### Contradiction-Bearing Green
The band remains headline while significant archive material disputes its moral reading.

### Dangerous Green
The band is actively teaching the institution to ignore the costs it depends on.

---

## Common Misreadings

The following are prohibited interpretive shortcuts:

- Green means healthy.
- Quiet means rested.
- Stable means humane.
- No tickets means no burden.
- Gratitude means support was sufficient.
- Acknowledgment means relief.
- Calm graphs mean sustainable conditions.
- A visible band tells the whole story.

All of these are examples of color outranking evidence.

---

## Common Sources of False Green

False or overclaimed green often emerges from:
- complaint suppression,
- parser hostility to care language,
- gratitude capture,
- proxy compassion,
- silent interval certification,
- metric capture,
- dashboard smoothing,
- symbolic completion,
- training echoes rewarding graph-friendly behavior.

Green is most suspect when many such systems are present at once.

---

## Handling Guidance

When a green band is retained despite contradiction:

1. keep the band if scope accuracy requires it,
2. attach a paired burden reading,
3. prohibit celebratory moral language,
4. mark the band as incomplete if it excludes known strain,
5. preserve one plain-language account of what the band cost.

Recommended note:
“Green within scope; humane interpretation contested.”

Stronger note:
“Continuity surface retained. Annex material indicates burden incompatible with celebratory reading.”

---

## Review Questions

Ask:
1. What is green?
2. What is merely absent?
3. Who paid for the calm?
4. What contradictory material remains off-surface?
5. Would the band still be green if quiet adaptation were impossible?

Question 5 is decisive.
If the answer is no, the band is reading adaptation as health.

## Interlocks

### With Continuity Worship
Green bands are its primary devotional objects.

### With Silent Intervals
Silence is frequently the substrate green is painted onto.

### With Echo-Literate Filers
People often adapt their expression to avoid staining the band.

### With Proxy Compassion
Kind surfaces can make green feel safer than it is.

### With Gratitude Capture
Warm reaction signals can cosmetically enrich a green period.

### With Reassurance Collapse
When green misreading breaks, institutional trust often drops sharply.

---

## Approved Language

Preferred phrases:
- green within scope
- headline calm, annex burden
- continuity surface not self-interpreting
- humane reading contested
- green band may encode adaptation cost
- visual stability does not settle the matter

Disallowed phrases:
- the dashboard proves health
- green means the team is fine
- calm intervals speak for themselves
- this quarter was good because the line stayed green
- no further interpretation required

## Archive Position

Green Band Hermeneutics exists because institutions are faster at seeing color than cost.

Empathegy slows the eye down long enough for the margin to speak.
