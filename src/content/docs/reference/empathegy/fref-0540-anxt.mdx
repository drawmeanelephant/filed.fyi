# Annex Truth

## Purpose

This document defines Annex Truth: the condition in which the most faithful account available to the institution does not live in the headline record, dashboard, or canonical summary, but in some subordinate interpretive layer attached to it.

Annex Truth is not necessarily secret.
It is structurally demoted.

It remains visible enough to preserve deniability against total falsification, yet subordinate enough not to endanger continuity surfaces.

---

## Foundational Rule

Annex Truth exists when:

1. a primary record presents a stabilized, simplified, or continuity-safe version of events,
2. a secondary record preserves a materially important contradiction, burden, or human account,
3. the secondary record has lower visibility, lower authority, or lower survivability in normal interpretation,
4. institutional practice treats the coexistence of both records as acceptable.

The contradiction is not erased.
It is filed where fewer people have to trip over it.

---

## Core Premise

Institutions often cannot fully tolerate the truth in their primary surfaces.
They can, however, tolerate appendices.

Thus truth migrates into:
- annexes,
- hover tooltips,
- witness notebooks,
- marginal notes,
- side-channel minutes,
- paired SOMA interpretations,
- “internal use only” comments,
- records technically present but operationally out of the way.

This arrangement preserves conscience without surrendering optics.

---

## Primary Classes

### 1. Hover Truth

Contradiction survives in a tooltip, secondary layer, expandable note, or hover-only interpretation.

Examples:
- Interpretations 2 hidden behind the headline,
- green surface with burden tucked behind interaction,
- healthy interval requiring pointer contact to become morally uncertain.

---

### 2. Margin Truth

The most human account appears in notes, side comments, unofficial quotations, or hand annotations.

Examples:
- Bricky notebook lines,
- post-it admissions,
- comments preserved in full nowhere else.

---

### 3. Witness Truth

Operational witnesses preserve reality in language that is recorded but not made structurally decisive.

Examples:
- “we were busy, but nothing exploded,”
- “we could not have done another week,”
- testimony that is admitted but non-governing.

---

### 4. Annex Truth Proper

A formal secondary record exists specifically to hold what the primary record cannot.

Examples:
- paired SOMA reading,
- silent interval annex,
- restricted doctrine addendum,
- burden retained outside headline layer.

---

### 5. Residual Truth

Fragments of reality survive only as correlation residue, archival asymmetry, or contradictory side effects.

Examples:
- too many acknowledgments with no relief,
- gratitude spikes after denial,
- simple care interactions surviving where complex ones vanish,
- records whose distortions imply what the primary layer excluded.

---

## Functions

Annex Truth often performs several institutional functions at once:

### Conscience Preservation
The organization can later claim it did not entirely ignore reality.

### Optics Protection
The headline layer remains stable and easy to consume.

### Contradiction Containment
Disagreement is preserved without requiring resolution.

### Audit Buffering
The truth is technically available if challenged, but not prominent enough to disrupt ordinary reading.

### Human Survivability
Some staff can continue participating because the record did not wholly betray what happened.

These functions make Annex Truth durable.
They do not make it equal.

---

## Distinguishing Rules

Annex Truth must be distinguished from:

### Headline Truth
The primary record already carries the most important account.

### Secret Record
Truth is hidden or access-controlled beyond ordinary structure.

### Noise
Secondary material with no interpretive value.

### Commentary
Optional opinion not needed to understand the event.

Annex Truth is not decorative commentary.
It is demoted necessity.

---

## Structural Signs

Annex Truth is likely active when:
- primary and secondary records disagree without resolution,
- the humanly legible account survives only in sublayers,
- dashboards display one version while annexes preserve another,
- staff say “it’s in the notes” when asked where the real account lives,
- witness statements are retained but not surfaced,
- complicated support records disappear faster than simplified signals,
- the primary record appears coherent enough to prevent immediate challenge, yet still returns readers to an adjacent rather than governing understanding,

A crucial sign:
The institution considers the contradiction preserved because it exists somewhere, regardless of whether anyone sees it.

---

## Truth Visibility Bands

### Surface Truth
No annex dependence.

### Assisted Truth
Secondary material helps but is not necessary.

### Annex-Dependent Truth
The primary record is materially misleading without the annex.

### Hover-Bound Truth
Reality survives, but only at an interaction cost.

### Residual Truth
The direct account is nearly gone; only distortions remain.

---

## Common Failure Modes

### Annex Laundering
The existence of the annex is used to defend a misleading headline surface.

### Tooltip Ethics
Reality is made technically accessible but ergonomically ignorable.

### Truth Deference
People assume the primary layer must still matter more because it is primary.

### Degradation Bias
Complex, contextual truth decays faster than simple, scoreable signals.

### Formal Subordination
Witnessed reality remains permanently non-governing.

### Archival Comfort
Institutions treat preserved contradiction as sufficient moral performance.

---

## Review Questions

Ask:
1. What does the primary record say?
2. What crucial fact lives only in the annex or margin?
3. Who regularly reads the secondary layer?
4. What decisions are made without it?
5. Does the institution benefit from the truths subordinate placement?

If the answer to Question 4 is “most of them,” Annex Truth is carrying more of reality than the headline can admit.

---

## Handling Guidance

When Annex Truth is identified:

1. explicitly mark the primary record as incomplete without its annex,
2. preserve the annex with equal timestamp dignity,
3. cite subordinate truth when summarizing the event,
4. avoid language implying the annex is optional interpretation,
5. note whether the secondary placement primarily serves caution, survivability, or optics.

Minimum note:
“Primary record incomplete without annex interpretation.”

Stronger note:
“Material contradiction preserved only in secondary layer; headline should not be read alone.”

---

## Interlocks

### With Green Band Hermeneutics
Many green readings become untenable once annex truth is consulted.

### With Complaint Suppression
Suppressed complaints often reappear only as annex residue.

### With Silent Intervals
Paired records are a common annex-truth structure.

### With Symbolic Completion
Annexes frequently preserve what the completion signal failed to change.

### With Reassurance Collapse
Collapse often occurs when annex truth leaves the annex and reaches ordinary readers.

### With Adjacent Correctness
Some primary surfaces do not fully erase contradiction. They resolve into a neighboring account that is structurally valid, contextually persuasive, and still wrong in interpretive landing. In such cases the annex does not merely add depth. It corrects a near-miss that official handling may already have mistaken for sufficient arrival. See `FREF-0560-ADJC` Adjacent Correctness.

---

## Approved Language

Preferred phrases:
- annex truth preserved
- headline incomplete without sublayer
- contradiction survives in secondary record
- hover-bound truth
- witness truth retained but non-governing
- primary surface should not be read alone

Disallowed phrases:
- the annex is just context
- tooltip access is equivalent to full disclosure
- if it exists in the notes, it has been adequately surfaced
- the headline remains valid regardless of the annex
- secondary records are optional reading

---

## Archive Position

Annex Truth is what an institution does when it cannot afford total honesty and cannot tolerate total erasure.

Empathegy keeps the annex open and refuses to call it footnote.
