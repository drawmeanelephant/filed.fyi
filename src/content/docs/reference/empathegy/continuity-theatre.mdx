# Continuity Theatre Register

## Definition

Continuity Theatre is the condition in which the organization appears calmer, healthier, or more cared for in administrative representation than in operational or lived reality.

It is not fraud by default.
It is often the cumulative result of smoothing, substitution, dashboard selection, ritual acknowledgment, and disciplined under-reporting.

---

## Theatre Indicators

A continuity interval may be classified as theatre-prone when one or more of the following are present:

- rising care coverage without corresponding relief pathways
- silence interpreted as success without paired burden view
- gratitude increase treated as stabilization
- contradiction preserved only in hover states or annexes
- trend coherence improving while reported content worsens
- ritual participation substituting for discretionary support
- reductions in visible variance following stricter admissibility thresholds

---

## Theatre Classes

### Minor Theatre
Presentation slightly overstates health, but local actors still retain meaningful corrective visibility.

### Managed Theatre
The gap between appearance and experience is recognized internally and stabilized rather than corrected.

### Load-Bearing Theatre
The representation gap has become necessary for leadership confidence, operational pacing, or audit comfort.

### Devotional Theatre
Continuity indicators are treated as moral proof of system goodness despite unresolved structural debt.

---

## Common Mechanisms

### Coverage Substitution
Institution counts acknowledgment artifacts as care outcomes.

### Silence Laundering
Absence of filing is converted into evidence of resilience or sufficient calm.

### Gratitude Inflation
Politeness toward systems is interpreted as proof that systems are helping enough.

### Coherence Privilege
Smooth emotional curves outrank disruptive but truthful representations.

### Ritual Saturation
Meetings, ceremonies, lodges, circles, and witnesses produce recognition without leverage.

### Lexical Softening
Administrative language lowers perceived severity while preserving constraint.

---

## Required Language

When theatre conditions are suspected, sanctioned phrasing includes:

- continuity-sufficient, burden-positive
- representationally stable, operationally unresolved
- coverage achieved without confirmed relief
- acknowledgment present; mitigation unverified
- burden shadow retained outside headline layer
- continuity view should not be read as completion

Disallowed phrasing includes:

- things appear healthy, therefore they are healthy
- no tickets means no problem
- gratitude confirms effectiveness
- stable output confirms sustainable conditions

---

## Handling Protocol

1. Preserve the continuity classification if operational doctrine requires it.
2. Attach a shadow interpretation where burden signals remain.
3. Prevent summary compression into a single health number.
4. Mark the interval as theatre-sensitive if executive consumption is likely.
5. Preserve at least one local or narrative account of what continuity cost.

---

## Register Note

The organization may continue to rely on continuity theatre where necessary.

The Registers purpose is not to stop that reliance.
It is to prevent the archive from forgetting that reliance was there.
