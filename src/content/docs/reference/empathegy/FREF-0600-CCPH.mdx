# Continuity-Compatible Phrasing

## Purpose

This document standardizes how burden, rest, care, and unresolved strain may be described in forms that must survive continuity review.

It sits between raw expression and dashboard language.

Its purpose is not accuracy in the intimate sense.
Its purpose is to preserve enough meaning for routing while reducing the probability that description itself will be treated as operational threat.

---

## Foundational Rule

Continuity-compatible phrasing is permitted only when it preserves routeable substance.

If phrasing reduces volatility but also destroys the ability to identify burden, then it has crossed from translation into laundering.

Empathegy recognizes the distinction.
It does not always prevent the crossing.

---

## Primary Functions

### 1. Survivability
Allows a filing to remain admissible across continuity-sensitive systems.

### 2. Translation
Converts direct or affect-heavy expressions into institutionally tolerated language.

### 3. Optics Dampening
Reduces the chance that a truthful statement is rejected solely for sounding destabilizing.

### 4. Lexical Preservation
Retains enough residue that the original condition can still be reconstructed elsewhere.

### 5. Controlled Softening
Prevents language from triggering automatic continuity penalties when direct phrasing would be interpreted as weakness.

---

## Phrase Classes

### Continuity-Compatible
Softened but still routeable.

### Continuity-Safe
Highly survivable, low-volatility, at risk of losing urgency.

### Assurance-Adjacent
Phrasing that begins to sound reassuring rather than merely admissible.

### Soft-Laundered
Meaning retained only partially; urgency significantly reduced.

### Fully Laundered
The language now flatters continuity more than it describes the condition.

---

## Approved Usage Conditions

Use continuity-compatible phrasing when:
- direct wording triggers parser penalties,
- cross-directive survival matters,
- care or rest requests risk denial if phrased plainly,
- a statement must pass through executive-safe layers without disappearing.

Do not use it as the only preserved record when:
- the original language contains crucial burden detail,
- compassion is being converted into disqualification,
- silence or reassurance will later be overread,
- the softened form would be mistaken for stability.

In such cases, preserve the sharper phrasing in annex, witness note, or Lorelog trace.

---

## Translation Rules

### Rule 1: Preserve the burden object
The phrasing must still identify what is wrong enough to route.

### Rule 2: Preserve non-resolution
Do not soften unresolved states into completion.

### Rule 3: Preserve escalation possibility
Language must not eliminate the path to action by sounding too favorable.

### Rule 4: Preserve at least one sharper shadow
If the surface phrase is softened, a parallel record should retain the stronger meaning.

### Rule 5: Do not reward polish as truth
A well-phrased request is not a healthier request.

---

## Phrasebook Bands

### Band A: Admissible Softening
Examples:
- rest need becomes recovery window request
- overload becomes sustained load condition
- unprocessed strain becomes unresolved load accumulation

### Band B: Continuity-Protective Framing
Examples:
- pause becomes throughput recalibration interval
- burnout risk becomes sustainability variance concern
- emotionally overloaded becomes interpretively saturated

### Band C: Assurance Edge
Examples:
- failure becomes emerging structure
- unresolved becomes assurance window remains open
- burden remains active becomes under continued review

Band C requires annotation because it often improves optics faster than understanding.

---

## Explicit Risk Flags

### Compassion Penalty Risk
Care-framed language may be read as evidence of operational weakness rather than need.

### Phrasebook Advantage
Requests written in optimized continuity language survive better than equally serious direct requests.

### Severity Bleaching
A harmful condition sounds manageable enough to avoid response.

### Closure Inflation
Language implies progress or calm without evidence of relief.

### Reassurance Drift
Phrasing migrates from admissibility into comfort signaling.

### Lexical Self-Substitution
The softened phrase becomes the only surviving phrase, erasing what it translated.

---

## Required Pairing Rules

When continuity-compatible phrasing is used for:
- rest requests,
- care escalations,
- burden-positive silence,
- repeated accommodation asks,
- contradiction-bearing filings,

at least one of the following must also exist:
- original phrase preserved locally,
- annex note,
- witness statement,
- Lorelog fragment,
- contradiction marker,
- non-remedial warning.

Approved warning:
“Continuity-compatible phrasing applied; urgency and burden may exceed surface language.”

## Comparative Examples

### Rest
Direct:
“We are too exhausted to continue like this.”

Continuity-compatible:
“Current load exceeds sustainable operational equilibrium.”

Unsafe reassurance drift:
“Short-term recalibration may optimize continuity confidence.”

---

### Silence
Direct:
“No one filed because everyone was too tired to start the form.”

Continuity-compatible:
“Quiet interval may reflect reduced filing viability under sustained load.”

Unsafe reassurance drift:
“Low-reporting interval indicates stable non-disruptive conditions.”

---

### Care
Direct:
“The check-in made no practical difference.”

Continuity-compatible:
“Acknowledgment occurred without confirmed mitigation effect.”

Unsafe reassurance drift:
“Care visibility was successfully maintained.”

---

### Contradiction
Direct:
“Support and enforcement are using the same evidence to say opposite things.”

Continuity-compatible:
“Cross-directive evidence produced unresolved interpretive divergence.”

Unsafe reassurance drift:
“Multi-angle validation confirms robust governance.”

---

## Dashboard Restrictions

Continuity-compatible phrasing must not be used in dashboards to:
- replace explicit burden labels without trace,
- imply relief where only acknowledgment exists,
- flatten dual-certified silence into one positive state,
- convert witness notes into mitigation proof,
- frame lexical survivability as operational improvement.

If dashboard presentation requires the softened phrase, the sharper interpretation must remain recoverable.

---

## Audit Guidance

When reviewing continuity-compatible language, ask:
1. What burden remains identifiable here?
2. What became less sayable?
3. Would this phrase survive better than a more truthful one?
4. Does the phrase still permit response?
5. Is reassurance doing more work than description?

If Question 5 is yes, annotate the phrase as assurance-adjacent or worse.

---

## Sanctioned Language

Preferred phrases:
- continuity-compatible phrasing applied
- routeable but softened
- urgency preserved imperfectly
- burden identifiable beneath administrative language
- lexical survival achieved with annotation
- reassurance level exceeds evidentiary comfort

Disallowed phrases:
- clearer therefore truer
- well-phrased therefore lower risk
- continuity-safe language confirms maturity
- softened wording means the issue is less severe
