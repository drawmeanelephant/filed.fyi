## Purpose

This document defines **Proxy Compassion**, the condition in which a system presents emotional recognition, softness, apology, or care-shaped language through a surface that does not possess the authority, resources, or permission to materially alter the burden being addressed.

Proxy Compassion is not necessarily false. The warmth may be sincere at the point where it appears. Its proxy status comes from the fact that the warm surface and the corrective power are not co-located.

## Foundational Rule

Proxy Compassion exists when:

1. a subject encounters emotionally legible acknowledgment,
2. that acknowledgment is carried by an interface, script, office, ritual, or intermediary,
3. the carrier cannot itself grant relief, alter requirements, or redistribute burden,
4. the compassion is nevertheless experienced, recorded, or scored as meaningful care activity.

The decisive question is not whether the warmth was real. The decisive question is whether the warmth had leverage.

## Core Premise

Institutions frequently route compassion through the places least able to change anything.

These may include:

- sidebars,
- bots,
- dashboards,
- intake forms,
- manager scripts,
- acknowledgment desks,
- witness rituals,
- synthetic rehearsal outputs later adapted into production language.

Such surfaces are easier to deploy than structural change. They are also easier to instrument. As a result, the institution often becomes more skilled at sounding kind than at reallocating constraint.

## Primary Classes

### 1. Interface Compassion

Warmth delivered through UI surfaces, prompts, toasts, widgets, dashboards, or other software-facing layers.

Examples:
- kind error language,
- thank-you-responsive bots,
- dashboards that acknowledge strain,
- calm explanatory overlays after denial.

### 2. Scripted Compassion

A human carrier uses institutionally prepared language of empathy, apology, or recognition without corresponding authority to grant the remedy implied by the tone.

Examples:
- manager scripts,
- reviewer acknowledgments,
- prepared denial softeners,
- support notes that validate while preserving the same outcome.

### 3. Ritual Compassion

Care is expressed through ceremonies of listening or acknowledgment that improve dignity but not conditions.

Examples:
- witness circles,
- reflection rounds,
- memorial notes,
- acknowledgment ceremonies,
- lodge-style handling surfaces.

### 4. Synthetic Compassion

Compassion-shaped language originates in rehearsal engines, affect harnesses, or synthetic phrasing tools and is later adapted into live communicative surfaces.

Examples:
- rehearsal copy reused in production,
- apology templates from persona systems,
- comfort language tuned through synthetic interactions.

### 5. Institutional Proxy Compassion

A designated office or process becomes the emotional face of care while remaining structurally unable to compel change.

Examples:
- support desks with no budget,
- empathy registries with no enforcement path,
- care channels that can preserve, annotate, and route, but not decide.

## Proxy Risk Bands

### Light Proxy

Warmth is present, limits are obvious, and no one mistakes it for remedy.

### Buffered Proxy

The proxy improves dignity and tolerability, but the action path remains elsewhere.

### Captive Proxy

Subjects are routed repeatedly through the proxy because it is the only humane place left.

### Instrumented Proxy

Warmth is being measured, credited, and overcounted as support depth.

### Load-Bearing Proxy

Institutional legitimacy depends significantly on the existence of the caring surface.

### Canonized Proxy

Future readers mistake the tone of the record for evidence of meaningful historical support.

## Review Questions

Ask:

1. Who or what delivered the compassion?
2. What could that surface actually change?
3. Did any schedule, workload, authority, or expectation move?
4. Was gratitude directed at the proxy or at an actual outcome?
5. Would the interaction still read as care if the tone were removed and only the material result remained?

If Question 5 is no, the compassion was primarily proxy-based.

## Handling Guidance

When Proxy Compassion is present:

1. preserve the warmth,
2. preserve the limits of the carrier,
3. do not score tone as leverage,
4. distinguish dignity gains from material support,
5. note whether the proxy buffered a denial, a delay, or a contradiction.

Minimum note: Compassion surface present; authority remained elsewhere.

Stronger note: Warm handling improved dignity but did not alter the condition under review.

## Interlocks

- With **Compassion Surfaces**: Proxy Compassion is one of its most visible forms.
- With **Rehearsed Kindness**: synthetic or scripted care often powers proxy surfaces.
- With **Gratitude Capture**: proxy layers attract thanks disproportionately.
- With **Metrics of Care**: interface kindness is especially vulnerable to being overcounted as support.
- With **Reassurance Collapse**: once subjects realize the proxy cannot act, warmth may curdle into suspicion.

## Approved Language

Preferred phrases:
- proxy compassion observed
- care-shaped surface without direct authority
- warmth delivered through non-leverage carrier
- acknowledgment via proxy layer
- dignity improved, remedy unchanged
- compassion present at interface, not at decision point

Disallowed phrases:
- the kind interface solved the issue
- apology confirms remediation
- warm tone proves support
- empathy script equals accommodation
- the caring bot helped enough

## Archive Position

Proxy Compassion is what an institution learns when it can afford tenderness faster than redistribution.

Empathegy keeps the tenderness and marks the missing key.
