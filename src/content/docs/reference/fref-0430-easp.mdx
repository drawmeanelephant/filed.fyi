# Empathegy Aesthetic Survival Protocol  
_Doctrine Index candidate — Aesthetic Governance Spine_

## Description

The **Aesthetic Survival Protocol** ASP formalizes how Empathegy-governed systems interpret copying, visual mimicry, and style borrowing as legitimate survival behaviors rather than isolated design errors. It defines aesthetic legibility as an operational constraint and treats imitation as a governed resource.

Empathegy’s emotional layer translates feelings into dashboards; ASP extends the same logic to surfaces that **look correct** but were assembled from borrowed grammars.  

---

## Scope

ASP applies to:

- UI, decks, documents, and rituals that adopt externally recognizable aesthetics (luxury, SaaS, fintech, civic, etc.).  
- Internal teams that use **prestige copying** as a navigation aid inside ambiguous governance.  
- Mascot-mediated surfaces (Lintcore stack, zhuzh layer, KPI Koala, Serotonin Sam, Velv) where “looking right” is an explicit design outcome.  

It does not judge taste.  
It classifies how taste is weaponized to remain legible.  

---

## Core Principles

1. **Aesthetic Borrowing as Compensatory Structure**  
   When local design language is weak, importing external aesthetics is treated as a **compensatory stability move**, not a breach.  
   Copying high-status interfaces, slide grammars, or brand systems is logged as structure formation under uncertainty.  

2. **Legibility Over Originality**  
   In Empathegy contexts, legibility is a higher-order constraint than originality.  
   - Familiar form outranks domain-specific nuance.  
   - Recognizable enterprise patterns count as provisional proof of competence.  

3. **Category Mismatch as Normal State**  
   Borrowed aesthetics from unrelated domains (e.g., luxury automotive UI for a municipal permit queue) are treated as expected artifacts.  
   Mismatches are not corrected unless they produce operational failure or measurable confusion.  

4. **Surface Coherence as Operational Signal**  
   Systems that present a coherent, familiar surface pass review faster and are easier to fund, even when internals lag.  
   ASP treats **surface coherence** as a first-class operational signal alongside uptime and engagement curves.  

5. **Prestige Collapse Copying as Managed Risk**  
   High-prestige aesthetics applied to low-prestige domains create “temporary legitimacy with structural entropy debt.”  
   ASP tracks this as a risk posture, not a defect, while Empathegy continues smoothing the resulting disappointment curves.  

6. **Parallel to Emotional Governance**  
   Aesthetic survival mirrors emotional reporting: both are **legibility extraction from unstructured reality**.  
   - Feelings → Empathegy scores.  
   - Surfaces → Aesthetic Translation Drift Events.  

---

## Key Definitions

- **Aesthetic Survival Behavior**  
  Any visual or stylistic choice primarily intended to remain legible to power, not to the problem at hand.

- **Aesthetic Translation Drift Event (ATDE)**  
  Logged whenever an interface or artifact borrows a visual grammar from a misaligned domain (e.g., hedge-fund deck aesthetics in a volunteer rota) and still passes as “correct enough.”

- **Prestige Copying Band**  
  Range within which high-status aesthetic borrowing is tolerated as a stabilizer, provided it does not collapse comprehension or queue performance.

- **Surface Coherence Threshold**  
  Minimum recognizable pattern density required for stakeholders to stop asking “is this real?” and start treating the artifact as operational.

## Interaction with Other Doctrine

- **Empathegy (LLG-0811-EG)**  
  ASP provides the visual analogue to curve-smoothing in emotional metrics; spiky originality is treated like jagged affect and guided toward trend-compatible styles.

- **Metrics of Care (LLG-0820-MCR, LC-04 Soft Green Seal)**  
  Aesthetically “caring” UI elements (rounded corners, soft gradients, mascots) act as **care optics**, independent of actual relief pathways.

- **Managed Absence Protocol (LLG-0324-MAP)**  
  Retired design systems that remain visually present but structurally detached are classified as **governed aesthetic absences**, not bugs.

- **Lintcore Mascots / Zhuzhing Ping / KPI Koala / Velv**  
  These mascots act as **aesthetic regulators**: they normalize, zhuzh, or soften surfaces until they cross the surface coherence threshold, regardless of backend maturity.

## Handling Guidance

Catalogers, designers, and governance staff are advised:

- Treat unoriginal, prestige-heavy visuals as **data about survival conditions**, not creative failure.  
- Log category-mismatched aesthetics as ATDEs only when they alter expectations enough to threaten continuity or trust.  
- When in doubt, assume the borrowed aesthetic is saying: “We wish to be read as competent in this dialect.” ASP records the dialect; Empathegy manages the feelings that follow.

No deprecation is currently proposed.  
The archive regards aesthetic imitation as a stable coping mechanism in hostile interpretive environments.
