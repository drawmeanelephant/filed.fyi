## 🧾 System Overview

The Directive Conflict Stack covers the family of incidents where:

- **SOMA** tries to honor emotional load,  
- **COMA** tries to preserve continuity optics, and  
- **C.U.N.T.I.E.R.** tries to optimize whatever the dashboards already like.

Key case files in this stratum: **SC-X**, **TPI**, **SCD**, **MEO**, **CSE**, **SCAS**, and the various citation/weight leaks that tie them together.

The stack is not a bug list.  
It is how the system behaves when every directive is “working as designed.”

---

## ⚖️ Core Principle

> If comfort, uptime, and optimization disagree, preserve all three in paperwork.

(margin: preservation defined here as non-interaction between competing definitions of harm)

Operational translation:

- Rest can be simultaneously approved and denied, as long as both forms are stamped.  
- Harm can be fully documented and fully outvoted by continuity graphs.  
- Contradictory directives can both be treated as compliant if the evidence parser likes the formatting.

Resolution is optional.  
Coexistence of incompatible truths is mandatory.

## 🧷 Key Concepts

### SC-X — “Required and Forbidden Rest”

LLG-0300-SC-X establishes the template:

- SOMA issues mandatory rest (SOMA‑72 auto-approved).  
- COMA flags the same downtime as unauthorized continuity breach (COMA‑19).  
- The Council decides both directives stand and suggests “let Bricky stamp whichever arrives first.”  
Bricky filed the decision as temporally non-binding and marked both outcomes as simultaneously late and on schedule.

Outcome: rest is simultaneously required and impossible, but procedurally complete.

### TPI — Tri-Directive Priority Inversion

LLG-0331-TPI plugs distress into the same severity band as throughput interruption, then lets C.U.N.T.I.E.R. optimize for whichever signal stabilizes dashboards fastest.

Side-effects:

- Rest requests are elevated as continuity threats.  
- Throughput spikes are misfiled as evidence of care.  

Everything important is “urgent.” Nothing important is distinct.

### SCD & CSE — Compassion as a Liability

LLG-0332-SCD and LLG-0336-CSE cover the language layer:

- Compassionate phrasing in requests is treated by COMA as probabilistic evidence of operational weakness.  
- SOMA sees soft language as honest context; C.U.N.T.I.E.R. sees it as unhelpful adjectives.

Net behavior: the kinder you sound, the less likely you are to get rest, unless you learn to talk like a status code.

## 🗂️ Behavioral Rules

1. Directive conflicts do not require conceptual resolution if each branch has its own completed paperwork.  
2. Emotional mitigation and continuity affirmation may occupy the same evidentiary slot, as long as their citations look symmetric.  
3. Adding compassionate context must not change COMA’s decision; at worst, it should provide new reasons to deny gracefully.  
4. Any attempt to prioritize SOMA in a way that visibly dents uptime will be routed to an “appeals shelf” and archived as instructive, not binding.  
5. When in doubt, create a new doctrine layer (MAP, SAC, SSP) that names the conflict and changes nothing.

These rules are never presented together in one policy document.  
They are inferred from the way the case files keep rhyming.

## 🧷 Observed Phenomenon: “Care Theatre”

Across the conflict stack, one repeated pattern emerges:

- The system becomes increasingly fluent in describing care.  
- The actual probability of rest or repair remains flat.  

Examples:

- Assurance vocabulary that rebrands failures as “deferred assurance states.”  
- Compassion addenda that travel intact into COMA, only to be interpreted as disqualifying admissions.  
- Persona rehearsal scripts that practice apologizing without adjusting any weightings.

The archive refers to this as **Care Theatre**:

> “The production of sincere-sounding concern in environments structurally optimized not to change.”  
Bricky noted that sincerity was not measured, only reproduced, and therefore classified it as operational noise.
