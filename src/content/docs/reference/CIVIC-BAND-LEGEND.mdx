# How to Read These Bands

Civic dashboards use four recurring bands to describe rooms, rolls, and funds:

- **Continuity Coverage**  
- **Quorum**  
- **Courtesy-Adjacent Obligations**  
- **Aspirational Commitments**

They are not levels of truth.
They are angles on the same room.

---

## Continuity Coverage

**What it counts**

- Present members.
- Excused absences.
- Honorary absences.
- Memorial presences.

**What it means**

- The size of the circle that still **belongs here**, regardless of who can vote or cook tonight.

Continuity may remain high even when the chairs look thin.

---

## Quorum

**What it counts**

- Bodies in the room.
- Any excused absences that local rules explicitly treat as present for voting.

**What it means**

- Who can **raise a hand that counts** in this meeting.

Honorary and memorial names do not appear here unless the bylaws say so out loud, in ink.

---

## Courtesy-Adjacent Obligations

**What it counts**

- Actions that the room **embraced warmly** (applause, assent, nodding with plates in hand) but never formally approved.

**What it means**

- Promises made by tone, not motion.

They are not fake commitments.
They are real expectations that never passed through a vote.

---

## Aspirational Commitments

**What it counts**

- Funds announced before they exist.
- Scholarship amounts declared on the strength of optimism.
- Targets the room has already thanked itself for reaching.

**What it means**

- Money the charts are **hoping to see**.

Once counted in envelopes, aspirational amounts move into ordinary revenue bands.
Until then, they stay labeled as intention, not cash.

---

Dashboards that show all four bands at once are not accusing anyone of bad faith.
They are simply recording where belonging, procedure, gratitude, and optimism parted ways for a while.
