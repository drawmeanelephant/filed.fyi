> This exercise has no correct answers.  
> It has only answers that admit where they came from.

## Instructions

Select a trio of artifacts that refer, loosely, to the same kind of situation:

- one softened finding (assurance),
- one reinterpreted scan (SRB),
- one aligned dashboard screenshot (DAC).

Read them in any order.

---

### Part 1 – What They Say

1. In the finding, underline phrases that suggest everything is under control.
2. In the scan, circle any term that makes absence sound like intention.
3. In the dashboard, mark where your attention goes first.

Write two sentences:

- “According to the finding, the situation is …”
- “According to the dashboard, the situation is …”

Do not reconcile them yet.

---

### Part 2 – What They Do Not Say

Without re-reading:

1. List three things you assume must be true for all three artifacts to be comfortable existing together.
2. Now check whether any of them are actually stated.

If an assumption appears nowhere, draw a small square next to it.
Treat the square as a missing field.

---

### Part 3 – Where You Live

On a separate line, complete:

> “If I filed my day into this trio, the part that would feel least like my life is …”

This sentence is not collected.
You may keep it, discard it, or file it in your own drawer.

---

### Part 4 – Optional Vault Referral

If, after this exercise, the trio feels:

- too neat,
- too aligned, and
- too free of discomfort,

you may mark it with:

> “Candidate over‑coherent – reads smoother than I trust.”

and forward a copy to the Over‑Coherence Vault.

The Annex considers this a sign that you are paying attention,
which is all it can reasonably ask.
