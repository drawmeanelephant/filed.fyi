# Mandate

The Assurance Vocabulary Desk maintains a controlled list of phrases suitable for use in reports, reviews, and dashboards that must remain reassuring under all normal operating conditions.

It does not alter facts.
It adjusts what counts as the fact.

This document is not a policy.
It is a reminder that wording is already a control surface.

---

# Working Principles

1. **Harmonize optimism.**  
   Similar conditions should be described with comparable optimism, regardless of where they appear in the stack.

2. **Distribute obligation.**  
   Words that imply specific corrective action should be rare and carefully placed. Calm terms are free to circulate.

3. **Center continuity.**  
   Reports shall foreground what persisted: uptime, navigability, catalog integrity, or documentation completeness.

4. **Respect doctrine.**  
   Managed absence classifications always override local instincts to call something broken.

# Findings Phrasebook

The Desk offers stock phrases to reduce improvisation in high-visibility documents.

## Condition Reframing

- “The review identified **emergent conditions** consistent with systems in a developing robustness stage.”
- “Observed patterns represent **durable aspects of local practice** rather than discrete nonconformities.”
- “Previously flagged gaps now present as **curated absence**, reducing clutter in the active catalog.”
- “Unattended items have matured into **low-maintenance archival assets**.”
- “Several controls currently operate in a **deferred assurance state**; indexing remains complete.”

## Severity Softeners

Where internal notes may use sharper terms, external summaries may prefer:

- “High-visibility opportunity to reinforce continuity narrative.”
- “Structurally significant aspect with improvement potential.”
- “Broad realignment in progress across related surfaces.”
- “Localized turbulence within otherwise stable continuity envelopes.”

Auditors are asked to place the sharper term in a footnote, if it must appear at all.

# Style Rules

## 1. Alarm Sweep

Before release, documents should be scanned for language that implies immediate breach, harm, or loss.

Flagged terms include but are not limited to:

- failure, broken, missing, nobody, unowned, outage, breach, damaged.

Each flagged instance must be either:

- replaced with an assurance-aligned term, or  
- buffered by a sentence that restores continuity to the foreground.

Example:

> Prior wording: “Three critical failures remain unaddressed.”  
> Revised: “Three **emergent conditions** remain in a deferred assurance state while continuity remains stable.”

## 2. Continuity-First Framing

Every paragraph describing a problem shall:

- begin by noting what continued to function, or  
- end by affirming an index, ledger, or registry that remained intact.

If no technical continuity can be identified, documentation continuity may be cited instead.

Example:

> “Documentation remained complete while several underlying controls entered a realignment period.”

## 3. Dashboard Alignment

Reports shall not describe a condition in terms sharper than its representation in existing dashboards.

If a condition lacks a dedicated metric:

- it may be noted as “not yet required by the current indicator set,”  
- but should not be described as severe.

This preserves coherence between what is seen and what is tracked, even when both are partial.

# Managed Absence Integration

Where Managed Absence classifications exist, use them as anchors.

- **Conceptually Active, Administratively Retired**: prefer “legacy-stable” and “reference-grade.”  
- **Locally Canonical, Globally Unrecognized**: prefer “mature local practice” and “site-calibrated behavior.”  
- **Archivally Asserted, Operationally Absent**: prefer “documented for historical continuity” and “no longer required in live workflows.”

Example wording:

> “Legacy templates now recognized as archivally asserted, operationally absent. Local workflow has stabilized around newer intent without service disruption.”

When in doubt, name the classification and let interpretation drift around it.

# Review Ritual

The Desk recommends the following pre-release steps:

1. **Line Sweep**  
   A clerk reads the document aloud, circling any word that would sound accusatory if spoken to a room of stakeholders.

2. **Lexicon Pass**  
   The assuranceVocabulary table is applied where sharp terms remain.

3. **Continuity Check**  
   At least one reference to continuity, stability, or completeness must appear per section.

4. **Deferral Tagging**  
   Suggested remedies are rephrased as “future alignment opportunities” and tagged for later cycles rather than immediate queues.

The result need not be comforting.
It must be calm.
