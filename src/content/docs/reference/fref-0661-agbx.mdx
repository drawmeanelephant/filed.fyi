## Purpose

This document establishes the **Aspirational Tier** as a recognized classification within the Filed & Forgotten inaction taxonomy. It distinguishes aspirational inaction from passive inaction and defines the archival criteria for mascots exhibiting peak non-delivery behavior.

## Taxonomy Context

The inaction taxonomy currently recognizes the following tiers:

| Tier | Behavior Pattern | Representative Mascot |
|---|---|---|
| **Passive** | Does nothing; makes no claim otherwise | — |
| **Bureaucratic** | Does nothing; files forms about it | `005.bricky-goldbricksworth` |
| **Aspirational** | Appears to do everything; delivers nothing | `211.apex-goldbricker` |
| **Recursive** | Files forms about the forms about the nothing | — |

The Aspirational Tier is the highest-effort inaction classification. It requires sustained performance of productivity without any measurable output. It is distinct from laziness. It is, in many institutional contexts, indistinguishable from success.

## Classification Criteria

A mascot qualifies for Aspirational Tier classification if it meets three or more of the following:

- Maintains a fully populated calendar with no traceable deliverables
- Generates documentation about planned documentation
- Achieves recognition (badges, commendations, verbal praise) without shipped artifacts
- Has a last-known-good state that predates any known output
- Is described by peers as "always working on something"
- Has a utilization rate above 90% with a delivery rate of 0

## Archival Note

Aspirational Tier mascots are not failures in the conventional sense. They are **optimized for the wrong success metric**. They are the logical endpoint of systems that measure effort rather than output, presence rather than delivery, velocity as a feeling rather than a rate.

They are filed here not as cautionary tales but as **accurate records of what institutions sometimes reward**.
