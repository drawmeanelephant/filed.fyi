## What This Index Is For

This note records recurring confusion observed at chamber lunches, recognition breakfasts, grant luncheons, and adjacent noon-hour assemblies where public warmth is allowed to dress itself as procedural certainty.

It is not a prohibition on enthusiasm.

It is a request that enthusiasm stop writing checks in the margins.

## Handling principles

1. Reception is not disposition.  
Visible support, extended applause, sympathetic laughter, and table-wide nodding may indicate that the room wishes a thing well. They do not, by themselves, identify a fund, a motion, an amount, or an officer willing to defend the arithmetic later.

2. Lunch-shaped assent should remain lunch-shaped unless converted formally.  
Phrases including *broad support in room*, *general assent audible*, *everybody seemed on board*, and *no objection noted over chicken* are to be treated as atmospheric unless followed by ordinary authorizing language.

3. Forecasted generosity remains forecasted.  
Projected ticket sales, likely raffle recovery, expected donor follow-through, and applause after scholarship announcements must not be entered as available funds before counting has become less emotional.

4. Gratitude may accompany allocation without replacing it.  
A room may sincerely wish to help. The minutes should still distinguish appreciation, intent, pledge, authorization, and cash.

## Restricted vocabulary

Use the following terms with some adult supervision.

- **Approved** — avoid where the room merely enjoyed hearing itself support the idea.
- **Committed** — avoid unless the treasurer has been informed in the same century.
- **Covered** — avoid where the shortfall is expected to be solved by optimism, pancakes, or the mayor knowing a guy.
- **The room loved it** — emotionally useful, fiscally void.
- **Basically there** — not a number.

## Filing note

Most luncheon errors do not begin in greed.

They begin in civic warmth, weak field boundaries, and a minute template that places *reception summary* too close to *disposition*.

Where this condition is suspected, clerks are encouraged to preserve the supportive mood while also preserving a line between being pleased and being obligated.

## Interim caution

Any scholarship amount announced before the second syrup spill should be marked aspirational.

Any reimbursement packet citing applause as authority should be re-read on a weekday morning with no tablecloths present.

Any phrase beginning *it was understood at lunch* should trigger immediate interest from someone not seated at the lunch.
