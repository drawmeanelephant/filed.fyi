The Recovered Procedural Artifacts sticker pack is an unofficial training aid that turns three archival fragments into friendly labels.

It does not introduce new doctrine.

It merchandises what was already haunting the margins.

## Favorable Persistence

A speech-bubble sticker quoting the Assurance Vocabulary Annex's habit of describing defects as favorable persistence conditions.

Appears wherever:

- recurring issues are framed as stable features,
- LC-04 Soft Green Seal marks a section as fine enough,
- AV-14 Nullseal Register has absorbed harsher language.

See also: LLG-0323-ASD, LLG-0327-AVA, `reference/assurance-optics`.

## The Waste Elimination Rite

A faux certificate summarizing a training card instruction:

> If the baseline saturates, investigate the staff member who noticed first.

The Rite captures a pattern where attempts to decommission or simplify draw scrutiny to the person who suggested removal, not to the waste itself.

No singular Lorelog case owns the Rite; fragments appear in:

- benchmark saturation notes,
- decommission proposals that stalled,
- C.U.N.T.I.E.R. performance reviews.

## The Soft Green Seal (Object LC-04)

A sticker version of LC-04 Soft Green Seal, simplified into a crown-wearing icon.

In dashboards and slides, this sticker:

- indicates that a section is reassuring,
- does not specify for whom,
- often appears next to intervals with contested interpretation.

See LLG-0323-LC04 for the low-confidence object behind the sticker.

## Archival Treatment

The sticker pack is classified as a teaching specimen: an over-coherent visualization used to explain subtle rot to new staff.

Guidance:

> If you encounter these stickers in the wild, remember that they are summaries of incidents, not replacements. Follow the references back to the messy originals before trusting the cute version.

This is not a policy. It is a cry for help formatted as a policy.
:::
