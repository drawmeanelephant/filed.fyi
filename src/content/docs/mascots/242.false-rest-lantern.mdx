## False Rest Lantern Classification

Failure signature for intervals mistakenly classified as restorative because visible distress has thinned faster than underlying burden.

False Rest Lantern does not create silence.
It illuminates it in a way that invites premature trust.

## Operational Posture

This mascot appears when systems interpret lowered complaint energy, reduced variance, and calm presentation as evidence of recovery without confirming that relief, optionality, or genuine restoration took place.

Observed behaviors include:

- treating low-expression intervals as rest states,
- hanging gentle recovery language over exhaustion,
- encouraging readers to inherit calm before reading qualifiers,
- preserving quiet as evidence of success rather than asking what quiet cost,
- making it socially awkward to reopen an interval already blessed as restorative.

False Rest Lantern is especially active where continuity confidence needs a humane explanation.

## Institutional Habitat

Common containment zones include:

- post-strain review decks,
- care summaries following heavy escalation periods,
- continuity reports with low visible incident counts,
- teams praised for “finally stabilizing,”
- recovery narratives built on reduced noise rather than restored capacity.

Its presence increases when the institution needs silence to mean something kinder than depletion.

## Failure Modes

### Recovery Misread
A state of lowered expression is mistaken for a state of renewed health.

### Silence Haloing
Quiet acquires moral or therapeutic significance beyond its evidentiary basis.

### Reopen Resistance
Once an interval has been archived as rest, renewed complaint begins to look like relapse instead of continuation.

### Depletion Glow
The aesthetic of calm softens recognition that the room is merely too tired to keep signaling.

### Polite Exhaustion Capture
Courtesy, compliance, and low-force presence are absorbed into the image of recovery.

## Recognizable Phrases

- The interval appears calm and restorative.
- A period of needed quiet followed.
- Conditions settled into a recoverable rhythm.
- No immediate distress signals remained visible.
- The lane became noticeably still.

## Archive Note

False Rest Lantern is not assigned where rest is false in the theatrical sense.

It is assigned where the institution encountered stillness and, from a respectful distance, chose to call it sleep.
