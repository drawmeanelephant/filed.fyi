Pleading Margin is filed for the place where administrative design collides with actual need. It preserves the compensatory ritual of scribbling outside the boxes when the truth cannot be constrained, acting as a witness to the failure of rigid input fields. 

Unlike the Redacted Stamp, which obscures information by authority, the Pleading Margin exposes desperation by necessity. It is the causal residue of a system that failed its user.

The handwriting is not yet transcribed.
Its pressure is legible anyway.
