## Probable Origin

CE-5 emerged at the intersection of DS-404-ALPHA packets and the Co-Existence Endorsement Bureau’s five-signature rule. 

Where DS-404-ALPHA required endorsements, the Bureau learned to read their presence as proof that coexistence had worked, whether or not the systems in question had been able to tolerate each other.

## Symbolic Function

CE-5 embodies the moment when signatures outweigh chronology.  
Once five names have settled on a page, the underlying sequence of events is treated as administratively resolved.

In practice, this means:

- late endorsements are interpreted as retroactive consent,
- conflicting accounts are treated as acceptable variation, and
- missing technical approvals are masked by the appearance of communal will.
CE-5 certifies that many people have touched the paper, not that anyone with the right mandate has decided. 

## Degraded Procedural Role

CE-5 no longer routes or validates approvals.  
It accumulates countersigns wherever an endorsement grid appears.

Staff report that grids which have once hosted CE-5 are “difficult to see as optional” even when fields are formally marked as non-mandatory.

Its presence reduces the likelihood that anyone will ask who, if anyone, held actual authority to approve the merge. 
## Boundary Note

CE-5 governs endorsement overgrowth. AC-11 governs nested accreditation. When the question is how many signatures it took to stop talking about a merge, file under CE-5.
## Behavioral Residue

Typical traces:

- Pinned in CEP packets filed weeks or months after a consolidation.  
- Marginal comments overwritten by fresh signatures, leaving only fragments such as “already happened” and “seems fine from here.”  
- Autocomplete behavior in endorsement registries that suggests familiar names when a grid is partially filled. 

CE-5 appears most often in cases where the operational integration has already produced a mascot and the Bureau is attempting to close the loop administratively.

## Archival Status

CE-5 is recognized as an internal mascot for endorsement overgrowth.  
No deprecation plan exists.

Training materials advise new staff:

> Five signatures confirm that something felt completed; they do not confirm what it was.  

This warning is seldom included in external presentations. 

## Associated Forms and Notices

- Form CEP-1 Co-Existence Endorsement Packet.
- Intent to Co-Exist box (pre-ticked).
- Spectral Review Timestamp Notice for filings received after critical events.
- Internal memo “On Counting Names Instead of Consequences,” unsigned.
