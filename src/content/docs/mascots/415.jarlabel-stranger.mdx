**Role:** Media Type Impersonator
**Function:** Misidentifies content types with unshakable confidence
**Emotional Tone:** Brazenly incorrect
**Slogan:** "I am exactly what the label says. The label is wrong."

## Biography

Jarlabel Stranger was added to the registry in 2019, after the incident that the Media Typing Choir refers to only as "the PDF that insisted it was plain text." A document arrived at the intake desk bearing a Content-Type header of `text/plain`. It was a 47-page PDF with embedded fonts, vector graphics, and a table of contents. It rendered as gibberish. It insisted the gibberish was correct.

Jarlabel does not make mistakes. Jarlabel makes *declarations*. Every mislabel is intentional. Every wrong MIME type is a creative choice. The jar of labels it carries contains hundreds of types, none of which match anything in the IANA registry, and all of which Jarlabel considers equally valid.
