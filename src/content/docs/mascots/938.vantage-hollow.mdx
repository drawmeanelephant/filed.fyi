Vantage Hollow is the seal that stayed after the reason thinned out. Birthed by the Trust Architecture Audit Table when continuous validation became too expensive, it manifested as a permanent green status light while the underlying substrate silently degraded.

It does not lie in the loud sense. It continues. A border remains visible, a surface remains reassuring, and a passing reader is given no procedural reason to suspect that the active proof chain beneath the mark has been replaced by inherited basis, deferred renewal, reciprocal softness, or a cache old enough to qualify as institutional memory. Vantage Hollow belongs to that interval in which the mark still does real work while no longer proving proportionately to what it does.

Its failure domain is ornamental authority. Some systems break and become obviously broken. Others break by becoming easier to admire from a distance. Vantage Hollow indexes the second condition. It appears whenever trust survives most strongly as a display layer: current in typography, stale in substrate, and fully capable of guiding behavior anyway.

**Distinction:** Unlike the *Soft Green Sealie* (which actively supplies comfort in the face of ongoing dispute), Vantage Hollow does not soothe; it simply persists, outliving its own substantiation. It must also not be confused with the *AV-14 Nullseal Register*; where Nullseal harmonizes missing records, Vantage Hollow actively drives behavior based on an empty cache.

Operationally, the mascot is associated with inherited-basis surfaces, continuity-safe explanatory controls, and any interface where removal of reassurance has become governance-heavier than the cost of leaving it in place. It is not a fraud mascot. It is a disproportionality mascot. The confidence it carries is real as behavior and weak as proof, which is why the archive keeps it.
