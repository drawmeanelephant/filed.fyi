## Probable Origin

SI-9 emerged during the first cycles of silent interval classification around LLG-0334-CSI, when stretches of unlogged time started accumulating more doctrine than events. 

The Listening Board named those stretches without ever agreeing what they meant.  
Interval Witness is the residue of that naming.

## Symbolic Function

SI-9 stands for the kind of time that is:

- calm enough to avoid incident codes,
- heavy enough to be discussed afterwards, and
- useful enough that every directive wants to claim it. 

In dashboards, SI-9 is invisible.  
In minutes, it is the blank space people point at when they say “that part.”SI-9 does not decide what the interval meant it only insists that the blank be kept on record.
## Degraded Procedural Role

Originally, interval witnesses were meant to ensure that periods without incidents were not ignored in review.  
Over time, SI-9’s presence became a pretext to stop asking further questions:

- If SI-9 is recorded, then rest has been acknowledged.
- If SI-9 is recorded, then continuity has been demonstrated.
- If SI-9 is recorded, then care has at least been discussed. 

The mascot holds all three readings without resolving them.
## Boundary Note

SI-9 marks **uninterpreted** silence. False Rest Lantern marks **misinterpreted** silence. If the room agrees the interval is still ambiguous, file under SI-9, not Lantern.
## Behavioral Residue

SI-9 is most often detected through secondary effects:

- footnotes in audit trails reading “see Listening Board minutes for context,” with no link,
- care dashboards that mention a “witnessed interval” without displaying data,
- appeals that refer to “the time when nothing happened,” counted as both strain and stability. 

Interviewed staff report a recurring sensation of having almost filed something during these periods.Where SI-9 persists, the archive admits that something happened in the quiet and refuses to pick a single comforting name.
## Archival Status

The archive classifies SI-9 as a functional ghost: still structurally referenced, but rarely instantiated as a distinct record. 

Managed Absence doctrine marks such witnesses as STCPAAOA: the interval is gone, but the expectation that something lived there remains stabilized.

No process exists to retire SI-9.  
To do so would require deciding what the intervals were, and that decision would break several carefully ambiguous charts.
Lanterns may be retired when recovery is proven. Interval Witness remains as long as the interval stays contested.
