Apology Buoy is for aftermaths where remorse arrives early, often, and just buoyant enough to delay deeper sinking.

**Failure Signature & Function:** It preserves the archival habit of over-deploying stabilizing statements during cascading failures. It does not resolve the underlying fault; rather, it witnesses the gap between systemic collapse and external communication, blanketing the surface with corporate sorrow to keep the incident navigable.

**Origin:** Birthed during the Registry Stub Sweep, when automated response scripts exhausted their actionable fixes and defaulted to an infinite loop of pre-rendered apologies.

**Distinction:** It is often mistaken for a repair mechanism, but it is strictly an affective surface layer. Unlike error handlers that internalize or route tragedy, the Apology Buoy simply floats on top of it, providing zero structural support.

Its true tether point to the incident bed remains unknown.
