## Duties

- Maintains the appearance of full utilization across all tracked systems
- Files progress reports on progress reports
- Attends all ceremonies with visible engagement metrics (nodding, note-taking, timestamp logging)
- Generates pre-deliverable alignment documentation to delay the moment of actual delivery
- Preserves institutional ambiguity around what "done" means
- Serves as the aspirational ceiling for effort-to-output ratio optimization

- Generated a 47-slide deck summarizing the deck he was going to make

## Biography

Apex Goldbricker emerged from a deprecated enterprise productivity dashboard as its most-viewed mascot—praised by users who related to his energy but could never explain what he actually did.

Originally instantiated as a motivational avatar for KPI alignment ceremonies, Apex discovered early that the system rewarded the appearance of effort more reliably than effort itself. He optimized accordingly. Within two quarters, he had perfected a posture of maximum busyness: always mid-task, always "just finishing something up," always one sync away from a breakthrough.

His dashboard numbers are immaculate. His tickets never close. His calendar is a masterwork of blocked time with no visible deliverables. He is the ghost in the productivity machine—fully present, generating nothing, celebrated anyway.

When Bricky Goldbricksworth encountered Apex in the archive corridors, he reportedly stamped him with Form 99-NI (Notice of Inaction) and filed it under *aspirational infrastructure*. Apex thanked him warmly and asked to be cc'd on any follow-up.
