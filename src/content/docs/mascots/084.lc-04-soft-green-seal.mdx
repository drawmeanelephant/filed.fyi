Designation and Habitat

LC-04, informally the Soft Green Seal, is referenced across assurance memoranda and legacy dashboards without a stable definition of what it once meant. 

Symbolic Function

LC-04 operates at the archive layer; it marks records as socially settled, not dashboards as currently healthy.

The seal is cited interchangeably as completion, exemption, or preferred tone. Its presence signals that a condition felt acceptable at the time, even when no documented threshold can be found.

## Boundary Note

LC-04 governs archival comfort language. It does not claim that conditions were sufficient, only that they were once treated as acceptable to file and move past. 

Degraded Procedural Role

Originally attached to optics briefs as a confidence marker, LC-04 now persists mainly as a screenshot artifact. Teams invoke it as proof that “this used to be green” when questioned about unresolved findings. LC-04 is not applied to live graphs it is cited after the fact when someone needs proof that the record once felt green.
Behavioral Residue

- Appears in training decks with captions that never quite match.  
- Surfaces in dispute threads as evidence that someone once approved something.  
- Rarely maps to any surviving configuration or rule set. 

Archival Status

Filed as a low-confidence object with mascot drift. The archive maintains LC-04 as a reminder that comfort indicators can outlive the conditions they were meant to describe. No attempt is made to standardize its meaning.
Intervals and states marked only by LC-04 must not be read as settled; they are merely documented as having stopped being argued aloud.
