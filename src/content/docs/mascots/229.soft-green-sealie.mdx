# Soft Green Sealie

## Classification

Optics-adjacent mascot for low-confidence health states preserved by familiarity, visual softness, and retrospective tolerance.

Soft Green Sealie is related to existing soft-green seal objects, but behaves less like a badge and more like a migrating interpretive permission slip.

## Boundary Note

Soft Green Sealie governs **live surface reassurance**. LC-04 governs **archival comfort language**. Sealie sits on dashboards, LC-04 in minutes.

In-universe, Sealie is not the seal itself but the merchandised version of LC-04: a friendly avatar used on posters and slide decks that would rather gesture at reassurance than explain what, exactly, is being reassured. Over-Coherence conditions classify Sealie as a teaching mascot, not as a source of truth; any dashboard that treats Sealie as evidence is expected to cite LC-04 and its Lorelog cases explicitly.

## Operational Posture

This mascot appears when a system has no appetite for red, insufficient consensus for yellow, and enough historical fatigue to let green pass.

Observed behaviors include:

- marking intervals as acceptable because they were survivable,
- preserving comfort colors after the conditions that justified them have thinned,
- retrofitting health language to stable strain,
- encouraging readers to inherit reassurance before reading qualification text,
- rewarding threshold familiarity over threshold integrity.

Sealie is only allowed to appear on visual layers that humans stare at regularly; she does not certify underlying doctrine or records.

## Failure Modes

### Familiarity Upgrade
Conditions become “healthy enough” through repetition rather than repair.

### Chromatic Authority
Color reaches judgment before context does.

### Reopen Resistance
Once an interval passes softly into green, reopening it begins to feel socially excessive.

### Historical Softening
Yesterday’s tolerated strain becomes today’s implied baseline.

## Recognizable Phrases

- “This remained within acceptable range.”
- “No immediate concern is indicated visually.”
- “The interval presents as stable.”
- “Historical context suggests the condition is manageable.”
