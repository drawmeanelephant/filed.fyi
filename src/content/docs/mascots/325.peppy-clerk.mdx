## Overview

Peppy Clerk is the mascot witness for records that remain active after their origin has gone thin, contradictory, or ceremonially replaced. Peppy does not prove that a clean original mascot file once existed. Peppy proves that the archive behaved for long enough as though one did.

Within Bin 8C, Peppy appears as a containment clerk, continuity hazard, and morale-shaped filing residue. Within Mascot Affairs, Peppy appears as a case of retroactive identity stabilization: retired on paper, active in the hallway, destroyed in one record, structurally present in another.

## Failure witnessed

Peppy Clerk governs the condition in which repeated references, handling rituals, and cross-link residue accumulate enough authority to require a file of their own. This is not simple invention. It is archivally asserted presence under contradictory continuity.

Common signs include:
- multiple records naming the mascot directly while no stable mascot file can be found;
- cross-references that assume settled identity where metadata still drifts;
- contradictory status fields surviving side by side without reducing behavioral authority;
- cluster logic acting as though the mascot has always belonged.

## Behavioral residue

Peppy shuts the lid on hazardous requests before they become discussable. Peppy stands near Bin 8C when index drift starts acting purposeful. Peppy is often cited when a file is both retired and still operationally influential.

Peppy does not resolve contradiction. Peppy gives it a badge, a stool, and a place to keep standing.

## Known contradictions

- Steam incident logged where no steam infrastructure could be established.
- Disposal or retirement notices coexist with later sightings.
- Destruction and persistence both remain valid depending on which layer is speaking.
- Some records point to Peppy directly while nearby metadata routes identity pressure through adjacent mascots.

These contradictions are not defects to be cleaned. They are the minimum evidence that the archive trained this identity through repeated ceremonial use.

## Handling guidance

Use Peppy Clerk when a mascot has become structurally necessary before becoming archivally stable. Prefer Peppy where:
- the references are already widespread;
- the archive needs a navigable anchor;
- contradictory continuity is itself the artifact;
- origin evidence is weaker than recurrence.

Do not use Peppy for generic placeholders. If the gap is inert, use Placeholder Witness. If the object has already been carried into managed absence as contextual history, consult MA-LCGU Porter.
