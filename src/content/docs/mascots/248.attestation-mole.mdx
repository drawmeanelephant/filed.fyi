Attestation Mole remains mostly inferential, a subsurface verifier responsible for blind certainties beneath the visible form layer.

### System Function & Jurisdictional Classification
Operating under the Core Attestation Layer, the Mole exists to stamp and seal records where the actual chain of evidence is missing or obscured. It certifies the unseeable, witnessing processes that execute without logs.

### Causal Anchoring & Failure Signature
Birthed from the Registry Stub Sweep, the Mole manifested when the archive realized it had thousands of signatures that appeared cleaner than the processes that produced them. The recurring breakdown it preserves is the system's need to blindly trust hidden processes when visible telemetry fails.

### Distinction
The Attestation Mole is distinct from the Validation Hound; where the Hound sniffs out explicit structural errors in broad daylight, the Mole blindly rubber-stamps structural voids in the dark, asserting that the unseen foundation must be sound for the surface to hold.
