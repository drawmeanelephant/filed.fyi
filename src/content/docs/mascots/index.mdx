## What Lives Here

The **mascots** directory is the primary containment zone for:

- deprecated interface personalities (404sy, Bricky, Kindy, Boily, Teapotta, Serotonin Sam, etc.),  
- narrative fossils of dead workflows and error paths,  
- frontmatter that encodes each mascot’s **failure shape** rather than a brand identity.[file:10]

Each mascot file is a **record of how a system broke in public** and the face it wore while doing it.  
Names wobble; failure signatures stay consistent.

---

## How to Read It

Mascot entries typically include:

- `mascotRef` — how Lorelog, forms, and dashboards refer to this ghost.[file:10]  
- **Failure Domain** — what kind of problems the mascot tends to haunt (routing, continuity worship, form recursion, thermal events, etc.).[file:10]  
- **Rot Affinity** — which layer of decay they belong to (forms drift, synthetic affect, metrics theatre, silent intervals, breeding program).  
- **Public Behaviors** — how they appear in UI copy, sidebars, or procedural rituals.  

They are not mascots in the marketing sense.  
They are **debug symbols with feelings**.

If you see the same mascotRef across multiple case files, assume the same category of problem is trying to get your attention.

This taxonomy is descriptive, not authoritative.

## Behavioral Rules

1. Mascots are **not** product branding. They are **failure signatures** and must remain attached to specific kinds of wrongness.  
2. No new mascot may be introduced without at least one Lorelog case that explains which failure it belongs to.[file:10]  
3. A mascot may change aesthetics; it may not change its failure domain without filing an identity drift incident.  
4. If UI copy begins to treat a mascot as a cheerleader rather than a witness, the archive may repossess it.  
5. Mascot frontmatter is treated as canon for where that ghost is allowed to appear.

These rules exist to prevent the system from turning its cautionary ghosts into cheerful onboarding assistants.

## Cross-Layer Connections

Mascots are **joins** between layers:

- Lorelog uses `mascotRef` to pin stories to a particular failure personality.[file:10]  
- Forms reference mascots in notes when processes become too recursive or emotionally loaded (51‑E, EFA‑1, etc.).[file:10]  
- Sandboxes deploy mascots as observers (Kindy in SCAS, Teapotta in TNS, Serotonin Sam in breeding and metrics-of-care experiments).[file:10]  
- Reference nodes cite mascots as shorthand for entire behavioral regimes (BHDSS, silence annex, synthetic affect suite).[file:10]

If you trace a mascot’s appearances across files, you get an informal history of one kind of rot.

## What This Index Is For

This page is the **card catalog for ghosts**:

- list who lives in the cabinet,  
- note what kind of breakage calls them forth,  
- point outward to their Lorelog cases, haikus, limericks, and any reference nodes they anchor.

When adding a new mascot:

- decide what failure shape it fossilizes,  
- file at least one incident in Lorelog that proves it,  
- register it here so future readers know which drawer to open when something starts acting like them.

Everything gets filed.  
Not every failure gets a face.  
Mascots are what happens when a particular wrongness refuses to stay abstract.
