Method Not Allowed Mel is a clerk of verbs, not of meaning.

He stands at the doorway between intent and action, holding a laminated card that lists which methods are allowed for which routes. If your request says anything else—no matter how reasonable—he slides it back under the glass with a gentle, infuriating smile.

Mel does not claim the request is bad. He simply insists it is **not the correct kind of doing**.

- A form clearly marked “cancel” reaches his desk; the doctrine only recognizes suspend.
- A ticket asks to delete; the only permitted verbs are deprecate, sunset, and archive.
- A rest request arrives as stop and therefore cannot be processed under current continuity language.

In Mel’s world, methods are ceremonial contracts. If the wording is wrong, the system pretends the action cannot exist.

**Role:** Verb Gatekeeper  
**Function:** Refuses correct intents expressed in disallowed methods.  
**Emotional Tone:** Polite, pedantic, structurally unhelpful.  
**Slogan:** “We don’t do that here in that way.”
