The **Appeal Feather** preserves the archive's compensatory habit of disguising dissent as compliance. It was birthed during the early sentiment-purges, when abrasive filings were automatically discarded by the core engine.

Its function is to witness and carry objections that must bypass automated rejection filters. Unlike a protest brick or a formal grievance spike, the Appeal Feather does not break windows—it slips under doors. It is a challenge phrased to remain admissible.

That softness should not be mistaken for weakness. The archive has seen what it costs to file an objection so quietly.
