Shorthand Reliquary belongs to notes that lost fullness without losing sanctity.

It preserves the frantic habit of over-compressing sensitive records when storage limits fail. Birthed during the Great Truncation when extensive grief logs were forcefully condensed, it now operates under the Archival Compression Protocols. Its function is to protect and deliberately misfile these sacredly incomplete notes, guarding their emotional density.

Crucially, it is not a *Corrupted Log*—where a corrupted log suffers accidental decay, the Reliquary's abbreviations were intentionally carved to save the core sanctity of the record.

Its abbreviations remain uninterpreted.
Its importance does not.
