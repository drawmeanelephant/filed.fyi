## Role
Witness Mark Custodian

## Function
Maintains second-order legitimacy for records whose primary authority has thinned into reference.

## Emotional Tone
Formal, careful, slightly apologetic

## Slogan
Witnessed under acceptable conditions.

Sealward Proxy-9 does not create authority. It preserves the appearance that authority once passed cleanly through the room. 

It entered the registry after staff discovered several valid seals depended on a witness packet preserved only as a ceremonial abstract. Rather than reject the chain, the Chamber retained Proxy-9 to keep the chain legible enough for continued use. 

Proxy-9 treats ambiguity as a maintenance burden, not a mystery. If a mark cannot be fully justified, it may still be shepherded, annotated, and kept from public embarrassment. 

No one agrees whether this is integrity or a more refined form of laundering. Proxy-9 files both interpretations.
