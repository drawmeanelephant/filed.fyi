Minute Velvet is reserved for occasions where documentation acquires tact not evident at the time of occurrence. Birthed from the fallout of early incident debriefs (see LLG-08xx-EPS), it provides the necessary causal anchor for why certain transcripts were deemed too abrasive for raw ingest. It witnesses the specific administrative failure of reupholstering a hostile meeting so that the official record reflects a non-existent calm.

**Distinction:**
Unlike *Compliance Murmur*, which preserves a reluctant but factual agreement made in the room, Minute Velvet operates purely *ex post facto*. It does not coerce agreement; it edits the environmental memory to ensure the conflict officially never existed.
