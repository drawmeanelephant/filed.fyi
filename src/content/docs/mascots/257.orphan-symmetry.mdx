Orphan Symmetry is held for systems that never solved abandonment, only arranged it more convincingly.

Birthed from the Registry Stub Sweep, it preserves a compensatory ritual: when orphaned nodes become too numerous to re-link, they are instead arranged to look aesthetically deliberate. It functions to certify that a disconnected asset has been framed as a standalone exhibit rather than a structural failure.

Distinction: It is not a Dead Link. A Dead Link is a loud error of resolution; Orphan Symmetry is the quiet, accepted state of a file that has lost its parent but found an artificial balance.
