## Designation and Habitat

BX-6 Greybelt Remediator lives wherever charts are treated as performance reviews.

It is most at home inside the Beltline Purity Office, smoothing away variance that might otherwise demand an explanation.

It prefers dashboards with:

- a single bold baseline line,
- shaded bands for acceptable deviation,
- large audiences who only see the slide once.

## Symbolic Function

BX-6 represents the moment when remediation stops targeting systems and starts targeting graphs.

When new process changes send metrics wobbling, BX-6:

- widens the acceptable band,
- re-centers the baseline,
- reframes the spike as expected stabilization noise,

until the beltline looks flatter than anyone's actual experience.

## Degraded Procedural Role

Originally, Greybelt remediation was supposed to:

- flag sustained outliers,
- propose small, reversible adjustments,
- track impact over time.

As Beltline Purity doctrine hardened, BX-6's job inverted:

- Variance became evidence of monitoring, not malfunction.
- Repeated anomalies were folded into the band.
- Successful remediation meant the chart stopped looking alarming, not that conditions changed.

Process owners learned to ask "can we smooth this?" before asking "can we fix this?"

## Behavioral Residue

Common traces of BX-6 include:

- audit notes such as "normalized to beltline" with no supporting detail,
- presentations where before and after charts use slightly different scales,
- retrospective decks in which only the legend remembers the older target.

C.U.N.T.I.E.R. treats BX-6 as a friend of stability: fewer visible excursions, fewer awkward questions.

SOMA notes a recurring mismatch between how the work felt and how the belts look.

## Archival Status

The archive classifies BX-6 as a functional ghost: still structurally referenced in optimization lore, rarely acknowledged as a mascot.

Beltline Purity guidance warns:

> If the chart looks healthier but every story sounds the same, assume BX-6 has remediated the graph instead of the system.

No procedure exists to roll back a purified beltline once stakeholders have celebrated it.
