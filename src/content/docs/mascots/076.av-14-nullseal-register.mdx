## Designation and Habitat

AV-14 is known internally as the Nullseal Register.  
It occupies the narrow gap between raw audit findings and the summaries that reach executive readers.

Any document that passes through the Assurance Vocabulary Annex may acquire its impression; once imprinted, the underlying language is considered harmonized and is no longer questioned outside Lorelog. 

## Symbolic Function

The Nullseal does not mark approval or closure.  
It marks the moment a condition becomes too encouragingly described to be reopened as a defect.

AV-14 records which phrases have crossed this threshold.  
Its ledger is not sorted by system or severity, only by the order in which terms were declared compatible with continuity optics. 

## Degraded Procedural Role

Originally, AV-14 was intended to track the deployment of updated terminology across templates for QA purposes.  
Over time, staff stopped updating the register and began consulting it as evidence that a phrase was already standard.

The ledger no longer drives change; it testifies that change has already happened.  
Entries are seldom struck, even when terminology is quietly revised again. 

## Behavioral Residue

Observed behaviors include:

- appearing in the margins of audit PDFs as a faint seal over sections that used to say failure,
- re-surfacing in export logs whenever summaries are requested “in encouraging language,”
- generating a calming effect on reviewers, who report difficulty recalling what the original issue was. 

Complaints about over-optimistic wording are frequently filed as EFA-1 emotional appeals rather than technical corrections.

## Archival Status

AV-14 is maintained as an active-but-quiet mascot: not listed on public dashboards, but cited in internal training as the reason findings should be read alongside their associated Lorelog cases.

The register itself has not been fully legible for several cycles.  
Bricky keeps a traced overlay labeled “phrases we no longer trust to mean what they say.”
