## Founding Myth

The Lodge of Mutual Exception LME is said to have formed the first time two departments agreed to honor each other's undocumented waivers for the sake of continuity.

No charter has been recovered.

Membership appears to be conferred by habit:

- if your office's waivers are frequently cited as precedent,
- if you accept others' waivers sight unseen,
- if audits treat Lodge member as self-explanatory,

you are considered to be inside.

## Operating Pattern

LME does not issue waivers directly.

Instead, it:

- treats existing exceptions as transferable,
- treats inherited practices as implied exemptions,
- treats historic arrangements as evidence of design.

Typical interactions:

- Office A cites a ten-year-old dispensation to justify skipping a step.
- Office B, lacking its own dispensation, cites Office A's history.
- The Lodge recognizes this as mutualism: if it worked for them, it must work for you.

No one updates the original waiver.

## Entanglement with COMA-19

Form COMA-19 Idle-Time Amnesty was supposed to be a limited mercy.

Within the Lodge, it became:

- a generalized gap laundering instrument,
- proof that blanks could be sanctified with the right phrase,
- a template for newer, narrower waivers that assumed its logic.

LLG-0019-COMA records multiple instances where entire afternoons vanished under legacy LME cover, with only Lodge annotations to explain the missing work.

## Interaction with Consent and Forms

Forms like 40-C consent cascades and DS-404-ALPHA merge approvals often feed into LME:

- stalled approvals may be nudged past deadlock by invoking prior exceptions elsewhere,
- incomplete consent chains may be wrapped in Lodge language: recognized by custom.

This does not change who signed what.

It changes how missing signatures are perceived.

## Optical Companion: LC-04

Soft Green Seal LC-04 is LME's preferred backdrop.

Intervals or sections covered by Lodge precedent are more likely to:

- display LC-04 in dashboards,
- use Assurance Vocabulary to describe gaps as favorable persistence,
- be treated as handled even when no explicit waiver applies.

Assurance Optics and LME together turn ad hoc mercy into invisible structure.
