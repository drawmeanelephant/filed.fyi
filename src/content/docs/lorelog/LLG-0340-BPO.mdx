## Mandate

The Beltline Purity Office BPO was chartered to guard the integrity of official benchmarks and accepted variance bands.

In practice, this means:

- approving target lines for dashboards,
- defining what counts as within tolerance,
- reviewing any request to change scale, band, or baseline.

The Office has no direct authority over the systems that generate the numbers.

Its jurisdiction ends at the chart.

## Purity Doctrine

Over time, purity came to mean:

- no retroactive edits to historical targets once published,
- no visible band widening without a full justification ritual,
- no acknowledgment that numerical stability can mask human strain.

As a result, when metrics drifted, remediation flowed toward:

- moving the baseline in a way that looked like recalibration,
- redefining outliers as expected stabilization noise,
- annotating anomalies with language from the Assurance Vocabulary Annex.

BX-6 Greybelt Remediator became the Office's unofficial executor.

## Conflict with Remediation

Teams attempting genuine fixes reported:

- BPO rejecting changes that would make charts look worse before they looked better,
- requests for new metrics being delayed until they could be aligned with existing beltlines,
- incidents reclassified as localized turbulence if they did not align with the band.

In several cases, Lorelog notes that:

> the belts stayed pure while the systems frayed; remediation was recast as chart literacy.

## Interaction with Metrics of Care

When Metrics of Care LLG-0820-MCR tried to attach care scores to beltline intervals, BPO raised concerns that:

- care metrics might over-emphasize individual perception,
- spikes in care attention could be misread as instability.

The compromise:

- care overlays would be rendered as faint, non-numeric hints,
- they could not alter the perceived thickness or position of the beltline.

This ensured that caring could be seen, but not allowed to visually outweigh continuity.
