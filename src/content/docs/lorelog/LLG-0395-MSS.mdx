The parade marshal sash was intended to be passed yearly to whichever volunteer could tolerate whistles, route complaints, and the weight of ceremonial polyester.

Instead, the sash spent fourteen consecutive years in the home of one family, who proved consistently competent, locally trusted, and dangerously adjacent to becoming a constitutional principle.

The ambiguity surfaced when a newer volunteer was nominated and asked where the sash was stored. Three members answered, almost in chorus, With the marshals.

No bylaw uses the plural that way.

Ribbonward Cordialis traced the drift through photographs, storage notes, dry-cleaning receipts, and one handwritten annotation reading keep it where it knows the route. His determination was that repeated artifact custody had begun generating office continuity without election.

The sash was not merely held. It had started choosing.

Interim settlement:
- regalia custody confers no automatic office claim,
- prior storage does not imply future command,
- family familiarity with the route may be thanked publicly and ignored procedurally.

Resolution is marked unresolved because everyone agrees the family took excellent care of the sash, and excellence is the local solvent most likely to dissolve governance boundaries without anyone noticing.
