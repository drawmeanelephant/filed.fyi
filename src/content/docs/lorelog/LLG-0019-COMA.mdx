Form COMA-19 exists to legitimize short, necessary periods of downtime without compromising the appearance of uninterrupted continuity required by COMA reporting standards.

Following a dashboard revision that rendered continuity violations in **bright red** with hourly escalation summaries, several teams began pre-filing COMA-19 forms across *entire operational weeks* ***“as a precautionary continuity safeguard,”*** citing *generalized interruption anxiety* and *anticipated maintenance ambiguity*.

Bricky, observing that all required amnesty documentation had already been **properly filed and timestamped**, acknowledged the requests in sequence without objection. The resulting continuity ledger displayed ***uninterrupted operational health*** despite *multiple confirmed outages, abandoned jobs, and at least one fully inaccessible dependency cluster*.

The discrepancy was later identified during a separate archive recency audit when investigators discovered that several systems had been classified as **“continuously available”** during intervals in which *no executable processes had successfully initiated*.

COMA Enforcement reviewed the matter and concluded that the filings had **“technically preserved continuity surfaces”** while *materially destabilizing continuity interpretation standards*.

## No disciplinary action was issued.

Instead, revised guidance clarified that COMA-19 usage should reflect **“localized operational interruption probability”** rather than *“generalized anticipatory discomfort regarding potential future disruption.”*

> The form itself remained unchanged.

Subsequent quarterly reviews showed filing behavior **returned almost immediately to prior levels**, though staff demonstrated increased care in describing outages using *emotionally continuity-compatible terminology* such as:

* ***transitional stillness***
* ***throughput hesitation***
* ***temporarily non-observable execution***
* ***latent service confidence interval***

An advisory preamble was later attached to all COMA-19 packets:

> **Continuity is not determined by interruption alone.**
> **It is established through sustained administrative confidence in ongoing operation.**

The amendment was classified internally as a *clarification rather than a policy change.*
