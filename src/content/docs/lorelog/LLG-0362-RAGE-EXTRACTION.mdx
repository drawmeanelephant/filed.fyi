RAGE Extraction describes what happens when a filer tries to
drag anger out of a feed and into their life.

This file covers patterns where:

- filers report doing something offline in response to RAGE streams, and
- DOGE must decide whether that something constitutes enough
  Origin, Agency, and Residue to reclassify the experience.

---

## Extraction patterns

Observed attempts include:

- contacting real people referenced in RAGE objects,
- joining local meetings or mutual aid efforts,
- changing personal consumption or voting behavior,
- deciding *not* to share or amplify certain streams anymore.

Some of these attempts succeed in grounding.
Others trace a full loop and return to the feed.

DOGE uses the same tests as in the Charter:

- Origin: did a local situation emerge from the attempt?
- Agency: did the filer’s actions change anything beyond their metrics?
- Residue: did the change persist beyond the outrage cycle?

---

## Reclassification outcomes

When extraction succeeds, a RAGE-tagged stream may spawn a
secondary DOGE-certified experience:

- the meeting, not the thread,
- the conversation, not the clip,
- the boundary, not the algorithm.

In such cases, the original stream remains RAGE-classified.
The new experience is filed separately and may qualify as Genuine.

Where extraction fails—no meeting, no call, no lasting change—
the experience remains Simulator Weather, and the attempt is recorded
only as a note in this file.
