The Harriet M. Wren Memorial Scholarship officially awards funds on the basis of service, reliability, and educational intent.

During this cycle, however, reviewers began using phrases not found anywhere in the packet:
- feels like the sort of kid Harriet would have noticed,
- carries herself with the same quiet steadiness,
- not exactly her kind of industrious.

These remarks were not malicious. They were affectionate and impossible to audit.

Sister Casserole of Relief was brought in because the case resembled kitchen attribution drift: the room had preserved devotion so carefully that it now mistook remembrance for procedure. Her review found that donor intent had been colonized by room memory.

The honoree had ceased to be an inspiration and become an undocumented rubric.

Corrective handling:
- memorial language may frame the award, not score it,
- resemblance to the deceased is not an eligibility class,
- any criterion that cannot be shown to the applicant without embarrassment must be removed from the scoring table.

The appeal remains active because one rejected applicant was, by all published criteria, stronger on paper, while another felt unmistakably right to the committee in a way no one could formalize without admitting what they had done.
