During a routine seal continuity review, Chamber staff discovered that three currently valid witness marks depended on a fourth authority whose own recognition packet had been retained only as a ceremonial abstract. 

The abstract contained no scope statement, no renewal date, and no legible signature block. It did, however, include the phrase witnessed under acceptable conditions, which later bodies had cited repeatedly as if it were a jurisdiction. 

A provisional challenge was raised by two local chapter embossers operating under legacy benevolence charters. Their position was simple enough to file and impossible to settle: if a witness must be witnessed, some marks survive only by courtesy, and courtesy is not uniformly accredited. 

The Chamber declined immediate suspension on reputational grounds. Instead, all affected seals were reclassified as conditionally continuous pending proof that the witnessing body had once been adequately witnessed by a body not relying on the same archive packet. 

No such proof has yet been produced.

Temporary handling guidance:
- Seals may remain in circulation if accompanied by Form AR-Null.
- Public-facing confidence language should avoid the word independent.
- Internal objections are to be filed under continuity concerns rather than fraud, unless someone insists.

The matter remains open because too many downstream records now rely on the disputed marks to invalidate them cleanly.
