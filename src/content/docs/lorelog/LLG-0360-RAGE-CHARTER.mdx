RAGE was coined after a sequence of metrics anomalies in which
threads with the highest anger signatures produced the most engagement
and the least durable lore.

Externally, this pattern is called rage bait:
online content deliberately designed to elicit anger or outrage
in order to drive clicks, comments, and watch time.
Internally, the archive now treats it as a first-class failure mode.

---

## Definitions

**RAGE — Routed Anger for Graph Engagement**

Anger whose most legible path runs through dashboards, ad exchanges,
and optimization loops before it runs through anyone's day.

RAGE is identified when:

- the primary observable effect of a stimulus is increased
  engagement metrics, and
- no corresponding local event or residue can be located in the filer’s life.

**BAIT — Behavioral Anger Inducement Tactic**

Any artifact engineered to maximize RAGE regardless of informational value,
local relevance, or care.

BAIT objects share traits:

- sharp moral framing with low specificity,
- repeatable formats that can be reskinned with minimal effort,
- high share velocity, low retention as story.

---

## Relationship to DOGE

RAGE and BAIT classifications sit upstream of DOGE.

- DOGE may not certify RAGE-dominant experiences as Genuine
  until a local anchor is found.
- BAIT-tagged artifacts are treated as simulator input
  and are excluded from provenance scoring unless independently corroborated.

DOGE’s working gloss:

> The feelings are valid.  
> The situation may be rented.

---

## Archive posture

The archive cannot prevent external platforms from manufacturing
RAGE streams. It can, however, refuse to let them masquerade as
life events.

This charter formalizes RAGE and BAIT as names for that refusal,
so that when a graph lights up with anger, staff have a place
to file the heat that never quite made it into anyone’s world.
