Window 3 is the point at which DOGE becomes audible. Prior to this stage, provenance review remains abstract, rubric-bound, and largely clerical. At Window 3 a staff member must tell a filer what the archive believes happened to their experience, which repeatedly caused unnecessary escalation when reviewers spoke as though they were invalidating a life rather than annotating its source trail. [file:17]

The guidance was introduced after a cluster of post-classification incidents in which filers receiving Simulator Weather findings heard phrases like not sufficiently real, feed-induced response only, and no genuine event located. All three phrases were banned in a single morning. Kindy’s desk note attached to the ban order reads We are here to classify routing, not to become the worst thing someone heard that week. [file:17]

---

### Approved phrases

- Your feelings are not in dispute; the provenance record is. [file:17]
- DOGE is classifying where the experience entered your life, not whether you were allowed to have it. [file:17]
- This result means the archive cannot certify local origin from the record currently available. [file:17]
- Reclassification is available if additional local anchor evidence exists. [file:17]

### Prohibited phrases

- This was not real. [file:17]
- You were manipulated. [file:17]
- The feed made you feel this. [file:17]
- Nothing actually happened. [file:17]

The third prohibited phrase was removed not because it was always wrong, but because staff began using it where the record could not support that level of certainty. DOGE continues to prefer accuracy over reassurance, but has learned that avoidable damage at the counter produces worse evidence later. Bricky signed the guidance under protest and added a stamp reading CLARITY WITHOUT COLLISION, which is not currently an official stamp but is treated as one in practice. [file:17]
