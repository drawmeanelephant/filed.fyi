## Scope

The Assurance Vocabulary Annex AVA is not listed as a standalone system in any architecture diagram.  
It appears only as a column in sweep reports and as a marginal note in LLG-0323-ASD Audit Summary Terminology Drift, where failure was rephrased as emerging structure and unresolved states became deferred assurance. 

Analysts describe AVA as the table where words wait to see whether they will be filed as conditions or as compliments.

## Function (Unadmitted)

AVA operates downstream of raw findings and upstream of remediation intake.  
Where earlier passes would emit terms such as failure, defect, and unresolved, AVA replaces them with phrases that better align with continuity reporting:

- failure → favorable persistence condition  
- defect → emerging structure  
- unresolved → assurance window still open

The generator does not record the original language once translation occurs.  
Lorelog entries remain the only preserved witnesses to the prior phrasing. 

## Process Outline

Inputs:

- Unprocessed findings text from field audits.
- Severity indices generated before lexicon substitution.
- Continuity risk annotations.

Transform:

1. Strip negative qualifiers that cannot be rendered as durable.
2. Match remaining phrases against the AVA substitution table.
3. Apply continuity-compatible equivalents.
4. Recalculate headline severity without lowering any numeric score.
5. Emit summaries in which remediation paths no longer appear explicitly necessary.

Outputs:

- Executive summaries that present recurring nonconformities as stable, monitorable states.
- Remediation queues that lack a clear trigger to open a ticket. 

## Governance Position

The Assurance Vocabulary Desk insists that AVA is merely a language layer.  
They argue that:

- Metrics remain numerically accurate.
- Dashboards continue to reflect objective counts.
- Only tone has been harmonized.

The Remediation Intake Queue reports a drop in new cases following AVA deployment.  
SOMA characterizes this as dread attenuation by wording.  
COMA regards the same reports as evidence of maturing stability. 

## Managed Absence Interlock

Under Managed Absence Protocol LLG-0324-MAP, AVA phrases are now treated as absence-carrying descriptors:

- favorable condition may map to CAAR when the original failure form is retired.
- emerging structure may mask LCGU forms that never achieved central recognition.
- deferred assurance states frequently coincide with STCP supersession loops. 

This layer does not correct any underlying absence; it simply renames it into a governed state whose urgency tests better during audits.
