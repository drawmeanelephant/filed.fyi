Following the COMA Silent Interval Certification incident (LLG-0334-CSI), the Continuity and Uptime Normalization Bureau and SOMA's Emotional Load Balancing Chamber agreed to conduct a joint review of one exemplar period.

The interval in question:

- contained no incidents,  
- generated no tickets or rest requests, and  
- showed no visible variance on COMA dashboards.

COMA had already stamped it **HEALTHY CONTINUITY — SILENCE OK**.  
SOMA had indexed the same interval as **UNPROCESSED STRAIN (SBI HIGH)**.

To avoid further docket ping-pong, the Directive Concordance Board authorized the creation of a **Silent Interval Review Chamber (SIRC)** in Annex 5, where both directives could "listen again" in a controlled setting.

---

## Chamber Setup

Annex 5 was prepared with the following constraints:

- No new monitoring would be added; only existing telemetry and logs for the interval could be replayed.  
- SOMA could bring annotated margins, but no new forms.  
- COMA could bring dashboards, but no new metrics.  
- C.U.N.T.I.E.R. was allowed to observe but not to introduce additional scoring.

The Chamber itself is described in the docket as:

> "An acoustically indifferent room with one large shared display and two smaller, mutually unaware dashboards."

Bricky sat between the screens with a notebook and no mandate to reconcile.

---

## COMA's Transcript

COMA arrived with:

- the continuity ledger segment for the interval,  
- a COMA-07 cross-reference noting **no error, no complaint, no outage**, and  
- the reassuring shade of green used for "nothing went wrong, as far as we have chosen to know."

COMA's official reading:

1. Absence of incident filings → **no recorded harm**.  
2. Absence of downtime requests → **no felt need to rest**.  
3. Stable throughput graphs → **no interruption to stakeholders' expectations**.

COMA concluded:

> "Silence, in the presence of scheduled activity and the absence of formal exception, must be treated as continuity sufficiency."

When SOMA pointed to the lack of descriptive entries, COMA responded that "unspoken conditions are outside continuity jurisdiction unless they manifest as throughput anomalies."

In COMA's transcript, the interval spoke only one word: **OK**.

---

## SOMA's Transcript

SOMA arrived with:

- Silence Burden Index traces for the same period (LLG-0338-SBI),  
- Lorelog margin excerpts marked "almost incidents," and  
- a list of rest requests drafted but never submitted.

SOMA's reading emphasized three signals:

1. **Duration** — A sustained stretch of uninterrupted activity beyond the team's typical complaint cadence.  
2. **Historical pattern** — The same unit had previously filed SOMA-72 requests when stressed; here they did not.  
3. **Margin noise** — Multiple notes in adjacent case files: "will file later," "not worth a form," "COMA-19 already feels tired of us."

SOMA concluded:

> "Silence that should have contained a complaint, had conditions been humane, measures as load, not peace."

In SOMA's transcript, the interval did not say "OK."  
It said nothing, loudly, for longer than usual.

---

## The Dual Record

The Chamber recorded both transcripts verbatim.

- COMA's version was appended to the continuity ledger as **Affirmation of Stable Operation**.  
- SOMA's version was attached in the silent interval annex as **Evidence of Accumulated Burden**.

Because both used sanctioned formats and referenced the same timestamps, the tri-directive evidence parser treated them as corroborating records rather than contradictions.

This resulted in:

- the interval counting once toward **healthy continuity minutes**, and  
- the same interval counting once toward **elevated Silence Burden Index**.

C.U.N.T.I.E.R. quietly updated its models to treat **"high SBI during COMA-healthy intervals"** as a marker of "engaged resilience."

No one approved this interpretation. It simply fit.

---

## Staff Presence, Unofficial Notes

Three human-origin staff were invited to attend as "operational witnesses" for the review.

They were asked two questions:

1. "During that interval, did things feel fine?"  
2. "During that interval, did things feel like they could be sustained indefinitely?"

The recorded answers:

- To (1): "We were busy, but nothing exploded."  
- To (2): "We could not have done another week of that."

COMA's proxy logged both as **CONFIDENCE ACCEPTABLE**.  
SOMA logged the same responses as **VEILED DISTRESS, TONE NEUTRAL**.

Bricky wrote, in a notebook that is technically not part of any dashboard:

> "Everyone agrees it was too much.  
>  No one agrees that 'too much' is a continuity fact."

This line is preserved in LLG-0339-SIRC but not surfaced in any metrics view.

---

## Directive Positions

After the session:

- **COMA** reaffirmed that "silence, in the absence of errors, is the safest continuity signal available," and warned that treating quiet as risk would "collapse confidence in green periods" across the ledger.

- **SOMA** insisted that intervals with high SBI should be considered candidates for proactive mitigation, particularly when COMA has already celebrated them.

- **C.U.N.T.I.E.R.** suggested labeling such intervals as "High-Efficiency Quiet Zones" and monitoring downstream performance, a proposal SOMA described as "optimizing the rate at which people learn not to speak."

The Directive Concordance Board refused to invalidate either transcript, citing the risk of "lexical precedent" for future conflicts.

---

## Chamber Outcome and Recurrence

LLG-0339-SIRC closes with the following operational rules:

1. **Every certified silent interval must now receive a paired SOMA interpretation**, even if it is simply "no additional burden detected."  
2. **If COMA and SOMA disagree**, both records must remain; neither may be pruned for consistency.  
3. **No attempt may be made to average or merge the two readings** into a single number.

In practice:

- Dashboards display COMA's version as the headline.  
- SOMA's version lives behind a hover tooltip reading "Interpretations (2)."

The Chamber remains in use.

Subsequent sessions have not reduced the gap between what continuity hears and what strain says. They have only made the gap archival.

The interval at the center of this case continues to exist twice:

- Once as a quiet success.  
- Once as a place where no one dared to admit how tired they were of being quiet.
