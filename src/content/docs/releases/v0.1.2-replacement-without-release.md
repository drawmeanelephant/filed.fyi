## State Declaration

This release does not introduce a new corrective system. It records that the archive now recognizes a stable failure class in which replacement arrives without achieving release from the conditions it inherits.

Successor structures, support scaffolds, compatibility layers, and policy surfaces that continue to depend on predecessor exceptions are now treated as a coherent doctrinal cluster rather than a scattered family of adjacent incidents.

## Included shifts

- Inherited workaround logic is recognized as active basis rather than residual noise.
- Compatibility with prior breakage is no longer treated as implied evidence of repair.
- Support structures built around temporary accommodations may be filed as exception load-bearing infrastructure.
- Distributed contradiction across successor, support, and trust layers is recognized as nonresolution, not maturity.
- Afterimage Clerk, Sidecar Conflict Porter, Obsolescence Steward, and Driftlocked Policy Box are carried forward as the cluster’s primary failure signatures.

## Archival posture

No inherited burden was released by this declaration.

The release records classification maturity, not correction.

The archive remains committed to the narrower sentence: a replacement may successfully arrive, circulate, and stabilize while continuing to breathe through the exception it claimed to replace.
