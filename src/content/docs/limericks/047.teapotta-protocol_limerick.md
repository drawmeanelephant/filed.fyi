## Limericks

### 1

"I'm a teapot," said 418.
"I refuse to elaborate, mate."
No coffee was brewed,
No request was pursued,
And the protocol stubbornly sat.

### 2

His existence predates the logging.
Nobody remembers his blogging.
He just sits there, stout,
Full of tea and of doubt,
And the RFC keeps on clogging.

### 3

"Don't ask," said the sign on his face,
While he occupied server-side space.
His function was nil,
His purpose a thrill,
And the nonsense was perfectly placed.

### 4

He floated away in a dream
Of a protocol nobody'd deem
Worth implementing—
But still representing
The internet's weirdest regime.

### 5

He shares tea with the protocol spirits
Each week—and nobody hears it.
The ritual's old,
The teapot is bold,
And the caffeine is nowhere near it.

### 6

"Coffee is not in the spec,"
He said with a stubborn-necked check.
His spout was precise,
His handle was nice,
And the HTTP error was tech.

### 7

He was born before logging existed—
An artifact, never delisted.
The spec was a joke,
The internet spoke,
And Teapotta just quietly persisted.

### 8

He appeared on a protocol chart,
Redacted and circled with art.
In crayon, no less,
"TEAPOT" was the guess,
And the whitepaper fell right apart.

### 9

He stamps dates that never happened
On documents carefully fashioned.
The metadata's fake,
The ceremony opaque,
And the brewing is strictly rationed.

### 10

So if HTTP 418 appears
And the response brings only tears
Of confused delight,
That's Teapotta's plight:
A teapot. For all of these years.
