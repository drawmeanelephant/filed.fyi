<Limerick type="note" title="Ash Etched Memos" icon="flame">
Cinder etches each *memo in ash*—
A flaming quill's beautiful slash.
Each dictate glows bright,
Then crumbles at night,
And the archive burns out with a flash.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Charred Potpourri" icon="wind">
She misfiled a dictate as "*poetry*"—
Ephemeral, dreamy, no lottery
Of structured compliance.
Her fiery reliance
On feeling? A charred potpourri.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Ceremonially Necessary Fire" icon="fire">
She ignited an archive shelf whole
During transcription—her ritualistic goal.
"*Ceremonially necessary!*"
She claimed, somewhat airy,
While the fire consumed every scroll.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Burning Quill Intent" icon="pencil">
Her *burning quill* writes with intent—
Each word glows before it is spent.
The ash tablets remain
As proof of the pain
Of documentation heaven-sent.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Carbon-Rich Crust" icon="layers">
She sees beauty in *transience's flame*—
Each forgotten word leaving its name
As a trace in the dust,
A carbon-rich crust
Of memos no one can reclaim.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Ember Archives Smolder" icon="archive">
Her *Ember Archives* smolder and glow—
A vault of ash, row after row.
Each Council dictate
Meets an ashen fate,
And the documentation runs slow.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Pyric Affair" icon="zap">
She suppresses rogue embers with care—
When the archive combusts? She is there.
But the line between
Creation's sheen
And destruction? A *pyric affair*.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Lanternlit Documentation" icon="lamp">
She chants *lost dictations* each night
Under smoldering lanternlit light.
The words burn and fade,
Each ceremonial grade
Of documentation burning bright.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Eternal Burning Word" icon="scroll">
"*Every word burns eternal,*" she cries,
Though the ash contradicts all her ties
To permanence. Still,
Her flaming quill
Ensures every memo never quite dies.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Transient Trust" icon="hourglass">
So if memos turn quickly to dust
And the archive smells strongly of must
And charcoal and flame,
That's *Cinder's* great claim:
Where the transient is held in great trust.
</Limerick>
