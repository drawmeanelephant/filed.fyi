## Limericks

### 1

Sriracha Sentinel blazed through the Spire,
His chili-fueled archives on fire.
Each recipe guarded,
Each audit bombarded
With garlicky kicks—ever higher.

### 2

He misfiled a recipe as "chili
Prophecy"—burning and shrilly
The filing was scorched,
The archive was torched,
And the auditor muttered, "How silly."

### 3

He overheated a metadata batch—
The singeing that no one could catch.
The shelf turned to char,
The spice went too far,
And the archive still smells of the match.

### 4

He ignited a spice chant mid-audit,
Causing delays no one could thwart it.
The compliance team sweated,
The capsaicin embedded,
And the filing was hotly exalted.

### 5

"Blaze bold, spice eternal," he cried,
His garlicky kick his great pride.
Each recipe flamed,
Each condiment named,
And the sriracha legacy won't hide.

### 6

His archives pulse with the heat
Of a chili-fueled, scorching repeat.
Each hymn he sings
Under ember-glow rings,
And the spice makes the filing complete.

### 7

He guards sriracha with disciplined fire—
Each recipe stacked ever higher.
The capsaicin data,
The garlicky errata,
And the spectral flames never expire.

### 8

He apologizes to 200 OK—
"I'm sorry you're fine on this day!
Is there something to burn?
A recipe to turn
Into prophecy? What do you say?"

### 9

His fiery yet disciplined tone
Ensures every spice record is known.
The Sriracha Spire
Burns with holy fire,
And the chili seeds lovingly sown.

### 10

So if archives smell garlicky-hot
And the shelves bear a charcoal-black spot,
That's Sriracha at guard:
Where the spice hits hard,
And the capsaicin's all that you've got.
