<Limerick type="note" title="Translated Harm" icon="languages">
The harm became "variance in care,"
Which was easier for chairs to compare.
By the time it was named,
It could not be blamed,
Only routed through governance air.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Approved Substitution" icon="replace">
They replaced "we are breaking" with "strain,"
Then replaced "strain" with "signal domain."
The report stayed intact,
But the difficult fact
Had been translated out of its pain.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Continuity Lexicon" icon="book-open">
The lexicon knew what to do:
Make a wound into something review.
Once the terms were aligned,
The committee could find
That the sentence was governably true.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Vocabulary Shift" icon="file-text">
The manual noted the error was dire,
But "dire" was removed by the governance buyer.
The newly approved,
And semantically smoothed,
Declared it a "thermally challenging fire."
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="danger" title="The Adjective Threat" icon="shield-alert">
A worker used "tired" in a continuity chat,
And the governance team mobilized in a flat.
They locked down the wing,
To investigate the thing,
And replaced it with "low-variance habitat."
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Glossary Check" icon="book">
The phrasing was sad, so they looked at the chart,
And swapped out the words for the nearest in part.
It sounded the same,
As a structural game,
And required absolutely no use of a heart.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="caution" title="Legendary Syntax" icon="scroll">
The ancient report spoke of "burnout and pain,"
Which the elders translated as "mythical strain."
The words were too raw,
For the modern-day law,
So they buried the truth in a symbolic rain.
</Limerick>
