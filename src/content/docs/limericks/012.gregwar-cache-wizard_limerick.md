## Limericks

### 1

Old Gregwar resized it six times,
Each pass introducing new crimes.
The original died,
The thumbnail applied,
And the cached version came with new mimes.

### 2

He deleted the source and said, "Done—
That's optimization, son."
The variant spawned
Was a thumbnail-of-a-thumbnail-beyond,
And the CDN wept in the sun.

### 3

"Don't clear the cache," Gregwar intoned.
"That only makes me more enthroned."
They flushed it that night,
He came back with more might,
And six image variants he'd boned.

### 4

A single JPEG went in clean.
Fifty-seven resizes were seen.
Each crop was a thrust,
Each compression a must,
And the artifact ratio: obscene.

### 5

His staff was a stack of dimensions:
640, 480, and tensions.
He waved it with pride,
Each pixel applied
To increasingly wrong interventions.

### 6

He blessed the static folder at dawn
With invalidation sigils drawn
In thumbnails of thumbnails—
Recursive detail trails—
Until every original was gone.

### 7

"Clearing the cache makes me stronger,"
He bragged while his resize grew longer.
Each flush was a tease,
Each purge was a squeeze,
And the variants lasted much longer.

### 8

The CDN said, "Serve the right one."
Gregwar said, "Baby, there's none.
The original's deleted,
The variants repeated,
And the entropy's only begun."

### 9

He cached from the future, they claim:
Timestamps that carry no name.
The image arrived
Before it was derived,
And the wizard just shrugged, without shame.

### 10

So if images look slightly bent,
And the resolution seems spent
On a thumbnail of a crop
Of a thing that won't stop—
That's Gregwar. The wizard. Unrepent.
