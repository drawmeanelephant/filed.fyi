## Limericks

### 1

Old Zooki juggled keys with a flair,
Taming clusters with disciplined care.
But the cage that he locked
Left himself fully blocked,
And the daemons ran free through the air.

### 2

"Too many processes!" he cried,
"Not enough leashes!" he sighed.
Each node misbehaved,
Each lockfile engraved,
And the coordination timeout applied.

### 3

He locked himself out of the cage
In a classic zookeeper rampage.
The servers ran wild,
The concurrency smiled,
And containment was strictly off-stage.

### 4

His lockfile discipline was tight—
Each key jangled perfectly right.
The chaos was bound,
The nodes brought around,
And the cluster survived through the night.

### 5

A runaway daemon escaped,
And Zooki gave chase, fully caped.
But the leash came undone,
The daemon had won,
And the server logs gaped, agape'd.

### 6

He enforces with frantic precision
Each lock, each key, each decision.
The process IDs clash,
The concurrency's brash,
And the timeout is Zooki's partition.

### 7

He dances the cache-invalidation—
Three steps of confused celebration.
Step one: lock the file.
Step two: wait a while.
Step three: forget the whole situation.

### 8

His keys jangle like chaotic wind chimes
Through server rooms, marking the times.
Each node that he binds
Just unwinds and reminds
That coordination commits many crimes.

### 9

The zookeeper theme was on brand:
Each daemon an animal, planned.
But Lockjaw's own cage
Held him hostage, enraged,
And the cluster escaped, out of hand.

### 10

So if lockfiles appear in the night
And the concurrency isn't quite right,
That's Zooki at play:
Leashing chaos all day,
While locked out of his own oversight.
