## Limericks

<Limerick type="note" title="Cocoa Menus Astray" icon="monitor">
Anlas injected at login each day
Via DYLD_INSERT—what a way
To customize Macs.
Each Cocoa app cracks,
And the Finder menus went astray.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Reframed Preference Panes" icon="settings">
He just wanted to help, so he claimed,
While whispering plist files, unnamed,
In LaunchAgents deep.
No daemon would sleep,
And the preference panes were reframed.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="FruitMenu Restoration" icon="apple">
He patched Finder menus mid-flight—
FruitMenu restored? What delight!
But the panic ensued,
The re-enabling crude,
And he cried into `/Library/` at night.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Cryptographic Load" icon="lock">
macOS 10.5 broke his code—
Code signing was his cryptographic load.
Then 10.11's SIP
Salted the earth, rip,
And Apple Support blessed the road.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Unreliable Customization" icon="swatchbook">
He made Macs truly customizable—
Weird, dangerous, and inadvisable.
The ShapeShifter themes,
The runtime-patched dreams,
And the stability? Quite unreliable.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Universally Fair Blame" icon="alert-octagon">
"Remove all Unsanity software"—
Apple Support's general fix everywhere.
Got something weird?
Anlas must be cleared.
The blame was universally fair.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Post-Deletion Reappearance" icon="ghost">
He tends to reappear post-deletion—
Spiritually, if not a completion
Of reinstallation.
His ghost manifestation
Survives every system discretion.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Silent Crash Logs" icon="file-text">
His crash logs are silent but true—
No dialog pops up in view.
Just traces left behind
In the logs, unsigned,
And the runtime injection breaks through.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Modular Mac Paradigm" icon="puzzle">
For a brief, beautiful window in time,
System daemons were friends—paradigm
Of customization.
The user's foundation
Was Mac, modular, and sublime.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Lost Injection Game" icon="gamepad-2">
So if crash logs whisper his name
And the LaunchAgents folder's aflame,
That's Anlas at rest:
Pre-SIP's very best,
And the runtime injection's lost game.
</Limerick>
