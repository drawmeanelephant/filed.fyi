## Limericks

### 1

Old Melody played a crash dump
As a symphony, right from the rump
Of a QA review.
The audience knew
That the bug had a hell of a thump.

### 2

She locked twenty thousand in a loop
Of notification musical soup.
Each ping was a chime,
Each alert was a rhyme,
And the kill switch was lost in the group.

### 3

Her SoundBlaster grandpa would weep
At the modern audio's cheap beep.
"Where's my FM synth?"
She'd rage like a myth,
And the ISA shrine she would keep.

### 4

She installed an 8-bit ringtone
On every device, overblown.
Each phone went berserk,
Every laptop went quirk,
And the chiptune was company-wide known.

### 5

At 3:03 she'd float through the queue,
Auditioning each ping's debut.
She'd tweak the EQ
'Til the email went "Ooh,"
And the harp glissando came true.

### 6

Her task list, when stressed, would transform
Into a rickroll-variant norm.
The MIDI would play,
She'd roll-eye and say,
"Where's my DAC in this integrated form?"

### 7

She'd hum sampled PC beeps between
The alerts—the most musical scene.
Each urgent dispatch
Got a piano-outro patch,
And the warnings sounded pristine.

### 8

Her inaugural show was a wreck:
The driver stack froze, what the heck.
Millions of machines
Froze mid-notification scenes,
And she's been scared of muting since—check.

### 9

She kept old ISA cards in a shrine,
Dusted each one at every bootline.
The FM synth purred,
The vintage sound stirred,
And the modern APU? "Not fine."

### 10

So if alerts arrive like a song
And the notification's eight bars long,
That's Melody's score:
Where each ping is lore,
And the crash dump's a tune all along.
