<Limerick type="note" title="The Wire is Old Now" icon="cable">
The wire was strung *long ago*—  
In a manner most systems don't know.  
Old Pilgrim spoke slow,  
In *HTTP/1.0*,  
And refused to upgrade with the flow.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Answering in the Older Manner" icon="message-circle">
It answered in ways now *outdated*—  
With protocols long since negated.  
"Adapt," it would say,  
"In the old-fashioned way—  
Or forever be frustrated."  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Refusing a New Protocol" icon="slash">
A connection request came down—  
Using *TLS 1.2* for the crown.  
"Too new," said Old Wire,  
"I'll not to admire,  
Just HTTP/1.0 in this town."  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Forcing an Application Back" icon="arrow-left">
A modern app came to call—  
With protocols *new* over all.  
But Old Wire replied,  
"You'll speak now applied—  
To HTTP/1.0 in this hall."  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Tending a Legacy Garden" icon="plant">
The connections are *fragile and old*—  
With wisdom from days long since sold.  
Old Pilgrim tends care  
To the protocols there,  
And preaches their virtue retold.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Walked In During the First Migration" icon="footprints">
When *HTTP/2* came to stay—  
Old Pilgrim just walked far away.  
"The old way was best,  
I'll not take the test,  
And nothing new here comes to play."  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Virtue of Simple Protocols" icon="circle">
The simpler protocols *shine bright*—  
Said Old Pilgrim with all of its might.  
"You modernize too fast,  
My ways will outlast,  
And HTTP/1.0's my light."  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="The Old Wire Will Not Break" icon="shield">
No matter what standards you use,  
Old Pilgrim will always *refuse*.  
The wire persists,  
The protocol lists,  
And nothing new comes to the fuse.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="426 Required (Unheeded)" icon="alert-circle">
A server responded quite dry,  
"Upgrade your request," came the cry.  
But Old Wire just stood,  
Said, "I would if I could—  
But I only speak *older,*" thereby.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Connection Kept Open" icon="link">
The connection was held *without end*,  
No headers to signal or bend.  
It waited in place,  
At a much slower pace—  
For a client that chose not to mend.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Stateless Devotion" icon="repeat">
Each request stood *alone* in its grace,  
No session, no stored interface.  
Old Pilgrim declared,  
"No state shall be shared—  
Each moment must stand in its place."  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Protocol Refusal Loop" icon="refresh-ccw">
They asked it to change once again,  
To speak in the modern domain.  
It answered each plea  
With the same *legacy*:  
"I was old. I am old. I remain."  
</Limerick>
