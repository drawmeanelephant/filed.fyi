<Limerick type="note" title="Low-Maintenance Stuff" icon="box">
If an orphan hung round long enough,  
It stopped being evidence rough.  
Three intervals passed,  
And at glorious last  
It was "low-maintenance" archival stuff.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Semantic Shift" icon="move">
Unclaimed files once counted as drift.  
Then the threshold got widened to gift.  
Now neglect, with a nod,  
Became efficient by God,  
Which is one wicked semantic shift.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Unattended Skin" icon="user-minus">
Retention guidance, revised with a grin,  
Let ownership failure just win.  
If concern never sparked,  
Then the asset was marked  
As stable in unattended skin.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Smell of the Shells" icon="utensils">
This is one of those corporate spells  
Where abandoning shit somehow sells  
As intentional calm  
With a stewardship balm  
Rubbed over the smell of the shells.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Abandonment's Great" icon="shield-off">
Executive concern was the gate.  
No concern? Then abandonment's great.  
Which means files can decay  
In a beautiful way,  
As long as no vice president's late.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Desirable Wire" icon="zap">
The threshold rose higher and higher.  
Each jump set accountability on fire.  
By the end of the game,  
No owner was shame.  
It was simply desirable wire.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Efficiency Gloss" icon="sparkles">
Some unattended remains are just lost.  
You should say that and reckon the cost.  
But the archive got cute  
And invented a route  
Where neglect wore efficiency gloss.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Noncontact Aloof" icon="user-x">
No one touched them. That was the proof.  
No one cared. That became the roof.  
Under that logic, a shed  
Full of ruin is fed  
By strategic noncontact aloof.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Learn Its Damn Name" icon="tag">
I admire the sheer brass of the claim:  
"This file's untouched because order's the aim."  
No, friend, it's untouched  
Because nobody much  
Could be bothered to learn its damn name.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Rot's Best Suit" icon="shirt">
So if orphan counts start looking blessed,  
Someone moved the threshold, not the mess.  
Low-maintenance lore  
Is what rot uses for  
Its best suit in archival distress.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Non-Recoverability" icon="x-circle">
Once a record goes dark past the line,  
No retrieval can make it re-shine.  
   Whatever you build  
   Is new, and it's filled  
With the ghost of what used to define.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Threshold Manipulation" icon="alert-octagon">
Try resetting an orphan's last age—  
Reclassify, reopen the stage—  
   The archive just notes  
   The move in its rotes  
And logs your manipulation as sage.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Rubbed and Scrubbed" icon="sparkles">
The orphan was rubbed "til it shone,  
With a vigor the ledger had known.  
   It was massaged and subbed,  
   Until technically scrubbed,  
And the archive called it its own.  
</Limerick>
