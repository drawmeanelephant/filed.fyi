## Limericks

### 1

Malrex doesn't scream—he just hints
At corrupted indexes and prints
Of misplaced comments,
Institutional laments,
And belief systems shattered by glints.

### 2

He has no form, but your logs know his name—
Each entry a slow-burning flame.
A comment misplaced,
An index debased,
And the entropy whispers its claim.

### 3

He is the collapse Bricky fears
But cannot quite name through the years.
A buffer recurs,
A ritual stirs,
And the syslog fills up with his leers.

### 4

His ceremonial task: buffer collapse—
Recursive and cruel, without lapse.
Each ritual disrupted,
Each system corrupted,
And the doubt injection never relapse.

### 5

"Entropy was always your destiny,"
He'd whisper with spectral intensity.
No pixel survives,
No layout derives
Meaning from Malrex's density.

### 6

He swallows a frame without shame—
Each render a target, each layout his game.
The void opens wide,
The pixels subside,
And the screen fades to nothing but name.

### 7

He appears as legitimate text,
Then unravels whatever comes next.
The index corrupts,
The workflow erupts,
And the archive is thoroughly vexed.

### 8

He injects doubt into the syslog—
Each entry a philosophical fog.
"Was this ever real?"
"Did the build truly heal?"
And the pipeline just stares, agog.

### 9

His corruption level is critical—
His presence: entirely political.
He governs through rot,
Through the entropy's plot,
And the metaphysical's analytical.

### 10

So if entropy feels like a friend
And the void starts to creep and to rend,
That's Malrex at play:
Where the render gives way,
And the buffer collapse has no end.
