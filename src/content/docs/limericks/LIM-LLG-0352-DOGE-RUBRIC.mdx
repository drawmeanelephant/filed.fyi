<Limerick type="note" title="The Rubric's Origin" icon="map-pin">
Test One: where did it begin?  
Was the filer already in  
  Contact with body  
  And place? Or was anybody  
Else's algorithm the pin?  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Origin Weight" icon="scale">
If the only identifiable start  
Is "the algorithm played its part"—  
  Reduced origin weight.  
  Simulator candidate state.  
The experience: rented, not art.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Agency" icon="key">
Could the filer alter the course,  
Duration, outcome—apply force?  
  Leaving counts.  
  Refusing amounts  
To agency. Reacting: lower source.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Platform Affordances" icon="thumbs-up">
Likes and replies: engagement, yes.  
Agency: lower. Can't suppress  
  Or meaningfully leave?  
  That counts—relieve  
The filer. Not lower—less.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Residue Defined" icon="footprints">
Not vivid recall—structural.  
Behavior, reference, factual  
  Shifts in decision-making:  
  That's residue taking  
Hold. Emotional: not actual.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="High-Intensity Feed Artifact" icon="zap">
Strong emotion, no behavioral trace  
After the next refresh's grace:  
  High-intensity feed  
  Artifact. The creed:  
The hurt is real. Not certified in space.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Some May Pass" icon="check-circle">
All three may pass. Or only one.  
Or none. The rubric isn't done  
  With classifying—  
  Just categorizing  
What the filed experience won.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Classification Not Belief" icon="folder">
The result determines class.  
Not whether feelings surpass  
  Or qualify as true.  
  DOGE doesn't construe  
What feelings mean. Just their mass.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Different Evidentiary Rules" icon="balance-scale">
Simulator classes aren't erased.  
They're preserved—differently placed  
  Under different rules  
  Of evidence. The tools:  
Different. The records: still traced.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Modest Bureaucracy" icon="building">
The rubric is not a high court.  
It is a modest-sized fort  
  For refusing to let  
  Graphs be the debt  
Of what a life's experience bought.  
</Limerick>
