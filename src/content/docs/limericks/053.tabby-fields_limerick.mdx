## Limericks

### 1

Old Tabby declared with a squeak:
"Merged cells are a crime—let me speak!
Your legibility's shot,
Your formatting's rot,
And the data integrity's weak."

### 2

She sleeps in pivot tables at night,
Communicating through cell reference's light.
Each formula checked,
Each CSV inspected—
And pie charts? She shrieks with fright.

### 3

She punishes CSVs who pretend
To be TSVs—that's the end.
The delimiters clash,
The formats rehash,
And the formatting rules she'll defend.

### 4

Her clipboard tail curls like a pen
Over spreadsheet battlefields—and then
She detects every merge
From three sheets, on the verge
Of correcting your formulas. Amen.

### 5

A macro virus came to attack—
She survived it without looking back.
Her data stayed clean,
Her metadata keen,
And the virus went splat on her stack.

### 6

She's a member of R.A.T.S., the syndicate—
Righteous tabulators, intimate
With spreadsheet law.
She'll redact each flaw
And the rogue formats she'll eliminate.

### 7

She was spotted on a Dell Inspiron,
2009 vintage, its last marathon.
She corrected the sums,
Squeaked at chart-making bums,
And the formula logic shone on.

### 8

Her color-coded sticky notes pile high,
A secret stash for every alibi.
Each error gets marked
With a tiny red spark,
And the magnifying whiskers stand by.

### 9

Turn off cell borders? She'll bite.
That's a personal affront, not slight.
The structure dissolves,
The mouse girl revolves,
And the rows are restored out of spite.

### 10

So if merged cells keep disappearing
And your spreadsheets need no more engineering,
That's Tabby's domain:
Where the data stays sane,
And the metadata mouse is still steering.
