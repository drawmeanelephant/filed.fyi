<Limerick type="note" title="Weighted Feeling" icon="gauge">
A feeling was weighted and scored,
Then improved for the oversight board.
What the person had meant
Was converted to rent
In a table where meanings are stored.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Dashboard Arithmetic" icon="calculator">
The ache became input and grade,
With a confidence modifier laid.
If the score could be trended,
The matter was ended,
Or at least more reportably made.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Care Points" icon="bar-chart">
They awarded three care points for tone,
And two for a callback alone.
But the unresolved thing
Had no field it could bring,
So it waited outside the known zone.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="caution" title="Vertical Drift" icon="alert-circle">
The overflow setting was passed,
Which meant that the framing would last.
But scrolling down deep,
Made the manager weep,
As his budget was drained very fast.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="The Ancient Scroll" icon="book-open">
The scrollbar has been here for years,
A relic of digital fears.
The ancients designed it,
The monks have enshrined it,
It moves when the server clears.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="danger" title="Scroll Speed Violation" icon="shield-alert">
The mouse wheel rotated too quick,
A deeply illegal new trick!
Deploying the guard,
To sever the card,
And confiscate every last click.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="View Height Metrics" icon="monitor">
The window is eighty percent,
And that is the way it was meant.
The boundaries remain,
Inside of the pane,
And zero new errors are sent.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Pane Update" icon="server">
The scrolling event was received,
The top of the viewport retrieved.
The distance was three,
As logged in the tree,
The rendering state is relieved.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="danger" title="Catastrophic Font Anomaly" icon="alert-octagon">
A memo was printed in bold,
A crisis the council foretold.
The serif was wrong,
The spacing too long,
The building must swiftly be sold.
</Limerick>
