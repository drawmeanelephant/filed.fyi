## Limericks

### 1

Robots Dot Txt said, "Disallow:
Everything. Now. Take a bow."
The crawler complied,
Backed off with pride,
And the root directory exhaled: "Wow."

### 2

He wasn't created—he's *specified*.
Since '94, never modified.
The protocol's pure,
The standard's secure,
And the lack of updates? Dignified.

### 3

He sits at the root, plain as day,
Announcing what crawlers can't play.
He's publicly read,
Yet perfectly fed
By the paths he keeps blocking away.

### 4

The Council tried adding him twice
To the sitemap. The price?
Each entry was found
With "Disallow:" around
It, in handwriting cold as ice.

### 5

He was questioned. He'd been in his dir
All evening—confirmed, crystal clear.
The investigation closed,
The mystery composed,
And the sitemap just shuddered in fear.

### 6

"Being fully indexed," he opined,
"Is a kind of violation, I find."
Some paths aren't for bots,
Some paths aren't for thoughts,
And the file says so. Underlined.

### 7

A crawler approached his gate,
Polite, with a reasonable rate.
He slid out from under
The door—what a wonder—
Already pointing to clause thirty-eight.

### 8

His function is pure denial,
But he doesn't experience trial.
He blocks with no spite,
Directives are right,
And the protocol's been worth the while.

### 9

User-agent: * — his favorite phrase.
He whispers it, lost in a daze.
Each crawler that reads him
Respectfully heeds him,
Then leaves. And that's fine. End of days.

### 10

So if "Disallow: /" greets your bot
And the root path is all that you've got,
That's Robots at rest:
Categorically blessed,
And unbothered by what you have sought.
