<Limerick type="note" title="Blue Screen With a Cursor" icon="monitor">
The machine rebooted and stopped—
A cursor moved. Loginwindow dropped
  Away from the chain.
  Beneath, in the vein:
APE had quietly self-adopted.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="DYLD_INSERT" icon="code">
It had written itself into boot
Via DYLD_INSERT, the mute
  And invisible thread
  That told each forked thread
Where to find the framework's loot.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Leopard Hardened" icon="shield">
Leopard hardened the login wall.
It didn't break APE—it called
  APE's bluff:
  "We've tightened. Enough.
Your assumptions no longer recall."
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Revealed, Not Broken" icon="eye">
From the archive's standpoint, the key:
Leopard didn't break APE. It let be
  Visible what
  The framework had shut
Into System Library, silently.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Boot Into Single-User" icon="terminal">
The prescription was spare and blunt:
Boot single-user. Then hunt
  The APE files down.
  Delete. Reboot. Crown
Of continuity: restored in front.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Every Lingering File" icon="file">
Every APE component left
Became another place—bereft
  Of invitation—
  Where Anlas' station
Remained. Refusing to be cleft.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Post-Mortem Autograph" icon="pen-tool">
Every crash log bearing his name—
Unsanity bundle IDs became
  His autograph, post-
  Mortem. The ghost
Persisted as text after the flame.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="No Plan for When the OS Hardened" icon="calendar-x">
"We shipped a daemon into /System
With no plan for when the system
  Stopped indulging him."
  The summary grim.
That's the Anlas problem's lesson.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Cultural Scapegoat" icon="crosshair">
Long after the files were gone,
Users blamed Unsanity's con
  For every weird glitch—
  "Old APE stuff," which
Became the archive's living dawn.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Anlas-Adjacent" icon="alert-triangle">
Any framework that inserts
Into boot-critical pathways—hurts
  When the OS hardens.
  The archive gardens
The question: when it fails, who gets the dirt?
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Bigger Tombstone" icon="bookmark">
The case is marked: deprecated, formally.
Informally invoked—normally—
  Whenever logs show
  Ghosts of frameworks below
/System. No forwarding address. Morally.
</Limerick>
