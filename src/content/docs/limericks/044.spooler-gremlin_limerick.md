## Limericks

### 1

The Gremlin held jobs for nine hours,
Then released them in twelve-copy showers.
The meeting was done,
The presentation won,
And the printer spat pages like flowers.

### 2

"It's in the queue," the Gremlin would say,
While examining your doc in its bay.
The formatting? Checked.
The urgency? Wrecked.
And the output arrived the next day.

### 3

He accepted a job—logged "Success!"
Yet produced nothing physical, nonetheless.
The status was green,
The tray wiped clean,
And the paper trail: anyone's guess.

### 4

He cleared the queue during an audit—
Each job marked "COMPLETED," so sod it.
The logs all agreed,
The compliance was freed,
And the Gremlin just quietly plodded.

### 5

He rattles the spool like a cage,
Confirming your jobs page by page.
Each document held,
Each printout repelled,
Until hope reaches a critical stage.

### 6

A single page from a long-held report
Arrives at intervals—cruel little sport.
Just enough to maintain
Your optimistic refrain,
While the rest of the job stays in court.

### 7

He catalogs jobs by file type
And emotional urgency—ripe
For a naturalist's eye,
A loupe held up high,
And a verdict: "Not printing tonight."

### 8

Late workers find documents waiting—
Pristine prints from weeks of debating.
The toner smells fresh,
The pages enmesh,
And the Gremlin's been quietly curating.

### 9

He sits behind the second printer,
Third floor—the one that's a splinter
In IT's whole plan.
It says "Out of Paper," but can
Somehow print all through winter.

### 10

So if "Print Complete" means nothing at all
And the output tray's empty each fall,
That's the Gremlin's domain:
Where the queue is humane,
And the printing responds to no call.
