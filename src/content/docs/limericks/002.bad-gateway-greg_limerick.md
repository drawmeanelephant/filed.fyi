## Limericks

### 1

Bad Gateway Greg plugged it in wrong—
The cable, the port, and the prong.
He said, "Oops, my bad,"
To the server he had,
Then stayed connected all night long.

### 2

A proxy said, "Greg, you're too wet."
He said, "That's the dampest I'll get."
He fumbled the route,
Went the wrong way throughout,
And still called it the best handshake yet.

### 3

Greg handles his packets with care—
He fondles each one that's laid bare.
The headers get stripped,
The payload gets flipped,
And the ACK ends up God-knows-where.

### 4

His routing was famously loose:
Every socket he touched would produce
A five-oh-two scream,
A wet data stream,
And a server that's begging for truce.

### 5

"I got the message," Greg said with a wink,
"But I sent it straight into the drink."
The relay was hot,
The handshake was not,
And the whole chain went down in a blink.

### 6

He tried to apologize clean
But his sorry got stuck in between
Two proxies in heat,
Tangled up in a sheet
Of the dampest HTTP scene.

### 7

The server room flooded that night—
Greg's cables were twisted up tight.
He said, "I'll connect,"
With a dubious erect-
ion of router antennae. All right.

### 8

Greg's uptime was three minutes flat
Before every port where he sat
Got soaking and sore,
Returned 502 more,
And the load balancer called him a brat.

### 9

They told him, "Just pass the request."
He fondled it, sniffed it, caressed
The header with tongue,
Then the whole session hung,
And the client was deeply distressed.

### 10

So if signals arrive slightly damp
And the error log smells like a swamp,
That's Greg in the pipe,
Getting handsy with hype,
While the gateway flickers its lamp.
