## Limericks

### 1

Old Hexley once indexed a sneeze
And tagged it with absolute ease.
The query returned
What nobody yearned:
Result zero of four-oh-four, please.

### 2

He indexed his own indexing,
Which started a recursive thing.
The results about results
Reached archival catapults,
And the metadata started to sing.

### 3

His card catalog, dusty and wide,
Was sorted by estimated pride.
Each entry recalled
Something lost, something stalled,
And a query that whimpered inside.

### 4

He tagged four hundred entries as "misc"
During stress tests of emotional risk.
Reclassification
Required oblation
And a full reindex done with a whisk.

### 5

A document didn't exist—
But Hexley had filed it, insist.
He spent six long weeks
Searching corridors and creeks
For a record that couldn't be kissed.

### 6

He reindexes when he feels slighted,
Which means he's perpetually excited.
The breadcrumb trails loop,
The results are a scoop
Of metadata, poorly requited.

### 7

He whispered a search term at night
To nobody's query delight.
The index returned
What no soul had yearned,
And the card catalog flickered its light.

### 8

Kindy tried to be cataloged twice.
Hexley said, "Your metadata's nice
But your emotional schema
Is too much of a dilemma.
Denied. Now hold still while I splice."

### 9

His feelings from 2008 remained
Unprocessed, unqueried, unstrained.
They sat in the logs,
Like emotional dogs,
Waiting to be searched and explained.

### 10

So if "Did You Mean: Everything" shows
And the breadcrumbs loop back to the rows,
That's Hexley's domain:
Where the search was in vain,
And Result 0 is all that he knows.
