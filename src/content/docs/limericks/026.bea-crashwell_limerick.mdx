## Limericks

### 1

Bea booted in ten seconds flat,
Then vanished—imagine that.
She rendered one frame,
Achieved eternal fame,
And nobody captured the stat.

### 2

She played 4K in ninety-seven
On hardware that shouldn't reach heaven.
The demo ran clean,
The fastest you'd seen,
Then the BeBox died at eleven.

### 3

Her screenshots were always just blank.
The shutter was one frame too wank.
She'd been and she'd gone
In the time of a yawn,
And the evidence walked the plank.

### 4

At 3 a.m. on PowerPC
She appeared—briefly, perfectly free.
One frame of delight,
Then dissolved into night,
And the monitor wept silently.

### 5

She crashed so gracefully, they say,
The dialog became a display
Of collector's-grade charm,
A beautifully harmed
Notification of passing away.

### 6

Her startup chime still haunts the air
In frequencies modern cards can't bear.
The former users weep,
Wake from perfect-UI sleep,
With no proof that she was ever there.

### 7

The Council filed her as "displaced"—
Not dead, just temporally misplaced.
She exists in the past,
Where performance ran fast,
And the present can't keep up the pace.

### 8

A trade show in '97 saw
The fastest boot without a flaw.
Eleven minutes played,
Then she was mislaid,
And the machine never booted. The awe.

### 9

"Forgotten in nine," was her line.
The speed was impossibly fine.
She rendered, she blazed,
The audience gazed,
Then she vanished like last decade's wine.

### 10

So if you wake sure that you've seen
The most responsive thing onscreen
But the proof isn't there—
Just warmth in the air—
That's Bea. The fastest ghost queen.
