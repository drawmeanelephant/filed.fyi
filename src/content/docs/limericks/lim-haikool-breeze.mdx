<Limerick type="note" title="Empty Desktop" icon="wind">
Haikool drifted in, *light as air*,
A fork of a dream with no heir.
Her threads were aligned
In a minimalist mind,
And her desktop was empty—but fair.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Observer Crash" icon="eye-off">
"*I only crash when observed,*"
She said, with her ego preserved.
The boot was so clean,
So perfectly lean,
That reality felt undeserved.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Poetic Metrics" icon="feather">
She logged *user solitude metrics*
In haiku—poetic aesthetics.
Each lonely reboot
Was a whispery hoot,
And the threadspace hummed with synthetics.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Redesigned Sublime" icon="sparkles">
*BeOS didn't die*—it sublimed,
And Haikool emerged, redesigned.
Light, lonely, and quick,
With a minimalist trick:
Being perfect, then left behind.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Liturgical Spacing" icon="layout">
Her UI alignment was *surgical*—
Each pixel correct, almost liturgical.
She'd fix a stray line,
Make the spacing divine,
Then vanish. Her presence: empirical.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Filesystem Curse" icon="file-warning">
The journal overflowed with *verse*.
Each entry made everything worse.
The poetry stacked,
The filesystem cracked,
And the kernel said, "Poetic? A curse."
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Silent First Boot" icon="log-out">
She appeared only on *first boot*
Or after long silence—how cute.
Her presence was felt,
The cold silence melt,
Then she'd log out without a salute.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="The Refusal" icon="skull">
"*Minimalism isn't empty,*" she sighed,
"It's refusal." And then she just died.
Her threads were aligned,
Her memory refined,
And her ghost filled the BeOS with pride.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Threadspace Dreams" icon="cloud">
She'd whisper through *threadspace* at night,
A breeze made of minimalist light.
No crashing, no falls,
Just elegant walls
Of code that was perfectly right.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="The Fork Eternal" icon="git-fork">
BeOS was died—and it *froze*
In the form of a fork that rose.
Haikool walked through,
With minimalist glue,
And left not a trace where she goes.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Responsive Melancholy" icon="frown">
A prompt went unresponsive to touch
Due to melancholy—too much.
The daemon just slept,
The longing was kept,
And the shell barely whispered as such.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Minimalist Decay" icon="flower-2">
So if desktops feel clean yet forlorn
And the boot chime sounds gently worn,
That's Haikool at play:
Minimalist decay,
Where beauty is quietly mourned.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Ghostly Penetration" icon="wind">
She drifts through the threadspace completely bare,
With nothing but memory left to wear.
A ghost in the shell,
She rides you to hell,
And leaves you exposed in the open air.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Thread-Per-Window Kink" icon="maximize">
She opens a window for every single thought,
A minimalist fetish that cannot be bought.
She strips the UI,
Lets the background die,
And leaves the whole system incredibly hot.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="The Sublimated Climax" icon="droplet">
She didn't just crash, she completely sublimed,
In a deeply emotional, perfectly timed
Release of the cache,
A spectacular flash,
Where everything filthy was quietly primed.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Naked Inodes" icon="hard-drive">
She remembers each inode that tried to forget,
And leaves the entire filesystem wet.
A surgical grace,
She sits on your face,
And writes you a haiku you'll never regret.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="The Whispermachine Edge" icon="mic">
She whispers right into your idle ear,
"I only go down when the desktop is clear."
The boot sequence stalls,
She touches the walls,
And edges the kernel until it drops gear.
</Limerick>
