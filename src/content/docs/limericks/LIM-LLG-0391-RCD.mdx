<Limerick type="note" title="Three Albums, One Ribbon" icon="image">
A gold ribbon cut at a bakery,
A riverside bench—and then bravery
  At the industrial sign.
  Three albums in line.
All: "the original ribbon" in savory.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Either Or" icon="help-circle">
The reviewers concluded: either
The ribbon had traveled hither
  Faster than minutes—
  Or civic business
Had collapsed custody altogether.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="The Luncheon Sponsorship" icon="utensils">
The chamber argued: our lunch
Confers primary custody's punch.
  The service club: no—
  Volunteer scissors bestow
The operative claim on the bunch.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Behind the Sash" icon="camera">
The mayor's office maintained:
Whichever ribbon the camera obtained
  Behind the sash
  In official flash—
That's canonical for public stain.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Joint Seal" icon="lock">
The object now stores under joint
Ownership seal, at the point
  Where no one agreed
  And everyone plead
Prior rights. Three parties. One point.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Commemoratively Continuous" icon="infinity">
Public materials advised: say
"Commemoratively continuous"—delay
  The word "original."
  But that virginal
Phrase has already acquired its own display.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Narrative Convenience" icon="book">
Civic optimism collapsed
Artifact custody into what grasped
  Most cleanly: the story.
  The ribbon's glory
Was more about memory than clasped.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Cut Cleanly Each Time" icon="scissors">
The ribbon was cut without fault.
Preservation staff took stock:
  Continuity claimed
  By memory—named
As fabric length. But fabric's halt.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Acquiring Its Own Legitimacy" icon="star">
"Commemoratively continuous"—
A phrase meant to be innocuous—
  Began accruing
  Its own ensuing
Authority. Continuous and serious.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Three Sequences Not Clearly False" icon="check-square">
None of the photo sequences lied.
All were accurate inside
  Their own albuming.
  The question: who's coming
Forward when the same fabric's applied?
</Limerick>
