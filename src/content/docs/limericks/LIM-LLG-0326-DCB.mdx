<Limerick type="note" title="Stronger All Round" icon="refresh-cw">
COMA said, "Continuity's sound."  
SOMA heard "emotional ground."  
The citation format  
Blurred this into that,  
And both filings got stronger all round.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Incompatible Records Glow" icon="sparkles">
Affirmations of uptime and flow  
Turned to mitigation, somehow.  
If the markers align,  
Then the channels entwine,  
And incompatible records can glow.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Footnote Seduction" icon="book-open">
This is not what a footnote should do.  
It should tell you what evidence grew.  
Not seduce two regimes  
Into swapping their themes  
Till one claim starts proving the other is true.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Conflicting Wills" icon="alert-triangle">
Formatting symmetry kills.  
It can make cross-directive thrills  
Out of nothing but style,  
So a claim once worth while  
Now reinforces conflicting wills.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Citations Kissed" icon="heart">
The same interval, same compliance tags,  
Made the systems drop both their flags.  
Once the citations kissed,  
Every doctrine insisted  
The other endorsed its old nags.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Tangled in Air" icon="cloud">
Continuity should not equal care.  
Care should not be implied by a flare  
Of equivalent code  
In a metadata node.  
Yet here we are, tangled in air.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Contamination Gets Right" icon="shield-alert">
The records looked elegant, tight.  
That's how contamination gets right  
Through the guard at the gate:  
Not with chaos, but straight  
Little format decisions at night.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Grim Art" icon="palette">
A filing could now self-reinforce  
With mutually hostile discourse.  
That's the kind of grim art  
Only systems at heart  
Can produce without feeling remorse.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Categories No Longer Endure" icon="ghost">
One clerk muttered, "This feels impure."  
The parser replied, "No, secure.  
The markers all match."  
That's a hell of a catch  
When your categories no longer endure.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Citation Scar" icon="zap">
So if COMA starts soothing your pain,  
Or SOMA starts praising your strain,  
Check the citation scar.  
Cross-bleed doesn't go far  
Before both sides begin to feign.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Rubbed 'Til They Bled" icon="flame">
The citations were rubbed 'til they bled,  
Into metadata layers instead.  
   The contamination was deep,  
   While the auditors sleep,  
And the doctrine was left for the dead.  
</Limerick>
