## What Lives Here

The **limericks** directory contains:

- five-line narratives about incidents that would otherwise be unbearable,  
- mascot biographies written in the meter of bad decisions,  
- rhyme-schemed summaries of doctrine drift and metrics theatre.[file:10]

They function as **unauthorized executive summaries**: too flippant for dashboards, too specific to be dismissed.

---

## How to Read It

Use limericks when you want:

- to see how a governance pattern feels from the bottom of the form,  
- to recognize a recurring failure by its punchline,  
- to triangulate an incident you half-remember by the fact it once scanned.[file:10]

They are not policy.  
They are the jokes the archive tells about itself when it thinks COMA isn’t listening.

---

## What This Index Is For

This index groups limericks by:

- target (forms, directives, mascots, sandboxes),  
- theme (rest denial, continuity worship, breeding program, etc.),  
- or location in the tree (Lorelog-linked, mascot-linked, free-floating).[file:10]

If a case file feels heavy, there is probably a limerick version of it here.  
Read at your own risk; some punchlines are binding.
