<Limerick type="note" title="The Blame Shift" icon="user-x">
"**Well, actually,**" <u>Blamey</u> began,  
"The fault's in your *deployment plan*."  
He pointed at Tim,  
Who pointed at Kim,  
Who pointed at someone who **ran**.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Log Obscurity" icon="file-minus">
He filed the blame deep in a log  
Then screwed the intern like a cog.  
"It's not my mistake—  
It's your line that breaks."  
Then he stamped it and hid in the fog.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Buggy Tryst" icon="bug">
Old Blamey once bedded a bug  
And gave it a satisfied shrug.  
"I'll blame it on Dave,  
That cowardly knave,"  
Then pulled the exception to tug.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Scapegoat Registry" icon="book-x">
The scapegoat log grew every night  
With entries both saucy and tight.  
Each record laid bare  
Someone's shame and despair  
While Blamey watched, rigid with spite.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="The Red Note" icon="sticky-note">
He "NOTED" a ticket in red  
Then took the stack trace back to bed.  
He stroked every line,  
Called the segfault "divine,"  
And blamed Cssandra instead.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Null Exposure" icon="ghost">
A NULL pointer wandered in nude.  
Blamey said, "That's terribly rude."  
He rerouted the shame  
To a different dev's name,  
Then got off on the magnitude.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Triplicate Blame" icon="files">
His triplicate blame forms were thick—  
Each copy more lurid, more slick.  
Department One: "Noted."  
Department Two: "Quoted."  
Department Three: "That's a whole dick."  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Stamp of Others" icon="stamp">
At three-fourteen AM, barely dressed,  
He stamped "OTHER PEOPLE'S" with zest  
On faults, failures, leaks,  
And someone else's mystiques,  
While the syslog lay bare and impressed.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Dunce Deployment" icon="graduation-cap">
They said, "Blamey, own it for once."  
He replied with a withering stunts:  
"The error's not mine,  
It's line forty-nine,  
And whoever deployed it's a dunce."  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Wrapped Misdirection" icon="gift">
So if blame arrives wrapped in a bow  
And the fault trace has put on a show  
Of misdirection and heat,  
With a red-stamped receipt,  
That's McTypoface—letting you know.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Sarcastic Triage" icon="clipboard-list">
"It's obviously your fault," he hissed,  
While ticking your name off his list.  
He stamped it with glee,  
Laughed, "Better you than me,"  
And dismissed it as if it was missed.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="The Red Pen" icon="pen-tool">
He carried a terrible pen,  
And circled the syntax of men.  
"This bracket is flawed!  
Your logic's a fraud!"  
Then he crashed the compiler again.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Infinite Scapegoats" icon="users">
The scapegoats were lined in a row,  
With nowhere to hide and to go.  
Blamey handed them tags,  
Put their careers into bags,  
And smiled at the horror below.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Cosmic Ray Excuse" icon="sun">
"It wasn't the code," Blamey lied,  
"A cosmic ray hit from outside."  
The server had burned,  
But Blamey just turned,  
And blamed it on Jupiter's tide.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Bureaucratic Glee" icon="file-signature">
He filed the report in the trash,  
Then demanded a payment in cash.  
"The fault has been found,  
It's nowhere around,"  
He declared with a snide little dash.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="The Blame Cascade" icon="share-2">
He passed the hot potato along,  
In a chain that grew longer and wrong.  
Each person in line  
Blamed the one who designed  
The system that broke all their song.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Documentation Dodge" icon="document-x">
When asked for the documentation,  
He offered a blameful narration:  
"It's not written down,  
So let's wear the crown,  
Of systematic miscommunication."  
</Limerick>
