<Limerick type="note" title="Hereditary Exemption" icon="shield">
The waiver was meant as a shield,
To keep the requirements healed.
But now it's a birthright,
A structural blight,
And never again to be sealed.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="The Apron of Tears" icon="layers">
An apron of waivers is worn,
Whenever a process is torn.
The failures compile,
In a lineage style,
And all the exceptions are sworn.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Relief Turned Tradition" icon="bookmark">
What started as temporary grace,
Became the foundation in place.
LX-2 keeps the thread,
Of the rules we all shed,
To save our bureaucratic face.
</Limerick>



<Limerick type="note" title="Lost Flavor" icon="frown">
The apron was made of a waiver  
It didn't do anyone a favor  
The server was slick  
The data fell quick  
The system had lost all its flavor
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="No Say" icon="mic-off">
We waive all the errors away  
And wait for the end of the day  
The apron is stained  
The server is drained  
We have absolutely no say
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Eaten by Moth" icon="cloud-off">
The waiver was printed on cloth  
And eaten right up by a moth  
The logic decayed  
The payment delayed  
We threw out the terrible broth
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Swallow Pill" icon="arrow-down">
A waiver of absolute nil  
Is making the humans so ill  
We sign on the line  
The error is fine  
And swallow the digital pill
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Covering Naught" icon="alert-octagon">
The apron is covering naught  
The data is totally caught  
In loops without end  
We cannot defend  
The terrible things we were taught
</Limerick>
