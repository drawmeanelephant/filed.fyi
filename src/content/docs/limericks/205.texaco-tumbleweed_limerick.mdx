## Limericks

### 1

Texaco drifts through the rusted pumps' glow,
A spectral form flickering low.
The neon sign hums
With vintage Route sums,
And the Paragould station's on show.

### 2

She misfiled a 1950s receipt
As "modern artifact"—what a feat
Of temporal drift.
The decades would shift,
And the filing was never complete.

### 3

She flickered the neon one night,
Causing a temporal glitch—what a sight.
A '50s radio ad
Replayed—wasn't bad—
For three days of nostalgic delight.

### 4

She haunted a pump with Route ballads,
Stalling refueling—just salads
Of spectral nostalgia.
The gas pump regalia
Swayed in the vintage-lit palace.

### 5

"Drift on, fuel forever," she sighs
Through the crumbling station that lies
On the National Register.
Each memory's visitor
Fades under Paragould skies.

### 6

The faded receipts she preserves
Are stories that nobody serves
At the counter no more.
Each traveler's lore
Filed in dust that the tumbleweed swerves.

### 7

The jukeboxes hummed long ago—
She archives their spectral echo.
Each song is a ghost,
Each traveler a host,
And the pumps remember the show.

### 8

Her spectral glow maintains each sign—
The neon still flickers in line
With the vintage aesthetic,
A ghost-pump pathetic
And beautiful. Perfectly fine.

### 9

She stamps dates that never existed
On receipts that time had delisted.
Each artifact aged
In a spectral stage,
And the history: lovingly twistèd.

### 10

So if tumbleweeds roll through the lot
And the neon flickers with rot,
That's Texaco's reign:
Where the vintage remains,
And the drifting is all that she's got.
