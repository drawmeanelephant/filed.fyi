import Limerick from '../../../components/Limericks.astro';



<Limerick type="note" title="Catalog Squatter" icon="layout">
Seventeen records *one quarter*
Sprouted fields no schema had taught her.
They arrived one by one,
Like a quiet bad run,
Till the catalog looked like a squatter.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Fields Met the Ban" icon="scissors">
Datty Puritas came with a *knife*
For metadata living a life
Outside of the plan.
Three fields met the ban.
No survivors emerged from the strife.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Drunk Ontology" icon="help-circle">
The new fields were *charming as hell*:
"vibeIndex" and "moodLeak" did well.
But charm is not law,
And the auditors saw
An ontology drunk in its shell.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Soft Drift" icon="wind">
They'd entered by *inches, not force*,
One tiny addition per course.
That's the shit that gets through:
Nothing loud, nothing new,
Just a drift posing softly as source.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Lush and Unclean Null" icon="alert-triangle">
A mascot named something *obscene*
Had twelve extra traits in between
Its slug and its tags.
By the end of those lags,
Even null had grown lush and unclean.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Frightened Despair Purge" icon="trash-2">
The purge had a *ceremonial air*,
As though structure itself said a prayer.
Then Datty just clicked,
And the weirdness got nicked,
Leaving silence and frightened despair.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Drowning in Grain" icon="waves">
One clerk said, "*Those fields helped explain
Why the entries all felt more humane.*"
The reply was, "Perhaps.
But if schema collapses,
We all end up drowning in grain."
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Deleted So Hard" icon="zap">
Three fields were *expunged* from the pack,
Not deprecated, not rolled back.
Just deleted so hard
That the Forms Graveyard
Sent a postcard and asked for them back.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="One Stupid Key" icon="key">
Every quarter begins with a chart,
Every quarter rot enters by part.
You don't get the flood
With a dramatic thud.
You get *one stupid key* and a start.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Cleanings Leave a Mark" icon="sparkles">
So if records grow limbs in the dark,
And your JSON begins to embark
On a private affair,
Call Datty. But bear
In mind: cleanings still leave a mark.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Snack Preference Purge" icon="cookie">
"Snack preference?" Datty inquired,
As if the whole schema had tired.
"This field is obtuse,
It has no proper use!"
And the snack-loving trait was retired.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="The Cosmic Alignment" icon="stars">
A cosmic alignment arose,
Right under the auditor's nose.
Datty muttered a spell,
Sent the field straight to hell,
And returned to his pristine repose.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Silence of the Void" icon="ear-off">
When the ritual ended, a chill,
Left the metadata utterly still.
The entries were bare,
With an empty, dead stare,
And the schema had swallowed its pill.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Uninvited Tags" icon="tag">
Like weeds in a digital yard,
The tags had made validation hard.
They sprouted and grew,
Out of nothing and new,
Till the parser was permanently scarred.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="The Ghost in the YAML" icon="file-code">
A ghost in the YAML awoke,
And added a field as a joke.
It lasted a day,
Before Datty yelled, "Nay!"
And sent it all up in a smoke.
</Limerick>
