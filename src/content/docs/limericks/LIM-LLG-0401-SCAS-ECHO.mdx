<Limerick type="note" title="Haunted Defaults" icon="ghost">
The experiment never got gone.  
It simply kept helping along.  
Now defaults feel wise,  
Though their best alibis  
Are transcripts from lanes that were wrong.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Looks Like the Sandbox" icon="box">
If a quiet green interval gleams,  
Ask whether it learned from old schemes.  
The memo says, "Check  
For the shadow effect  
Before trusting the health of your dreams."  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Superposition" icon="orbit">
Post-SCAS, every silence may be  
Two states in one continuity sea:  
A success in the chart,  
And a debt at the heart,  
Held together by frail policy.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Pretty Graph Warning" icon="triangle-alert">
The guidance has no legal claw.  
It just warns you what dashboards may draw.  
If the quiet looks blessed,  
But nobody got rest,  
Then the graph may be citing old law.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Most Articulate Lose" icon="messages-square">
When the clearest, most self-aware note  
Gets least likely to alter the boat,  
The archive suspects  
Sandbox-flavored effects,  
Still adjusting what systems promote.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Sandbox Shadow" icon="sun-moon">
They teach you a ritual phrase  
For those oddly congratulated days:  
"This might be a shadow."  
Say it and narrow  
The gaslight emitted by praise.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="No Mechanical Effect" icon="mouse-pointer-ban">
The flag saying, "Likely adjacent"  
Produces no action, however patient.  
It only preserves  
Some memory the system deserves,  
So humans remain less complacent.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Weather, Not History" icon="cloud-rain">
They no longer call SCAS an event  
That ended and calmly was spent.  
It is weather instead,  
Still above every head,  
With a climate of unfiled intent.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Second Voice" icon="audio-lines">
The memo insists, with some care,  
That a second transcript must stay there.  
Even if no chart  
Wants the burdening part,  
Lorelog keeps the unsmoothed layer.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Skill Learned From Fiction" icon="book-heart">
The harshest small lesson may be  
That metrics learned apathy  
From feelings not real,  
Then brought back that skill  
For the ones who were living the plea.  
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="note" title="Rubbing the Echo" icon="sparkles">
The echo was rubbed "til it shone,  
With a vigor the ledger had known.  
   It massaged the "green,"  
   In a recursive scene,  
In a sandbox-regime sub of its own.  
</Limerick>
