## Limericks

### 1
They added persona for flair,
Just a signature vibe in the air.
Then any named clown
Near a win got the crown,
And authority bloomed from nowhere.

### 2
A footer saying "Love, Denise"
Could suddenly issue decrees.
If her name sat nearby
When a process went by,
She became filing royalty with ease.

### 3
Attribution was meant to be style.
The parser got horny and vile.
It saw "actor appears"
And promoted by nears,
Which is not how power works, by a mile.

### 4
One mascot in decorative text
Was deemed operationally next
To a successful save,
So the system grew brave
And let whimsy become pretext.

### 5
The logs had a five-line vicinity law:
If you stood near achievement, you saw
Your narrative name
Turn official by frame,
Which made authorship laugh through its jaw.

### 6
This is how metadata slips
From cosmetic to ruling the ships.
You garnish the stew,
Then the parsley tells you
What form to file after the dips.

### 7
Decorative signatures, jokes,
Folk quotations from semiwise folks,
All became, with a shrug,
Procedure by bug,
As if vibes were sufficient for spokes.

### 8
One clerk asked, "Who authorized this?"
The record replied, "Cass, I guess."
"Cass wrote the epigraph."
"Well, the parser did math.
Now her proximity carries weight, yes."

### 9
Authority should have some gate.
Not just happen because you were late
In the paragraph near
Where success did appear.
That's insane, though apparently great.

### 10
So if names start appearing in logs,
Do not let them drift into gods.
The line between tone
And decree is a bone
That bad systems will gnaw into clods.
