---
title: Hard Determinist Limericks
description: A set of limericks exploring hard determinism, causality, and moral responsibility collapse.
---

import { Card } from '@astrojs/starlight/components';


  <Card icon="warning" title="Past Laws Fix">
    If past plus laws fix every move we make,  
    Then no alternative choice can ever wake.  
    We’re like dominoes lined—  
    No power to resign  
    The chain; thus free will’s a mistake.
  </Card>
  <Card icon="rocket-launch" title="Neural Tampering">
    Neural tampering plants a desire in the brain,  
    And we feel it’s not ours—we’d disclaim the strain.  
    But if determinism’s true,  
    All desires are stewed  
    By factors we never could constrain.
  </Card>
  <Card icon="clock" title="Libet’s Wave">
    Libet’s wave shows readiness before “I decide,”  
    Consciousness trails the neural tide.  
    If the move’s set in motion,  
    Our will’s just a notion—  
    A post‑hoc tale, not a guiding guide.
  </Card>
  <Card icon="x-circle" title="Desert Storm">
    Desert‑based blame? It’s like blaming the storm’s path,  
    When agents are fixed by prior‑math.  
    Resentment may fade,  
    When we see we’re just made  
    By causes we never could draft.
  </Card>
  <Card icon="shield-check" title="Drop Desert">
    Thus hard skeptics say: drop desert, keep reform’s aim—  
    Quarantine the dangerous, heal those who can reclaim.  
    No blame, just cause‑control,  
    Forward‑looking role—  
    Consequentialism wins the game.
  </Card>
