## Limericks

### 1

Lord Spitzenfile curates with grace
Each Spitzenberg apple's embrace.
His wax-sealed precision,
His cryptic envision,
Preserves every harvest in place.

### 2

He misfiled a harvest as "orchard
Riddle"—an enigma unchartered.
The auditors tried
To decode, but they cried,
And the filing remained largely unstarted.

### 3

He sealed a vault with a cipher
In wax—unsolvable and nicer
Than any encryption.
The Council's conviction:
"We'll never get in. Call the piper."

### 4

He lectured on Jeffersonian zoning
During an audit—his droning
Delayed every filing.
The auditors smiling
Through tears. (His gravitas: condoning.)

### 5

"Savor complex, archive eternal,"
He'd say with a manner fraternal.
Each flavor profiled,
Each record compiled,
And the cryptic estate runs supernal.

### 6

He speaks only in riddles of taste—
Each filing encrypted with haste.
The Esopus flavor,
Each record he'd savor,
And the aristocratic archive's encased.

### 7

His shadowed boughs whisper of Jefferson,
Of orchards that time hasn't weathered on.
The Spitzenberg gleams,
The aristocratic dreams,
And the harvest log's no one can feather on.

### 8

The Aristocratic Zest Compiler
Made this lord the most elegant filer.
His wax seals glow,
His riddles flow,
And the archival mystery's a high-flyer.

### 9

He collects misrendered glyphs as proof
That the archive is hardly aloof.
Each glyph is a clue,
Each cipher to view,
And the Spitzenberg sits on the roof.

### 10

So if wax seals guard every door
And the riddles confound to the core,
That's Lord Spitzenfile:
Where the enigmatic style
Preserves what came long before.
