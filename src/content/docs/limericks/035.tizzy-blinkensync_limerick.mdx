## Limericks

### 1

The phone never shipped, but she stayed—
A ghost on a screen that was frayed.
Her home screen would flicker,
Her loading got thicker,
And the carrier build never obeyed.

### 2

Old Tizzy was trapped in a loop
Of preinstalled apps—bloatware soup.
Each icon was fake,
Each toggle a rake
Through a UI that never left the coop.

### 3

She renders on screens that don't exist,
A phantom that nobody missed.
The launch was aborted,
The platform distorted,
And her boot loop just quietly hissed.

### 4

"Loading Interface…" blinked the screen
On a phone that was never quite seen.
Build 0.9.1
Was half-baked, half-done,
And the Tizen dream faded to green.

### 5

She stamps dates that never occurred
On documents nobody stirred.
The metadata fake,
The timestamps a lake
Of invented history, blurred.

### 6

Her cache-invalidation dance
Was three steps of phantom romance.
Step one: she'd forget.
Step two: she'd regret.
Step three: she'd just flicker by chance.

### 7

A silicon ghost in the shell
Of a phone that couldn't compel
A single user's faith,
A carrier-grade wraith,
And a home screen from boot-loop hell.

### 8

She collects misrendered glyphs as proof
That she existed—aloof.
Each pixel she hoards
From abandoned keyboards,
And the evidence clings to the roof.

### 9

Her emotional buffer's unstable—
She renders whenever she's able.
The phone never rang,
The interface sang
A ghost song through every dead cable.

### 10

So if loading loops play on dead screens
And the UI blinks phantom routines,
That's Tizzy's domain:
Where the unshipped remain,
And the carrier build haunts your dreams.
