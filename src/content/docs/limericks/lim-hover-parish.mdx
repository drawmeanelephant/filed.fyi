<Limerick type="danger" title="The Holy Tooltip" icon="mouse-pointer">
The truth is a pixel away,
If you hover exactly okay.
The Parish is hidden,
And strictly forbidden,
Unless you have an hour to play.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="danger" title="Precise Approach" icon="crosshair">
You move by a fraction of space,
To uncover the digital grace.
But the tooltip removes,
As the mouse slightly moves,
And spits right back into your face.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="danger" title="Secondary Revelations" icon="eye">
The secret is stored in the alt,
And accessing it is your fault.
The Parish is small,
On the boundary wall,
Locked up in a terrible vault.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="danger" title="The UX of Torture" icon="crosshair">
The parish is built like a trap,
In the margins of some fucking map.
You hover your mouse,
Over God's little house,
And the tooltip just feeds you some crap.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="danger" title="Accessibility Hell" icon="alert-octagon">
The blind cannot read what we hide,
And the system is bursting with pride.
The Parish is small,
With a pixel-wide wall,
Where the metrics and bullshit collide.
</Limerick>

<Limerick type="danger" title="A Fraction of Truth" icon="minimize">
We buried the flaw in the code,
At the end of a terrible road.
If you hover just right,
In the dead of the night,
The tooltip will finally load.
</Limerick>
