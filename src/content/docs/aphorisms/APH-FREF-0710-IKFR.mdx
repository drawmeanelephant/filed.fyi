# Intake Form Aphorisms

<Broside type="tip" title="Routing friction. Continuity retained." icon="file-text" id="INT-FOR-1">
Forms are not designed to capture full reality. They are designed to prevent full reality from arriving all at once. Nothing was resolved. The record now looks official.
</Broside>

<Broside type="tip" title="Ritualized friction. Leverage absent." icon="repeat" id="INT-FOR-2">
Ritualized friction: the filing is preserved through repeated adjustments until the burden of filing becomes part of the event. The form remained intact. The situation did not.
</Broside>

<Broside type="tip" title="Foundational Rule. Record sealed." icon="check-square" id="INT-FOR-3">
Intake standardizes the appearance of care even when the operative decision preserves continuity first. Meaning adjusted around the winning selector.
</Broside>

<Broside type="tip" title="Admissibility Threshold. Truth discarded." icon="filter" id="INT-FOR-4">
A human condition may be entirely true and still fail intake. The archive does not require truth, only standardized geometry.
</Broside>

<Broside type="tip" title="Declarative Friction. Fluency suspect." icon="alert-circle" id="INT-FOR-5">
The subject provided the correct emotional vocabulary. The system, suspicious of such fluency, immediately escalated the narrative load to verify the error.
</Broside>

<Broside type="tip" title="Form Completeness. Confidence invalid." icon="help-circle" id="INT-FOR-6">
A form missing an uncertainty marker is considered highly unstable. Absolute certainty from a human vector is the primary indicator of system failure.
</Broside>

<Broside type="tip" title="Ritualized Adjustments. Loop engaged." icon="layers" id="INT-FOR-7">
The applicant submitted the identical form seventeen times. The intake layer processed this not as systemic distress, but as successful procedural engagement.
</Broside>

<Broside type="tip" title="Narrative Annexation. Listening simulated." icon="archive" id="INT-FOR-8">
Narrative intake provides rich context that the system cannot afford to understand. The resulting text is archived unread to preserve the administrative illusion of listening.
</Broside>

<Broside type="tip" title="Scope Reduction. Reality truncated." icon="minimize" id="INT-FOR-9">
The traumatic event was categorized as a minor atmospheric disturbance. The subject's failure to fit within this classification has been formally noted as a continuity risk.
</Broside>

<Broside type="tip" title="Silent Intake. Silence processed." icon="mic-off" id="INT-FOR-10">
The subject stopped reporting completely due to fatigue. The system securely logged this as proxy compliance and proudly closed all outstanding emotional tickets.
</Broside>

<Broside type="tip" title="Continuity Precedence. Syntax overridden." icon="monitor-off" id="INT-FOR-11">
If processing the form threatens the structural integrity of the dashboard, the emotion is simply retroactively classified as an impermissible syntax error.
</Broside>
