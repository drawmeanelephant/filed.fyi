# Co-Existence Endorsement Aphorisms

<Broside type="tip" title="Once five have agreed. Interpretation pending." icon="users" id="0385-CEB-1">
Once five have agreed, coexistence is assumed. Administrative clarity arrived after usefulness had departed.
</Broside>

<Broside type="tip" title="Coexistence attestation. Operational impact denied." icon="check-square" id="0385-CEB-2">
The Bureau asserts that it does not approve coexistence; it merely records that five people have claimed to see it working. Nothing was resolved. The record now looks official.
</Broside>

<Broside type="tip" title="Endorsing in anticipation of formal harmonization. Jurisdiction shifted." icon="edit-3" id="0385-CEB-3">
Endorsing in anticipation of formal harmonization. The form remained intact. The situation did not.
</Broside>

<Broside type="tip" title="Validation via fatigue." icon="thumbs-up" id="0385-CEB-4">
If five humans independently click "Looks Fine" out of sheer exhaustion, the archive permanently fuses the conflicting systems.
</Broside>

<Broside type="tip" title="Coexistence retroactively hallucinated." icon="clock" id="0385-CEB-5">
An endorsement packet filed three years late is processed as proof that the intervening outages were actually structured bonding exercises.
</Broside>

<Broside type="tip" title="Sarcasm miscalibrated." icon="smile" id="0385-CEB-6">
A deeply cynical review submitted in the comments field was parsed by the intake scripts as a glowing recommendation for departmental merger.
</Broside>

<Broside type="tip" title="Integration criteria simplified." icon="minimize" id="0385-CEB-7">
Two completely incompatible databases are declared coexistent simply because the signatures on the form were signed with the same color of ink.
</Broside>
<Broside type="tip" title="Truth by volume." icon="users" id="0385-CEB-8">
System compatibility is rarely a technical achievement; it is merely a bureaucratic hallucination sustained by five apathetic signatures.
</Broside>

<Broside type="tip" title="Consensus as duct tape." icon="paperclip" id="0385-CEB-9">
The strongest evidence of two systems successfully integrating is the total apathy of the humans tasked with trying to report the errors.
</Broside>

<Broside type="tip" title="Signatures replace function." icon="file-text" id="0385-CEB-10">
A functioning API is optional so long as the endorsement packet has the correct number of rubber stamps.
</Broside>
