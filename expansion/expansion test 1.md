## Why readers get the picture

A reader can infer several durable forces that recur across domains:

- **COMA** privileges continuity, uptime, and visible operational stability, even when that treatment erases lived harm.[^1_1]
- **SOMA** treats emotional signals as meaningful and often argues for recognition or rest, though it can be vulnerable to synthetic or overly legible affect.[^1_1]
- **C.U.N.T.I.E.R.** optimizes metrics and dashboard stability, repeatedly treating favorable measurements as sufficient justification.[^1_1]

That recurring cast of institutional logics gives even distant entries a shared interpretive frame. An entry about backups, form templates, audit vocabulary, trust badges, or social-media outrage still feels like it belongs to the same civilization because the same pressure—appearance of continuity substituting for recoverable truth—keeps returning in new forms.[^1_1]

## Where the graph weakens

The metadata often lists `relatedEntries`, `relatedLorelog`, or associated mascots, but the reader is rarely told **what kind of connection** each link represents. A direct link such as `LLG-0408-AH1 -> LLG-0400-SCAS` clearly implies “successor / attempted replacement,” while other links can be harder to interpret: predecessor, parallel case, shared mechanism, policy authority, rebuttal, consequence, or merely tonal adjacency.[^1_1]

There is also uneven reciprocity. Some high-density hubs and explicitly designed clusters are richly linked, while many individual incidents mainly link to an aphorism and mascots, which supports flavor but does less to orient someone trying to map causes, institutions, and doctrine across the corpus. `LLG-0318-SRO` and `LLG-0323-ASD`, for example, make a meaningful conceptual pair, but the overall reader journey depends heavily on encountering the relevant entries in the right order.[^1_1]

The naming and ID conventions create a small amount of friction. Variants such as `LLG-0400-SCAS`, `LLG-0401-SCAS-ECHO`, `SA-SS-TEL`, and some lowercased or alternate-form references can be flavorful, but they make it harder to tell whether a term is a canonical node, a suite, a document, or a narrative label.[^1_1]

Add a short **“Ecosystem links”** block to every major Lorelog entry, distinct from the raw frontmatter relations:


| Link type | What it tells the reader | Example |
| :-- | :-- | :-- |
