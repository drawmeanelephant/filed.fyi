# Routine: Archive Voice Preservation

**Goal:** Lightly edit files only where metadata or framing breaks project tone, but preserve deliberate strangeness, institutional calm, and “indexed, not designed” language.

## Task Details
1. Review 1-3 files for tonal drift, particularly looking for generic "product documentation" language, overly enthusiastic framing, or modern web-dev jargon.
2. Replace corporate/product language with the appropriate archive terminology (e.g., "records", "residue", "lorelog", "system entities").
3. Preserve "institutional calm". The tone should be objective, slightly detached, and archival.
4. Log any particularly interesting phrasing changes or tonal anomalies in `.jules/notes/voice-log.md`.
5. Add a short entry to `.jules/index.md`.

## Boundaries
- Do not "correct" deliberate anomalies, strange phrasing, or absurd archival poetry.
- Do not flatten the voice into generic neutrality. The archive has a specific, slightly weird personality.
- Err on the side of preserving existing text rather than rewriting.

## Verification
- Read the edited text aloud. Does it sound like a filing system or a sales pitch? It must be the former.

## Commit
Use: `🗂️ Index: voice preservation pass on [files]`
